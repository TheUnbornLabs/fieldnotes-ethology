# Glossary — The Leopard (*Panthera pardus*)

**Solitary** — living and hunting alone rather than in a group; the leopard's fundamental social organisation.
**Kleptoparasitism** — the theft of food caught by one animal by another; leopards face it chiefly from lions and hyenas.
**Caching** — storing a food item for later consumption; in leopards, hiding kills so they can be fed on over several days.
**Hoisting** — dragging and lifting a carcass up into a tree, the leopard's signature anti-theft behavior.
**Rosette** — a ring-shaped cluster of dark spots on the leopard's coat that provides camouflage in dappled light.
**Melanism** — an excess of dark pigment producing a black-coated individual; the genetic basis of the "black panther."
**Crepuscular** — most active around dawn and dusk.
**Home range** — the area an individual leopard uses and knows; male ranges typically overlap those of several females.
**Scent-marking** — depositing urine, scats, and rubbed scent to communicate identity, sex, and reproductive state.
**Scrape** — a mark made by raking the ground with the hind feet, combining a visual and a chemical signal.
**Ambush** — a hunting strategy based on concealment and a short, sudden attack rather than a long chase.
**Adaptability** — the behavioural flexibility that lets leopards exploit a wide range of prey, habitats, and even cities.
**Vibrissae** — the long, sensitive whiskers used to judge gaps and orient at close range in darkness.
**Tapetum lucidum** — a reflective layer behind the retina that improves the leopard's low-light vision.
**Obligate carnivore** — an animal whose diet must consist primarily of animal prey.
**Polygyny** — a mating system in which a male may mate with several females, mediated in leopards through range overlap.
