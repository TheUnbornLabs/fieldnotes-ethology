# Short Quiz — The Leopard (*Panthera pardus*)

1. The leopard's most famous behavior, hoisting kills into trees, primarily functions to:
   A. Keep meat cool
   B. Protect the kill from kleptoparasitic lions and hyenas
   C. Attract a mate
   D. Teach cubs to climb

2. The leopard is best described by activity pattern as:
   A. Strictly diurnal
   B. Strictly nocturnal only
   C. Predominantly nocturnal and crepuscular, but flexible
   D. Active only in the dry season

3. A "black panther" is:
   A. A separate species of big cat
   B. A melanistic leopard or jaguar
   C. A hybrid of a leopard and a lion
   D. An albino leopard

4. The leopard's social organisation is best described as:
   A. Living in prides
   B. Strictly solitary, with a mother–cub bond
   C. Cooperative pack hunting
   D. Large mixed herds

5. Most leopard communication with neighbours is:
   A. Face-to-face vocal display
   B. Indirect, through scent-marking and scrapes
   C. Cooperative calling in groups
   D. Purely visual within a group

6. Compared with the leopard, the cheetah is a specialist in:
   A. Stealth and strength
   B. Tree-climbing
   C. Speed and open-ground pursuit
   D. Group hunting

7. Which best explains how leopards survive near cities such as Mumbai?
   A. They become tame and approach people
   B. They stay hidden, hunt at night, and exploit prey like feral dogs
   C. They abandon hunting entirely
   D. They form large social groups for safety

8. The leopard's rosette coat mainly provides:
   A. Temperature regulation
   B. Camouflage in dappled light
   C. A warning signal to rivals
   D. Waterproofing

9. On the IUCN Red List, the leopard as a species is currently assessed as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Extinct in the Wild
   D. Not Evaluated

10. Leopard cubs survive their vulnerable early weeks chiefly through:
   A. Group defence by many adults
   B. The father guarding the den
   C. Concealment and being moved between hidden sites by the mother
   D. Living high in trees from birth

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. C
7. B
8. B
9. B
10. C
