# References / Further Reading — The Leopard (*Panthera pardus*)

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those that should be checked against a database before final publication. Following the collection's citation policy, no authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, or page numbers have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Mel Sunquist and Fiona Sunquist, *Wild Cats of the World* — a standard scholarly reference on the biology and behavior of the cat family, including the leopard.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Panthera pardus* (global status: Vulnerable), with separate assessments for subspecies such as the Amur leopard. Consult the current Red List entry for range, population, threats, and subspecies status.
- Panthera — the Leopard Program of the global wild-cat conservation organisation Panthera, a recognised source of science-based information on leopard ecology, threats, and conservation.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for *Panthera pardus*.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as territoriality, solitary spacing systems, communication, and predator–competitor interactions.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live web on 2026-07-02 and confirmed:

- **Scientific name & taxonomy:** *Panthera pardus*, genus *Panthera*, family Felidae, order Carnivora — confirmed (IUCN Red List; Animal Diversity Web).
- **IUCN Red List status:** *Panthera pardus* assessed globally as **Vulnerable** (criteria A2cd; assessment published 2024) — confirmed via IUCN Red List (species 15954). Subspecies such as the Amur leopard are far more threatened (Critically Endangered), matching the book's Section 2.
