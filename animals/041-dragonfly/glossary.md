# Glossary — The Dragonfly

- **Predictive interception** — a hunting strategy in which the predator steers toward where the prey will be, not where it currently is, so their flight paths converge at a future intercept point.
- **Internal model** — an internal neural representation of a target's motion and of the animal's own body state, used to guide anticipatory steering.
- **Compound eye** — an eye built from many separate optical units, giving a wide field of view and high sensitivity to motion.
- **Ommatidia** — the individual optical units that make up a compound eye; a dragonfly eye can contain up to roughly 30,000.
- **Selective attention** — the neural ability to lock onto a single target and suppress responses to competing stimuli in a cluttered visual scene.
- **Small target motion detector (STMD)** — a class of dragonfly neuron that responds selectively to tiny moving objects, the visual signature of prey.
- **Nymph (naiad)** — the wingless aquatic larval stage of a dragonfly, itself an active predator; it dominates the animal's total lifespan.
- **Labial mask** — the nymph's hinged, extensible lower lip, folded under the head, that shoots forward to seize prey with terminal hooks or pincers.
- **Rectal gill** — the gas-exchange structure inside a dragonfly nymph's hind gut, also used to power jet propulsion.
- **Jet propulsion** — rapid movement produced by forcefully expelling water from the rectal chamber; used by nymphs to escape or lunge.
- **Wheel position** — the ring- or heart-shaped mating posture dragonflies form during sperm transfer.
- **Sperm competition** — competition among the sperm of different males to fertilize a female's eggs; in dragonflies it includes physical removal of rival sperm by the male's genitalia and post-mating mate guarding.
- **Obelisk posture** — a thermoregulatory posture in which a perched dragonfly points its abdomen toward the sun at midday to reduce heat load.
- **Territoriality** — defense by males of a stretch of shoreline, water patch, or airspace to control access to egg-laying females.
- **Migration** — long-distance seasonal movement; the globe skimmer *Pantala flavescens* undertakes one of the longest known insect migrations.
- **Hemimetabolous** — developing gradually from nymph to adult through successive molts, without a resting pupal stage.
