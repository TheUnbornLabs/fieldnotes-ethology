# Quiz — The Dragonfly

1. To which suborder do true dragonflies belong?
   A. Zygoptera
   B. Anisoptera
   C. Mantodea
   D. Lepidoptera

2. "Predictive interception" means the dragonfly:
   A. Waits for prey to land nearby
   B. Chases directly behind its prey
   C. Steers toward where the prey will be
   D. Emits a sound to stun prey

3. Roughly how many ommatidia can a single dragonfly compound eye contain?
   A. About 100
   B. About 1,000
   C. Up to about 30,000
   D. Exactly one

4. The dragonfly nymph captures prey mainly using its:
   A. Venomous fangs
   B. Extensible labial mask
   C. Silk web
   D. Front wings

5. Jet propulsion in a dragonfly nymph is powered by:
   A. Flapping gills on the legs
   B. Expelling water from the rectal chamber
   C. A gas bladder
   D. Rapid tail flicks like a fish

6. The "wheel position" refers to:
   A. A hunting flight pattern
   B. A thermoregulatory posture
   C. The mating posture during sperm transfer
   D. A migratory formation

7. The obelisk posture helps a dragonfly:
   A. Attract mates with sound
   B. Reduce heat load by pointing the abdomen at the sun
   C. Camouflage against bark
   D. Store eggs

8. Which species is famous for one of the longest insect migrations?
   A. *Mantis religiosa*
   B. *Pantala flavescens*
   C. *Tenodera sinensis*
   D. *Apis mellifera*

9. Selective-attention neurons in the dragonfly brain:
   A. Track every target at once equally
   B. Lock onto one target and suppress competitors
   C. Detect only stationary objects
   D. Control wing muscles directly

10. Compared with the praying mantis, the dragonfly is best described as:
    A. An ambush predator using stereoscopic vision
    B. A filter feeder
    C. An aerial pursuit predator using predictive interception
    D. A scavenger

## Answer Key

1. B — True dragonflies are the suborder Anisoptera; damselflies are Zygoptera.
2. C — The dragonfly steers to a future intercept point rather than chasing the prey's current position.
3. C — Dragonfly compound eyes can hold up to roughly 30,000 ommatidia, among the highest of any insect.
4. B — The nymph shoots out its hinged labial mask to seize prey.
5. B — Nymphs expel water from the rectal chamber for jet-propelled escape.
6. C — The wheel position is the mating posture during sperm transfer.
7. B — Pointing the abdomen at the sun reduces exposed surface and heat load.
8. B — The globe skimmer, *Pantala flavescens*, is a champion long-distance migrant.
9. B — Certain STMD neurons lock onto one target and suppress competitors.
10. C — The dragonfly is an aerial pursuit predator using predictive interception, contrasting with the mantis's stereoscopic ambush.
