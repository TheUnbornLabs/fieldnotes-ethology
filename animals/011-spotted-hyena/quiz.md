# Short Quiz — The Spotted Hyena (*Crocuta crocuta*)

1. To which group do hyenas actually belong?
A. Canids (dog family)
B. Feliforms (cat-like carnivorans)
C. Mustelids (weasel family)
D. Ursids (bear family)

2. In a spotted hyena clan, who is socially dominant?
A. The largest male
B. A male coalition
C. Adult females
D. Rank is unstructured

3. How is social rank primarily determined in spotted hyenas?
A. Won through fighting each generation
B. Inherited from the mother
C. Decided by body size alone
D. Assigned by the oldest male

4. What is the main component of the spotted hyena's diet in most studied populations?
A. Prey it hunts itself
B. Lion leftovers
C. Insects
D. Plant matter

5. The spotted hyena's "pseudo-penis" is:
A. Evidence the animal is a true hermaphrodite
B. A greatly enlarged clitoris caused by prenatal hormones
C. Found only in males
D. A scent gland

6. The famous hyena "laugh" most likely signals:
A. Happiness and play
B. Excitement or social stress, often in a subordinate
C. A mating invitation only
D. A territorial all-clear

7. Which describes the spotted hyena's social structure?
A. Solitary except when mating
B. Small permanent packs led by a male
C. Large fission-fusion clans
D. Seasonal migratory herds

8. The long-distance contact call of the spotted hyena is called the:
A. Roar
B. Whoop
C. Bark
D. Bugle

9. The relationship between hyenas and lions over carcasses is best described as:
A. One-way theft by hyenas
B. One-way theft by lions
C. Two-way kleptoparasitism
D. No overlap in diet

10. What is the spotted hyena's IUCN Red List status?
A. Critically Endangered
B. Endangered
C. Vulnerable
D. Least Concern

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. A
5. B
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. C
10. D
