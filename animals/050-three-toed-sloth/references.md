# References / Further Reading — The Three-Toed Sloth (*Bradypus*)

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Research on sloth ecophysiology and behavior associated with Bryson Voirin — including field studies of wild sloth sleep and activity — is a strong starting point for the physiology and daily-life material in this book.
- Research and public-education work on sloth biology and conservation associated with Rebecca Cliffe (and the Sloth Conservation Foundation) covers behavior, movement, and the ecology of *Bradypus*.
- IUCN Red List — for the current, species-level conservation assessments of the individual *Bradypus* species (brown-throated, pale-throated, maned, and pygmy three-toed sloths). Verify each species' current listing directly.
- Standard animal-behavior texts (e.g., Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for the general framework linking diet, energy budgets, and behavior applied throughout this book.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Genus ***Bradypus*** (three-toed / three-fingered sloths); superorder Xenarthra; distinct from and only distantly related to two-toed sloths (*Choloepus*) — confirmed.
- Named species (brown-throated *B. variegatus*, pale-throated *B. tridactylus*, maned *B. torquatus*, pygmy *B. pygmaeus*) — confirmed.
- **IUCN status is species-specific:** brown-throated sloth (*B. variegatus*) is Least Concern; the **pygmy three-toed sloth (*B. pygmaeus*) is Critically Endangered** (listed CR since 2006; endemic to Isla Escudo de Veraguas, Panama; population estimated only in the hundreds to low thousands) — confirmed (IUCN; USFWS Species Status Assessment; ADW; EDGE of Existence). The maned sloth (*B. torquatus*) is also of elevated conservation concern (historically Vulnerable). The book's Section 2 statements are consistent with these findings.
