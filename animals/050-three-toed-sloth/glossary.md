# Glossary — The Three-Toed Sloth (*Bradypus*)

- **Folivory** — a diet based on eating leaves, which are typically low in energy and protein, high in fiber, and often chemically defended.
- **Low metabolic rate** — an unusually slow rate of energy use, allowing an animal to survive on very little food.
- **Thermoconformer** — an animal that lets its body temperature track the surrounding environment rather than holding it constant, saving the energy that active warming would cost.
- **Crypsis** — concealment through camouflage and stillness that makes an animal hard for predators to detect.
- **Algae symbiosis** — the relationship in which green algae live in a sloth's grooved hairs, contributing to camouflage and forming part of the debated sloth–moth–algae association.
- **Xenarthran** — a member of the superorder Xenarthra, the ancient South and Central American lineage of sloths, anteaters, and armadillos.
- **Arboreal** — living primarily in trees.
- **Inverted suspension** — hanging upside down beneath branches, which sloths do with little muscular effort thanks to hooked claws and specialized tendons.
- **Gut retention time** — how long food remains in the digestive tract; in sloths it is exceptionally long, allowing slow fermentation of leaves.
- **Latrine behavior** — the sloth's habit of repeatedly defecating at a chosen site, in its case usually the base of a tree, reached by a risky weekly descent.
- **Coprophagy** — the eating of feces; relevant background for discussing gut and latrine biology, though the three-toed sloth's defining habit is its latrine descent rather than coprophagy itself.
- **Camouflage** — coloration and pattern, such as the sloth's algae-tinted coat, that help an animal blend into its surroundings.
- **Solitary** — living alone rather than in pairs or groups, as adult three-toed sloths do.
- **Convergent evolution** — the independent evolution of similar traits in unrelated lineages, as in the resemblance between three-toed and two-toed sloths or between sloths and koalas.
