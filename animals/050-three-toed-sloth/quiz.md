# Quiz — The Three-Toed Sloth (*Bradypus*)

1. The three-toed sloth belongs to which group of mammals?
   A. Primates
   B. Marsupials
   C. Xenarthrans (with anteaters and armadillos)
   D. Rodents

2. Why does the three-toed sloth move so slowly?
   A. It is lazy and unmotivated
   B. Its low-energy leaf diet and slow metabolism cannot support fast movement
   C. Its legs are broken
   D. It moves fast at night and only appears slow

3. How much do wild three-toed sloths actually sleep per day?
   A. About 20 hours
   B. Only 2 hours
   C. About 9–10 hours
   D. They never sleep

4. The greenish tint of a sloth's fur comes from:
   A. Dirt and mud
   B. Green algae growing in grooved hairs
   C. A pigment in the skin
   D. Reflected light from leaves

5. The three-toed sloth's digestion is notable because:
   A. It is extremely fast
   B. It does not digest leaves at all
   C. It relies on eating meat
   D. Food is retained for many days in a large multi-chambered stomach

6. Which behavior exposes the sloth to its greatest predation risk?
   A. Feeding in the canopy
   B. Swimming
   C. Its weekly descent to the ground to defecate
   D. Sleeping

7. The three-toed sloth is a thermoconformer, meaning it:
   A. Keeps a perfectly constant body temperature
   B. Lets its body temperature track the environment and basks to warm up
   C. Generates heat by shivering constantly
   D. Cannot survive temperature change

8. Compared with the three-toed sloth, the two-toed sloth is generally:
   A. More active, broader in diet, and more willing to defend itself
   B. Identical in every way
   C. A close relative in the same genus
   D. Strictly a leaf-only specialist that never bites

9. The sloth's main defense against predators such as harpy eagles is:
   A. Fast escape
   B. Loud alarm calls
   C. Camouflage and near-total stillness (crypsis)
   D. Group defense

10. The resemblance between sloths and koalas is best explained by:
   A. Close common ancestry
   B. Convergent evolution of unrelated tree-dwelling leaf-eaters
   C. Koalas being a kind of sloth
   D. Coincidental diet only

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. D
6. C
7. B
8. A
9. C
10. B
