# Quiz — Book 029: The Cuttlefish

1. Cuttlefish belong to which animal group?
   A. Cephalopod molluscs
   B. Fish
   C. Crustaceans
   D. Marine reptiles

2. The evidence indicates cuttlefish perceive color:
   A. Better than humans
   B. Only in blue and green
   C. Not at all — they appear to be colorblind
   D. Through their skin, not their eyes

3. Which skin cells switch colored spots on and off under direct muscular and nervous control?
   A. Leucophores
   B. Iridophores
   C. Chromatophores
   D. Papillae

4. A "sneaker" male cuttlefish gains mating access by:
   A. Fighting off all rivals
   B. Mimicking a female's coloration and posture
   C. Building the largest nest
   D. Producing the loudest call

5. Split (dual) signalling refers to a male that:
   A. Splits into two animals
   B. Shows courtship colors and rival-directed colors on opposite sides at once
   C. Changes color only at night
   D. Mates with two females simultaneously

6. The cuttlebone functions mainly as:
   A. A backbone
   B. A protective outer shell
   C. A digestive organ
   D. A buoyancy-control structure

7. In the Schnell et al. study, cuttlefish demonstrated:
   A. Tool manufacture
   B. Delayed gratification (self-control)
   C. Vocal learning
   D. Long-distance migration

8. "Episodic-like" memory in cuttlefish involves remembering:
   A. Only the location of food
   B. What was eaten, where, and when
   C. The faces of other cuttlefish
   D. Magnetic compass directions

9. The mobile display of dark bands sweeping across the body, used in hunting, is called the:
   A. Deimatic display
   B. Zebra pattern
   C. Passing-cloud display
   D. Papillae display

10. Compared with an octopus, a cuttlefish characteristically has:
    A. No internal skeleton and eight arms only
    B. Gills instead of a mantle
    C. True color vision
    D. A rigid internal cuttlebone and two long feeding tentacles

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. D
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. D
