# Glossary — Book 010: The Cheetah

- **Coalition** — a stable group of male cheetahs, often littermate brothers, that stay together for life to hold territory and gain mating access.
- **Solitary** — living and ranging alone; the typical pattern for female cheetahs outside of raising cubs.
- **Genetic bottleneck** — a sharp reduction in population size that leaves survivors with reduced genetic diversity, seen in the cheetah's evolutionary history.
- **Semi-retractable claws** — claws that stay partly exposed, giving the cheetah cleat-like grip for high-speed running.
- **Diurnal** — active during daylight; the cheetah's hunting schedule, which helps it avoid nocturnal competitors.
- **Kleptoparasitism** — the stealing of another animal's kill, which cheetahs frequently suffer at the hands of lions, leopards, and hyenas.
- **Sprint** — a very short burst of maximum-speed running, the cheetah's core hunting method.
- **Thermoregulation** — the control of body temperature; rapid overheating limits the length of a cheetah's chase.
- **Cub mortality** — the death rate of young; famously high in cheetahs owing to predation.
- **Home range** — the area an animal habitually uses; cheetah females range widely without strictly defending it.
- **Tear marks** — the dark facial stripes running from eye to mouth, thought to reduce glare and aid visual focus.
- **Cursorial** — adapted for running; describes the cheetah's pursuit-hunting body plan.
- **Obligate carnivore** — an animal that must eat meat to survive, as all cats do.
- **Coursing** — hunting by pursuit; historically the role trained cheetahs played for royalty.
