# Glossary — The Horse (*Equus caballus*)

**Prey species** — an animal whose behavior is organized around avoiding being eaten; in the horse this shapes its senses, sociality, and flight response.
**Flight response** — the horse's primary defense: to flee first from a perceived threat and assess afterward.
**Flight zone** — the space around a horse within which an approaching threat triggers movement away; central to low-stress handling.
**Harem band** — the natural social unit of mares and their young accompanied by a stallion.
**Bachelor band** — a looser all-male group formed by young or displaced stallions that lack a harem.
**Allogrooming** — mutual grooming between two horses, which strengthens social bonds and can lower heart rate.
**Hindgut fermenter** — an animal, like the horse, that digests fibrous plants using microbes in an enlarged cecum and colon, requiring near-continuous feeding.
**Monocular vision** — sight in which each eye views its own side of the world separately; gives the horse a very wide field but limited depth perception.
**Binocular vision** — sight in which both eyes' fields overlap, giving good depth perception; in the horse this is a narrow zone directly ahead.
**Dichromatic vision** — color vision based on two cone types; horses see mainly blues and yellows and are not colorblind.
**EquiFACS** — the Equine Facial Action Coding System, a tool for objectively cataloging horse facial muscle movements, useful for reading emotion and pain.
**Dominance** — a relationship in which one animal reliably yields to another over a resource; not the same as leadership.
**Leadership** — influence over where a group moves; in horses it is fluid and can be shared, not fixed in a single "alpha."
**Vigilance** — alert scanning for danger, often shared across group members so not all must watch at once.
**Flehmen response** — curling the upper lip to draw scent over a specialized sensory organ, used to assess smells including reproductive state.
**Socialization period** — an early developmental window in a foal when experiences strongly shape later temperament.
