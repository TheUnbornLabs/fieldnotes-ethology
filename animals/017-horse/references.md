## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Sue M. McDonnell, *The Equid Ethogram: A Practical Field Guide to Horse Behavior* — a standard illustrated catalog of horse behaviors.
- George H. Waring, *Horse Behavior* — a standard scholarly reference on the behavior of horses.
- Genetic research on the origins of the modern domestic horse, associated with Ludovic Orlando and Pablo Librado and colleagues, pointing to a western Eurasian steppe lineage ("DOM2") spreading from roughly 4,200 years ago — present cautiously as an active, evolving field; verify specific dates and claims before publication.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for Przewalski's horse (*Equus przewalskii*). The domestic horse itself, as a domesticated animal, is not assessed for conservation status.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for the horse.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as domestication, communication, dominance, and prey defense.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live IUCN Red List and Animal Diversity Web:

- The horse, *Equus caballus* (also *Equus ferus caballus*), family Equidae, order **Perissodactyla** (odd-toed ungulates, with rhinos and tapirs) — confirmed. As a domesticated animal it is **not IUCN-assessed**. Source: Animal Diversity Web.
- Its wild relative, **Przewalski's horse** (*Equus przewalskii* / *Equus ferus przewalskii*), is a separate IUCN-assessed taxon listed **Endangered**; it was declared extinct in the wild (1960s) and has since been reintroduced (now >50 mature free-living individuals) — confirmed current. Source: IUCN Red List, Przewalski's Horse assessment (iucnredlist.org).

The DOM2 domestication lineage/date, EquiFACS, vision, and social-structure specifics remain to be traced to their primary sources as noted above.
