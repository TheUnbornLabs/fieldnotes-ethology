## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- George B. Schaller, *The Serengeti Lion: A Study of Predator–Prey Relations* — a foundational scholarly study of lion behavior and ecology in the Serengeti.
- The long-term Serengeti lion research associated with Craig Packer and colleagues — a major body of field work on lion social behavior, coalitions, infanticide, and mane signalling. Named here in general terms; specific findings should be traced to their individual publications and verified before final publication.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Panthera leo* (global status: Vulnerable). Consult the current Red List entry for range, population trend, and threats.
- Panthera and the Lion Recovery Fund — recognised conservation organisations working on lion protection, human–lion conflict mitigation, and prey recovery.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for *Panthera leo*.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as cooperation, communication, sexual selection, and social structure.
