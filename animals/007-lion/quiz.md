# Short Quiz — The Lion (*Panthera leo*)

1. Among the cats, the lion is notable for being:
   A. The fastest hunter
   B. The only truly social species
   C. The only nocturnal species
   D. The smallest big cat

2. The stable, long-term core of a lion pride is made up of:
   A. A single dominant male
   B. Related adult females and their cubs
   C. Unrelated males in a coalition
   D. Nomadic subadults

3. A male coalition's period of holding a pride is called its:
   A. Territory
   B. Crèche
   C. Tenure
   D. Dispersal

4. Lions are most active as hunters:
   A. In the heat of midday
   B. Around dusk, at night, and near dawn
   C. Only during the dry season
   D. Never at night

5. Infanticide by incoming males after a takeover is best explained as:
   A. Random aggression
   B. A response to food shortage
   C. A reproductive strategy that returns females to breeding sooner
   D. Defense of cubs

6. A "crèche" in lion society refers to:
   A. A boundary scent post
   B. A nursery group in which pride females rear cubs together
   C. The male coalition
   D. A solitary birthing den

7. The claim that male lions never hunt is:
   A. Correct
   B. False; males do hunt, especially as nomads and from ambush
   C. True only in Asia
   D. True only for older males

8. A male lion's mane is best understood as:
   A. Pure decoration with no function
   B. An honest signal of condition and testosterone
   C. Evidence of jungle habitat
   D. A cooling structure

9. Lions and tigers in the wild today:
   A. Regularly meet and fight
   B. Share the same African range
   C. Do not share range and do not naturally meet
   D. Hunt together

10. On the IUCN Red List, the lion is currently assessed as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Extinct in the Wild
   D. Not evaluated

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. B
