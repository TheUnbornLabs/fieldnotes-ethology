## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- **IUCN Red List status:** **Vulnerable** (criterion A2acd; assessed 2012, unchanged as of the current Red List). Book text confirmed correct. Source: IUCN Red List entry for *Ophiophagus hannah*.
- **Taxonomy — CLARIFIED:** *Ophiophagus hannah* was long treated as a single species. A 2024 revision (Das, Gowri Shankar et al., *European Journal of Taxonomy*) split the king cobra complex into four species — *O. hannah* (Northern), *O. bungarus* (Sunda), *O. kaalinga* (Western Ghats), and *O. salvatana* (Luzon). A note was added to Section 2 so the single-species framing is not overstated. The IUCN "Vulnerable" assessment still refers to the traditional broad *O. hannah*. Sources: European Journal of Taxonomy 2024; Mongabay; Nature news.
- **Family / placement:** Elapidae, front-fanged, own genus (not *Naja*) — confirmed via Animal Diversity Web and The Reptile Database.
