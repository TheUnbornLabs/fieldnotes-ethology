# Short Quiz — The King Cobra (*Ophiophagus hannah*)

1. What does the king cobra primarily eat?
   A. Small mammals and birds
   B. Other snakes
   C. Fish and amphibians only
   D. Insects and eggs

2. To which snake family does the king cobra belong?
   A. Viperidae
   B. Pythonidae
   C. Elapidae
   D. Colubridae

3. What is unusual about king cobra reproduction compared with most snakes?
   A. It gives live birth
   B. The male incubates the eggs
   C. The female builds a nest and guards the eggs
   D. It lays eggs in water

4. The king cobra's raised hood is best described as:
   A. A guaranteed prelude to an attack
   B. Primarily a defensive bluff to deter intruders
   C. A cooling mechanism
   D. A courtship-only display

5. Which sense is most important for the king cobra when tracking prey?
   A. Infrared heat detection
   B. Echolocation
   C. Chemoreception via tongue-flicking and the Jacobson's organ
   D. Colour vision alone

6. The king cobra's low-frequency "growl" is aided by:
   A. Vocal cords like a mammal's
   B. Tracheal diverticula acting as resonating chambers
   C. Rubbing scales together
   D. The rattle at the tail

7. Which statement about king cobra hearing is correct?
   A. Snakes are completely deaf
   B. They hear airborne music clearly
   C. They sense ground vibration and some low-frequency airborne sound
   D. They rely on external ears

8. What is the IUCN Red List status of the king cobra?
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Extinct in the Wild
   D. Not Evaluated

9. When a snake charmer plays a pungi, the cobra sways because it is:
   A. Dancing to the melody
   B. Tracking the movement of the pipe as a potential threat
   C. Hypnotised by the sound
   D. Trying to eat the instrument

10. What happens to king cobra hatchlings after they emerge?
    A. The mother feeds them for months
    B. They are cared for by the father
    C. They are independent immediately, with functional venom
    D. They remain in the nest through winter

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. C
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. B
10. C
