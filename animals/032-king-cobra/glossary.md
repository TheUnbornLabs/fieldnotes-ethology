# Glossary — The King Cobra (*Ophiophagus hannah*)

**Ophiophagy** — a diet specialised on eating other snakes; the root of the king cobra's genus name, *Ophiophagus*.
**Neurotoxin** — a venom component that disrupts the nervous system, in the king cobra's case rapidly immobilising prey by interfering with nerve-to-muscle signalling.
**Hood** — the flattened, spread neck the king cobra raises in a defensive display, formed by loose skin supported by elongated ribs.
**Tracheal diverticula** — pouch-like extensions of the windpipe that act as resonating chambers, giving the king cobra's warning "growl" its low pitch.
**Growl** — the distinctive low-frequency defensive sound of the king cobra, deeper than the typical snake hiss and produced with the aid of tracheal resonating structures.
**Jacobson's organ (vomeronasal organ)** — a paired chemosensory structure in the roof of the mouth that analyses scent molecules delivered by the tongue.
**Tongue-flicking** — the repeated protrusion of the forked tongue to sample scent particles from the air and surfaces for delivery to the Jacobson's organ.
**Chemoreception** — the detection and interpretation of chemical cues; the king cobra's primary sense for tracking prey and mates.
**Ectotherm** — an animal that relies on external heat sources to regulate body temperature, as all snakes do.
**Nest-building** — the female king cobra's construction of a mound of raked leaf litter in which she lays and guards her eggs; a near-unique behavior among snakes.
**Envenomation** — the injection of venom by a bite; in humans a king cobra envenomation is a serious medical emergency.
**Diurnal** — active mainly during daylight hours, as the king cobra generally is.
**Elapid** — a member of the family Elapidae, the front-fanged venomous snakes that includes cobras, kraits, mambas, coral snakes, and sea snakes.
**Oviparous** — reproducing by laying eggs rather than giving live birth.
**Clutch** — the set of eggs laid and, in the king cobra, guarded by the female during incubation.
**Home range** — the area an individual uses to hunt and live, which for the solitary king cobra can be large.
