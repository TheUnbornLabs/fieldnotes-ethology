# Glossary — Book 026: The Orca

- **Matriline** — the fundamental unit of orca society: a mother and her descendants, who in many populations travel together for life.
- **Pod** — a group of related matrilines that regularly travel and associate together.
- **Clan** — a larger grouping of pods that share a common ancestry and overlapping vocal dialect.
- **Ecotype** — a population adapted to a particular way of life (e.g. fish-eating resident versus mammal-eating Bigg's) that differs in diet, body form, dialect, and behaviour, and rarely interbreeds with others.
- **Resident (orca)** — a North Pacific ecotype that specialises on fish, especially salmon, and lives in large, highly stable matrilineal pods.
- **Transient / Bigg's (orca)** — a North Pacific ecotype that specialises on marine mammals, hunts stealthily, and lives in smaller groups; named for researcher Michael Bigg.
- **Dialect** — the set of learned, stereotyped pulsed calls specific to a matriline, pod, or clan, functioning like a family accent.
- **Cultural transmission** — the passing of learned behaviours, such as dialects and hunting techniques, between individuals and across generations by social learning rather than by genes.
- **Menopause / grandmother effect** — the post-reproductive life stage of female orcas, during which experienced older females improve their families' survival by serving as ecological knowledge-keepers.
- **Carousel feeding** — a cooperative technique in which orcas herd herring into a tight ball and stun them with tail slaps.
- **Wave-washing** — an Antarctic technique in which orcas coordinate to create a wave that washes a seal off an ice floe.
- **Intentional stranding** — deliberately beaching to seize prey such as sea lion pups from the surf, as documented at Península Valdés, Argentina.
- **Echolocation (biosonar)** — sensing by sound: emitting clicks and interpreting the returning echoes to locate and characterise prey and surroundings.
- **Incipient speciation** — the early stage of populations splitting into separate species; orca ecotypes are a leading candidate, driven substantially by learned culture.
- **Apex predator** — a predator at the top of the food web with no natural predators of its own; the orca is a global apex predator.
