# Quiz — Book 026: The Orca

1. To which biological family does the orca belong?
   A. It is a baleen whale
   B. It is the largest member of the dolphin family, Delphinidae
   C. It is a porpoise
   D. It is a type of fish

2. The stable core unit of orca society is the:
   A. Matriline
   B. Territory
   C. Solitary pair
   D. Temporary herd

3. North Pacific "resident" orcas specialise on:
   A. Seals and sea lions
   B. Large baleen whales
   C. Fish, especially salmon
   D. Sharks

4. Orca vocal dialects are:
   A. Innate and identical in all orcas
   B. Used only for echolocation
   C. A full language with grammar
   D. Learned, stereotyped call repertoires specific to a matriline or clan

5. "Wave-washing" is a hunting technique in which orcas:
   A. Herd herring into a ball and stun them
   B. Beach themselves to grab sea lion pups
   C. Create a wave to sweep a seal off an ice floe
   D. Chase salmon up rivers

6. The "grandmother effect" in orcas refers to:
   A. Old females losing their role in the group
   B. Post-reproductive females improving their families' survival as knowledge-keepers
   C. Grandmothers being expelled from the pod
   D. A myth with no scientific support

7. Regarding orcas and humans, the scientific record shows:
   A. No confirmed records of wild orcas killing humans
   B. Frequent fatal attacks on swimmers
   C. That orcas actively hunt people
   D. That the name comes from attacks on sailors

8. Orca ecotypes (residents, Bigg's, offshore) are significant because they:
   A. Are all identical in diet and behaviour
   B. Change type from year to year
   C. Are simply age classes of the same group
   D. Differ in diet, dialect, and form and rarely interbreed, suggesting incipient speciation

9. A major, insidious threat to wild orcas is:
   A. Loss of body hair
   B. Bioaccumulation of contaminants such as PCBs in their blubber
   C. Competition with fish for oxygen
   D. Excessive echolocation

10. Compared with bottlenose dolphins, orca society is best described as:
    A. More stable, built on lifelong matrilines
    B. More fluid and alliance-based
    C. Entirely solitary
    D. Identical in structure

## Answer Key

1. B
2. A
3. C
4. D
5. C
6. B
7. A
8. D
9. B
10. A
