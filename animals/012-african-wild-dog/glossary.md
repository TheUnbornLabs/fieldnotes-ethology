# Glossary — The African Wild Dog (*Lycaon pictus*)

**Obligate cooperation** — a way of life in which an individual cannot succeed alone and depends on the group for hunting, defence, and reproduction.
**Coursing** — hunting by running down prey over distance in sustained pursuit rather than ambushing it.
**Endurance hunting** — wearing prey to exhaustion through prolonged pursuit, exploiting stamina rather than a single burst of speed or strength.
**Regurgitation** — bringing up swallowed meat to feed pups, den guards, nursing females, and injured or old packmates.
**Allo-parenting** — care of young by pack members other than the mother, central to wild dog pup-rearing.
**Reproductive skew** — the concentration of breeding in one dominant pair while other adults forgo reproduction to help.
**Dominant pair** — the breeding male and female whose reproduction the pack collectively supports.
**Quorum / voting** — a collective decision reached once enough individuals signal readiness; in wild dogs, apparently tallied through pre-hunt "sneezes."
**Pack cohesion** — the tight social integration that binds a wild dog pack and on which its survival depends.
**Hunting success rate** — the proportion of hunts that end in a kill; unusually high in wild dogs, though exact figures vary by habitat and prey.
**Dispersal** — the departure of maturing dogs, often in same-sex sibling groups, to found or join new packs.
**Kleptoparasitism** — the theft of a kill by another animal, such as lions or hyenas taking wild dog prey.
**Cursorial predator** — a hunter adapted for sustained running rather than ambush.
**Crepuscular** — most active around dawn and dusk.
