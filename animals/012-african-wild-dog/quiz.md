# Short Quiz — The African Wild Dog (*Lycaon pictus*)

1. The African wild dog is best classified as:
   A. A feral domestic dog
   B. A distinct wild species in its own genus, *Lycaon*
   C. A member of the hyena family
   D. A subspecies of gray wolf

2. Wild dogs bring down prey primarily by:
   A. Ambush from cover
   B. A single high-speed sprint
   C. Sustained coursing and endurance
   D. Overpowering prey with brute strength

3. The "sneezing" behaviour documented before a hunt appears to function as:
   A. A way to clear the airways
   B. A quorum-style vote on whether to hunt
   C. An alarm call warning of lions
   D. A courtship display

4. Reproduction in a typical wild dog pack is:
   A. Shared equally among all adults
   B. Concentrated in a single dominant pair (reproductive skew)
   C. Limited to subordinate females
   D. Absent in most years

5. Wild dogs feed pups, guards, nursing females, and injured packmates by:
   A. Leading them to the carcass
   B. Caching meat for later
   C. Regurgitating swallowed meat
   D. Hunting separately for each

6. Compared with lions, the wild dog's hunting success rate is generally:
   A. Much lower
   B. About the same
   C. Notably higher
   D. Impossible to estimate

7. A major reason wild dogs kill and eat very quickly is:
   A. Cruelty
   B. Kleptoparasitism by lions and hyenas
   C. A small stomach
   D. Poor digestion

8. At a wild dog kill, priority access to food usually goes to:
   A. The dominant male only
   B. The pups
   C. The oldest adults
   D. Whoever made the kill

9. The single greatest disease threat to wild dogs from domestic dogs includes:
   A. Rabies and canine distemper
   B. Only external parasites
   C. Foot-and-mouth disease
   D. No significant disease risk

10. On the IUCN Red List, *Lycaon pictus* is currently assessed as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Near Threatened
   C. Endangered
   D. Extinct in the Wild

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. C
6. C
7. B
8. B
9. A
10. C
