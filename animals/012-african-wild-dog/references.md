## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Scott Creel and Nancy Marusha Creel, *The African Wild Dog: Behavior, Ecology, and Conservation* — a standard scholarly reference on wild dog biology, behaviour, and conservation.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Lycaon pictus* (global status: Endangered). Consult the current Red List entry for range, population trend, and threats.
- IUCN SSC Canid Specialist Group — a recognised expert body providing species information and conservation status for wild canids, including the African wild dog.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for *Lycaon pictus*.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as cooperation, reproductive skew, and collective decision-making.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live IUCN Red List and Animal Diversity Web:

- Scientific name *Lycaon pictus*, family Canidae, monotypic genus *Lycaon* — confirmed. Source: IUCN Red List; Animal Diversity Web.
- IUCN Red List status: **Endangered** (small, declining, severely fragmented population; strongholds in southern and eastern Africa) — confirmed current. Source: IUCN Red List, *Lycaon pictus* assessment (iucnredlist.org).
- Distinctive anatomy: four toes per foot (first digit lost on the forefeet), unlike the five toes of most canids — confirmed. Source: Animal Diversity Web (*Lycaon pictus*).
- Wild lifespan of roughly ten years (commonly cited as ~10–12) — consistent with Animal Diversity Web.

The "sneeze quorum" hunt-initiation finding and quantitative hunting-success figures remain to be traced to their primary sources as noted above.
