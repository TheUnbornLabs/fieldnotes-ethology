# Short Quiz — The Red Fox (*Vulpes vulpes*)

1. The red fox is best described as:
   A. An obligate carnivore that hunts large prey in packs
   B. A dietary generalist that forages largely alone
   C. A strictly solitary animal with no social structure
   D. A colonial breeder living in large communal groups

2. On the IUCN Red List, *Vulpes vulpes* is assessed as:
   A. Critically Endangered
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Least Concern
   D. Extinct in the Wild

3. The fox's basic social unit is:
   A. A large pack of unrelated adults
   B. A breeding pair holding a territory, sometimes with non-breeding female helpers
   C. A single lifelong solitary individual
   D. A herd led by a dominant male

4. "Scatter-hoarding" (caching) in foxes means:
   A. Storing all surplus food in one large larder
   B. Sharing every kill immediately with the group
   C. Burying surplus food in many separate dispersed locations
   D. Eating everything found at once without storing

5. The reported north-south alignment of the fox's "mouse pounce" (Cerveny et al.) should be presented as:
   A. A firmly proven fact
   B. An intriguing but debated finding awaiting confirmation
   C. A disproven myth
   D. Evidence that foxes are cats

6. The eerie fox "scream" heard at night is:
   A. A sign the fox is dying
   B. Always a sign of a fox attack
   C. A normal breeding-season communication call, especially by vixens
   D. Produced only by cubs

7. "Gekkering" refers to:
   A. A long-distance contact howl
   B. A rapid, stuttering call used in close-range aggressive or excited encounters
   C. A scent-marking behaviour
   D. The mouse pounce

8. Which senses are most important to the fox when locating hidden small prey?
   A. Vision and touch alone
   B. Hearing and smell, aided by low-light vision
   C. Echolocation
   D. Taste and electroreception

9. The Russian farm-fox experiment (Belyaev and Trut) selected foxes for:
   A. Larger body size
   B. Coat colour only
   C. Tameness toward humans
   D. Faster running speed

10. Compared with the gray wolf, the red fox is:
   A. A cooperative pack hunter of large prey
   B. A solitary-foraging generalist that rarely hunts in groups
   C. More endangered and far less widespread
   D. Unable to hold a territory

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. C
5. B
6. C
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. B
