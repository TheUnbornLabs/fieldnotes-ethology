# Short Quiz — The Komodo Dragon (*Varanus komodoensis*)

1. The Komodo dragon is best described as an example of which evolutionary phenomenon?
   A. Island gigantism
   B. Convergent flight
   C. Sexual mimicry
   D. Hibernation specialization

2. To which group does the Komodo dragon belong?
   A. Crocodilians
   B. Dinosaurs
   C. Monitor lizards (Varanidae)
   D. Amphibians

3. Which sense is most important for a dragon locating a distant carcass?
   A. Hearing
   B. Smell, via the forked tongue and Jacobson's organ
   C. Echolocation
   D. Magnetoreception

4. The modern understanding of how dragons subdue large prey emphasizes:
   A. Dirty-mouth bacterial infection alone
   B. Constriction like a python
   C. Venom (anticoagulant and blood-pressure-lowering) plus serrated wounds and blood loss
   D. Electric shock

5. Why are juvenile Komodo dragons largely arboreal?
   A. To catch flying insects only
   B. Because they cannot walk
   C. To stay warm at night
   D. To avoid being cannibalized by adults

6. Parthenogenesis in Komodo dragons means:
   A. They change sex seasonally
   B. Females can produce offspring from unfertilized eggs, without a male
   C. They lay eggs in water
   D. Two females fertilize each other

7. At a large carcass, access to food among dragons is mainly determined by:
   A. Body size and dominance
   B. Age only
   C. First to arrive, strictly
   D. Random chance

8. The Komodo dragon's teeth are:
   A. Flat, grinding molars
   B. Serrated, blade-like cutting teeth
   C. Fused into a beak
   D. Absent

9. What is the dragon's conservation status on the IUCN Red List?
   A. Least Concern
   B. Extinct in the Wild
   C. Endangered
   D. Not Evaluated

10. A behavior young dragons use to deter adults from eating them is:
    A. Playing dead in open ground
    B. Producing loud alarm calls
    C. Forming defensive herds
    D. Rolling in feces to smell unappealing

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. B
4. C
5. D
6. B
7. A
8. B
9. C
10. D
