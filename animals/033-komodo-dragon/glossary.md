# Glossary — The Komodo Dragon (*Varanus komodoensis*)

- **Island gigantism** — the evolutionary tendency for some animals confined to islands to grow much larger than mainland relatives, often when large predators or competitors are absent.
- **Ambush predator** — a hunter that waits concealed and attacks with a sudden short-range rush rather than a long chase.
- **Monitor lizard (varanid)** — a member of the family Varanidae, a group of active, often large lizards that includes the Komodo dragon.
- **Venom gland** — a gland that produces venom; in the Komodo dragon, glands in the lower jaw deliver compounds that impair clotting and blood pressure.
- **Anticoagulant** — a substance that prevents blood from clotting, promoting continued blood loss from a wound.
- **Jacobson's organ (vomeronasal organ)** — a chemosensory organ in the roof of the mouth that detects scent molecules delivered by the tongue.
- **Forked tongue / chemoreception** — a split tongue whose two tips sample scent from slightly different points, giving directional chemical information used to track food and mates.
- **Cannibalism** — the killing and eating of members of one's own species; in Komodo dragons, adults may eat juveniles.
- **Arboreal juveniles** — young dragons that live in trees, largely to avoid predation by larger, ground-dwelling adults.
- **Parthenogenesis** — reproduction from unfertilized eggs, allowing some females to produce offspring without a male.
- **Serrated teeth** — blade-like, saw-edged teeth that cut deep, ragged wounds when the animal pulls back.
- **Ectotherm** — an animal that relies on external heat sources to regulate body temperature ("cold-blooded"), with generally low energy needs.
- **Endemic** — native to and found only within a particular, often small, geographic area.
- **Osteoderms** — small bony plates embedded in the skin that armor and toughen the body.
- **Feeding aggregation** — a temporary gathering of otherwise solitary animals at a food source, here ordered by body size.
