## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Auffenberg, Walter. *The Behavioral Ecology of the Komodo Monitor.* The foundational long-term field study of Komodo dragon behavior, ecology, feeding, and social structure.
- IUCN Red List — *Varanus komodoensis* species assessment. The authoritative source for the Endangered status, geographic range, population, and threats (including habitat loss and climate/sea-level pressures).
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — *Varanus komodoensis* profile, for taxonomy, diet, size, and general life history.
- Venom research on *Varanus komodoensis* led by Bryan G. Fry and colleagues, describing venom-gland structure and anticoagulant / blood-pressure-lowering venom components that revised the older "septic mouth bacteria" hypothesis. *The general finding is well established; verify the specific paper, journal, and year before final publication.*

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- **Scientific name / taxonomy:** *Varanus komodoensis*, order Squamata, family Varanidae — confirmed via IUCN Red List and Animal Diversity Web.
- **IUCN Red List status:** **Endangered** — confirmed. Uplisted from Vulnerable to Endangered in 2021 (fewer than ~1,400 adults across eight subpopulations; climate/sea-level-driven decline). Book text correct. Sources: IUCN Red List; NPR coverage of the 2021 IUCN update.
- **Venom (not "septic mouth"):** Confirmed. Venom glands in the lower jaw deliver anticoagulant and blood-pressure-lowering compounds; the older bacteria-only hypothesis has been largely overturned — work led by Bryan G. Fry and colleagues. Book text correct; specific paper/year still to be cited from a database.
