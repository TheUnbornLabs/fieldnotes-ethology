# Short Quiz — Termites

1. Termites are most closely related to which group?
   A. Ants
   B. Bees
   C. Cockroaches
   D. Beetles

2. Termite eusociality compared with that of ants is best described as:
   A. Inherited from a common social ancestor
   B. A case of convergent evolution
   C. Identical in genetic basis
   D. Dependent on haplodiploidy

3. Termite workers and soldiers are:
   A. All sterile females
   B. All sterile males
   C. Of both sexes
   D. Fertile reproductives

4. Termites digest cellulose by:
   A. Their own digestive enzymes alone
   B. Symbiotic microbes or cultivated fungus
   C. Not digesting it at all
   D. Chewing it into a paste

5. The self-organising process that coordinates mound construction is called:
   A. Physogastry
   B. Trophallaxis
   C. Stigmergy
   D. Echolocation

6. The fungus cultivated by fungus-growing termites is:
   A. *Termitomyces*
   B. *Penicillium*
   C. *Saccharomyces*
   D. *Cordyceps*

7. In many mound-building termites, the tall mound mainly functions as:
   A. The queen's private chamber
   B. A ventilation and climate-control structure
   C. A food store
   D. A solid defensive wall with termites throughout

8. A termite's most urgent alarm signals are transmitted by:
   A. Loud sounds
   B. Bright flashes
   C. Vibrational head-banging
   D. Ultrasonic calls

9. Unlike a honeybee colony, a termite colony's male reproductive (the king):
   A. Dies immediately after mating
   B. Lives on and reproduces with the queen for years
   C. Does not exist
   D. Becomes a worker

10. Most termite species can best be described ecologically as:
    A. Destructive timber pests
    B. Decomposers and soil engineers
    C. Predators of other insects
    D. Parasites of trees

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. C
6. A
7. B
8. C
9. B
10. B
