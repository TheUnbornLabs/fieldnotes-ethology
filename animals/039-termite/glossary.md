# Glossary — Termites (order Isoptera, within Blattodea)

- **Eusociality** — the highest level of animal social organisation, defined by overlapping generations, cooperative care of young, and a reproductive division of labour with sterile castes.
- **Caste** — a distinct, specialised class of individuals within a colony (e.g. worker, soldier, reproductive) differing in form and role.
- **King and queen** — the founding reproductive pair of a termite colony; unlike in ants and bees, both are long-lived and reproduce together over the colony's lifetime.
- **Worker** — a sterile termite (of either sex) that forages, builds, tends brood, and cultivates fungus.
- **Soldier** — a sterile defensive caste, often with enlarged jaws or a chemical-spraying head, usually unable to feed itself.
- **Alate** — a winged reproductive termite produced for the dispersal (nuptial) flight that founds new colonies.
- **Stigmergy** — coordination in which individuals respond to cues left in the environment by previous work, so that building self-organises without a blueprint or leader.
- **Mound / termitarium** — the constructed nest structure of many termites; in mound-builders, largely an above-ground ventilation and climate-control structure rather than the main living space.
- **Termitomyces / fungus garden** — the cultivated fungus and the spongy "combs" on which fungus-growing termites (Macrotermitinae) grow it to digest plant material.
- **Gut symbionts** — the protists and bacteria living in a termite's gut that break down cellulose; acquired after hatching through social contact rather than inherited in the egg.
- **Cellulose digestion** — the breakdown of the tough plant polymer cellulose, achieved by termites only via microbial symbionts or cultivated fungus, not by the termite alone.
- **Trophallaxis** — the mouth-to-mouth or anus-to-mouth exchange of food and fluid among nestmates, which also spreads pheromones and gut microbes.
- **Physogastry** — the extreme swelling of a mature queen's abdomen with eggs, turning her into a near-immobile egg-laying organ.
- **Nasute soldier** — a soldier caste with a nozzle-like frontal projection used to spray sticky or toxic defensive secretions at intruders.
- **Ecological engineer** — an organism that reshapes its physical environment for many other species; termites engineer soil structure, decomposition, and nutrient cycling.
- **Extended organism** — the concept that a termite colony together with its physiologically active constructed nest behaves in some respects like a single self-regulating organism.
