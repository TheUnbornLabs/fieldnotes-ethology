## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- **Group scope / taxonomy:** Termites are eusocial insects nested within **Blattodea** (the cockroach order), with the wood-feeding *Cryptocercus* cockroaches as their closest relatives — CONFIRMED by modern phylogenetics (Isoptera now treated as an infraorder/epifamily within Blattodea; "termites are eusocial cockroaches"). Book text correct. Sources: Royal Society Proc. B phylogenomic study; Inward et al. "Death of an order" (PMC); Wikipedia (citing these).
- **Diversity:** "Roughly 3,000 described species" is consistent with commonly cited figures — reasonable. Not a conservation concern as a group (correct).
- Mound-ventilation, fungus-mutualism, and gut-symbiont specifics remain flagged above; not individually re-verified here.
