# Glossary — The Poison Dart Frog (Family Dendrobatidae)

**Aposematism** — the strategy of advertising a genuine defence (such as toxicity) with a conspicuous warning signal, typically bright colouration, so that predators learn to avoid the animal.

**Warning coloration** — bright, conspicuous body colours and patterns that serve as an aposematic signal to predators.

**Sequestration** — the process by which the frogs take up and store defensive compounds from their food rather than breaking them down or making them internally.

**Alkaloid** — the class of nitrogen-containing defensive compounds that dendrobatids obtain from arthropod prey and store in their skin.

**Diet-derived toxicity** — toxicity that comes from the animal's diet rather than being synthesised by the animal; captive-bred dart frogs raised on alkaloid-free food are non-toxic.

**Parental care** — behaviours by which parents protect and provision their offspring; unusually well developed in dendrobatids for amphibians.

**Tadpole transport** — the carrying of newly hatched tadpoles on a parent's back to small water bodies where they can develop.

**Obligate egg-feeding** — the *Oophaga pumilio* system in which the mother must repeatedly feed each tadpole unfertilised "nurse" eggs, on which the tadpole entirely depends.

**Advertisement call** — the species-specific male vocalisation used to attract mates and defend territory.

**Territoriality** — the defence of an area (with its calling sites and breeding pools) against rivals, prominent in male dart frogs.

**Müllerian mimicry** — the convergence of two or more genuinely defended species on a shared warning signal, so that each reinforces predators' avoidance.

**Batesian mimicry** — the resemblance of a harmless species to a defended, warning-coloured one, gaining protection without the matching defence.

**Crypsis** — the alternative anti-predator strategy of avoiding detection through camouflage, contrasted with the dart frog's aposematism.

**Chytrid fungus** — *Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis*, the pathogen causing the amphibian disease chytridiomycosis, a major conservation threat to dart frogs.
