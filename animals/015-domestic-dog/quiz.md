# Short Quiz — The Domestic Dog (*Canis familiaris*)

1. The domestic dog descends from:
   A. An extinct population of gray wolves
   B. Modern gray wolves living today
   C. Coyotes
   D. Jackals

2. The timing and geography of dog domestication are best described as:
   A. Fully settled and known precisely
   B. Still genuinely debated and presented cautiously
   C. Proven to be exactly 5,000 years ago in Africa
   D. Irrelevant to dog behavior

3. Compared with wolves, dogs are especially skilled at:
   A. Cooperatively hunting large prey
   B. Maintaining lasting pair bonds
   C. Following human pointing and gaze
   D. Defending rigid territories as a pack

4. The "dominance" or "alpha" approach to dog training is criticized because:
   A. It works too well
   B. It misapplies an outdated captive-wolf model, and dogs are not organized by a rigid hierarchy
   C. Dogs have no social behavior at all
   D. It relies too much on rewards

5. A wagging tail in a dog signals:
   A. Guaranteed friendliness
   B. Arousal and engagement, with meaning depending on context and body language
   C. That the dog is colorblind
   D. Hunger only

6. The "guilty look" in dogs is best explained as:
   A. Proof of human-like moral guilt
   B. A dominance display
   C. An appeasement response to owner cues
   D. A sign of physical illness

7. Dogs' color vision is:
   A. Absent — they see only black and white
   B. Dichromatic, based mainly on blues and yellows
   C. Identical to human vision
   D. Better than human vision in every way

8. Most of the world's dogs are:
   A. Free-ranging village and street dogs
   B. Pedigree show dogs
   C. Working sled dogs
   D. Laboratory animals

9. The socialization period in puppies is:
   A. A single day at birth
   B. A limited early window for forming lasting positive associations
   C. The breeding season
   D. Old age

10. Mutual dog–owner gaze has been reported to engage:
   A. Echolocation
   B. Magnetoreception
   C. A dominance hierarchy
   D. An oxytocin bonding loop resembling the parent–infant bond

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. C
7. B
8. A
9. B
10. D
