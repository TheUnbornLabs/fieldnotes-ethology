# Quiz — The Monarch Butterfly (*Danaus plexippus*)

1. What is unusual about the monarch's annual migration?
   A. A single butterfly flies the whole round trip
   B. No single individual completes the round trip; several generations are involved
   C. Only males migrate
   D. It happens entirely underground

2. The autumn migratory generation of monarchs is notable because it:
   A. Lives only a few days
   B. Cannot fly
   C. Reproduces immediately after emerging
   D. Lives much longer than summer generations and delays reproduction

3. A time-compensated sun compass requires the monarch to combine sun position with:
   A. An internal circadian clock
   B. The phase of the moon
   C. Ocean currents
   D. Echolocation

4. Research by Reppert and colleagues located key circadian clocks for the compass in the monarch's:
   A. Wings
   B. Legs
   C. Antennae
   D. Abdomen

5. The monarch's magnetic compass is described as:
   A. Its only navigation tool
   B. A light-dependent backup to the sun compass
   C. Used only by caterpillars
   D. Nonexistent

6. Monarch caterpillars feed almost exclusively on:
   A. Milkweed
   B. Grasses
   C. Oak leaves
   D. Nectar

7. Cardenolides are:
   A. Nutrients the monarch lacks
   B. A type of pheromone
   C. Toxins from milkweed that monarchs store for defense
   D. Wing pigments with no defensive role

8. The monarch's bright orange-and-black coloration functions as:
   A. Camouflage
   B. A warning (aposematic) signal of toxicity
   C. A cooling mechanism
   D. A mating call substitute

9. One documented risk of mass captive-rearing and release is:
   A. It cures the parasite *Ophryocystis elektroscirrha*
   B. It guarantees population recovery
   C. It makes butterflies larger
   D. It can spread the parasite *Ophryocystis elektroscirrha* and impair migration

10. A key contrast between the monarch and the painted lady (*Vanessa cardui*) is that the monarch is:
    A. A generalist feeder with no fixed overwintering site
    B. Non-migratory
    C. A milkweed specialist that funnels to specific overwintering sites
    D. Chemically undefended

## Answer Key

1. B — No single monarch makes the round trip; it is a multigenerational relay.
2. D — The autumn "super" generation is long-lived and enters reproductive diapause, delaying breeding.
3. A — A time-compensated sun compass corrects for the sun's movement using an internal circadian clock.
4. C — Circadian clocks essential to the compass lie in the antennae (Reppert and colleagues).
5. B — The magnetic compass is a light-dependent backup used when the sun is obscured.
6. A — Monarch caterpillars are milkweed specialists.
7. C — Cardenolides are milkweed toxins that monarchs sequester for defense.
8. B — The orange-and-black pattern is aposematic warning coloration, not camouflage.
9. D — Mass rearing/release can spread *Ophryocystis elektroscirrha* and impair migratory ability.
10. C — The monarch is a milkweed specialist that funnels to specific overwintering sites, unlike the generalist painted lady.
