# Glossary — The Monarch Butterfly (*Danaus plexippus*)

- **Multigenerational migration** — a migration completed by a succession of generations rather than by single individuals, so that no one animal makes the whole round trip.
- **Super generation (Methuselah generation)** — the long-lived autumn generation of monarchs that flies south, overwinters, and begins the return, living far longer than summer generations.
- **Time-compensated sun compass** — an orientation mechanism that reads the sun's position and corrects for its movement across the sky using an internal clock, maintaining a constant heading.
- **Circadian clock** — an internal biological timekeeper running on roughly a 24-hour cycle; in monarchs, clocks in the antennae help compensate the sun compass.
- **Magnetoreception** — the ability to sense the Earth's magnetic field; monarchs use a light-dependent magnetic compass as a backup for the sun compass.
- **Aposematism** — the use of conspicuous warning signals (such as bright coloration) to advertise that an animal is toxic or otherwise unprofitable to attack.
- **Cardenolide** — a class of bitter, heart-affecting steroidal toxins produced by milkweeds; monarchs store them for defense.
- **Sequestration** — the process by which an animal takes up and stores chemicals from its food (here, milkweed toxins) in its own tissues.
- **Milkweed host plant** — plants of the genus *Asclepias* and relatives; the obligate food of monarch caterpillars and the source of their toxins.
- **Overwintering roost** — the dense clusters in which migratory monarchs pass the winter at specific forest (Mexico) or coastal (California) sites.
- **Diapause** — a state of arrested development or reproduction; the autumn migratory generation enters reproductive diapause, delaying breeding until spring.
- **Metamorphosis** — the transformation through distinct life stages; monarchs undergo complete metamorphosis (egg, larva, pupa, adult).
- **Instar** — a stage between molts in a caterpillar's growth; monarch larvae pass through five instars.
- **Spermatophore** — a package of sperm and nutrients transferred from male to female during mating.
- **Aggregation** — a grouping of individuals drawn together by shared preferences or mutual attraction, without cooperative social organization.
