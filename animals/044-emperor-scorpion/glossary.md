# Glossary — The Emperor Scorpion

- **Pincers (pedipalps)** — the large grasping front appendages of the scorpion, used to seize, crush, and hold prey and to grip during courtship and threat.
- **Chelae** — the pincer-like "claws" at the ends of the pedipalps; the emperor scorpion's primary tools for capturing and subduing prey.
- **Telson** — the last tail segment, bearing the venom gland and the stinger; used selectively for larger or struggling prey and in defense.
- **Venom** — the toxic secretion delivered by the telson; in the emperor scorpion it is comparatively mild, in contrast to highly venomous species like the deathstalker.
- **Fluorescence** — the emission of blue-green light by the scorpion's cuticle under ultraviolet illumination, produced by compounds such as beta-carboline; its biological function is debated.
- **Cuticle** — the tough, protective outer exoskeleton of the scorpion, which is the layer responsible for the fluorescent glow.
- **Pectines** — a pair of comb-like sensory appendages under the body, unique to scorpions, dragged over the substrate to sample chemical and textural cues.
- **Trichobothria** — fine, movement-sensitive hairs (notably on the pedipalps) that detect faint air currents, helping the scorpion sense approaching prey and threats in the dark.
- **Substrate vibration** — tremor transmitted through the ground; the scorpion detects it via leg receptors to locate and orient toward moving prey.
- **Viviparity** — giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs; the emperor scorpion is viviparous, with a long internal gestation.
- **Scorpling** — a newborn scorpion; emperor scorplings are pale and soft and ride on the mother's back until their first molt.
- **Maternal care** — the extended protection a female provides to her young, including carrying them on her back and defending them until they harden and disperse.
- **Arachnid** — a member of the class Arachnida (spiders, scorpions, mites, ticks, harvestmen); characterized by eight legs, two body regions, and no wings or antennae — scorpions are arachnids, not insects.
- **Ambush (sit-and-wait) predation** — capturing prey by waiting, hidden and still, for it to come within reach, rather than by actively pursuing it.
- **Spermatophore** — a stalked sperm packet the male deposits on the ground and the female takes up during the courtship "promenade."
- **Promenade à deux** — the tactile courtship "dance" in which the male grips the female's pincers and leads her while seeking a site to deposit the spermatophore.
