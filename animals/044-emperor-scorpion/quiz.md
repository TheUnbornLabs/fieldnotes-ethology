# Quiz — The Emperor Scorpion

1. To which group does the emperor scorpion belong?
   A. Insects
   B. Arachnids
   C. Crustaceans
   D. Myriapods

2. How does the emperor scorpion mainly subdue its prey?
   A. By injecting highly potent venom
   B. By crushing it in its powerful pincers
   C. By spinning a web
   D. By chasing it down over long distances

3. The blue-green glow of the emperor scorpion under UV light is:
   A. Definitely used to lure prey
   B. Produced by bioluminescent bacteria
   C. Fluorescence of the cuticle whose function is still debated
   D. A sign of illness

4. Which sensory structures, unique to scorpions, sample the substrate for chemical and textural cues?
   A. Trichobothria
   B. Pectines
   C. Compound eyes
   D. Antennae

5. The emperor scorpion reproduces by:
   A. Laying eggs in a nest
   B. External fertilization in water
   C. Live birth (viviparity)
   D. Budding

6. Newborn emperor scorplings:
   A. Immediately hunt on their own
   B. Ride on the mother's back until their first molt
   C. Are abandoned at birth
   D. Are carried by the father

7. Compared with the deathstalker, the emperor scorpion has:
   A. Larger pincers and milder venom
   B. Smaller pincers and stronger venom
   C. Both stronger pincers and stronger venom
   D. No pincers at all

8. Which structures detect faint air currents to warn of approaching prey or threats?
   A. Pectines
   B. Book lungs
   C. Trichobothria
   D. The telson

9. Socially, the emperor scorpion is best described as:
   A. Strictly solitary and always aggressive
   B. Unusually tolerant, sometimes forming aggregations, with maternal care
   C. A eusocial species with a queen and workers
   D. Migratory in large swarms

10. The emperor scorpion's conservation concern stems mainly from:
    A. Habitat expansion
    B. Collection for the exotic pet trade (regulated under CITES)
    C. Being a crop pest that is exterminated
    D. Climate-driven range expansion

## Answer Key

1-B, 2-B, 3-C, 4-B, 5-C, 6-B, 7-A, 8-C, 9-B, 10-B
