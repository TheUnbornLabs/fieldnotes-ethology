## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- A. Peter Klimley, *The Secret Life of Sharks* — a leading shark researcher's synthesis of white shark hunting behavior, senses, and ecology.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for the great white shark (*Carcharodon carcharias*), for conservation status, geographic range, and threats such as fishing and bycatch.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species account for *Carcharodon carcharias*.
- The scientific literature on the ampullae of Lorenzini and elasmobranch electroreception — a well-established body of work; name generally and verify specific papers before publication.
- Reports of killer whale (orca) predation on white sharks, including selective feeding on the shark's liver, documented off South Africa and California — name the phenomenon generally; verify the specific studies and locations before publication.
- Standard animal-behavior and ethology texts such as Alcock, *Animal Behavior*, for general framing of senses, predation, and life-history strategy.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Scientific name *Carcharodon carcharias*; Class Chondrichthyes, order Lamniformes, family Lamnidae — confirmed.
- IUCN Red List status Vulnerable (VU A2bd) at the global level — confirmed current (2019 assessment; fewer than ~3,500 mature individuals estimated). Note the Mediterranean subpopulation is separately assessed as Critically Endangered. Sources: iucnredlist.org species 3855; CMS. No change needed.
