# Book 030 — The Great White Shark: Short Quiz

1. What structure warms the white shark's muscles, gut, and brain?
   - A. The rete mirabile
   - B. The swim bladder
   - C. The lateral line
   - D. The claspers

2. The ampullae of Lorenzini detect:
   - A. Dissolved oxygen
   - B. Weak electric fields
   - C. Water temperature only
   - D. Ultraviolet light

3. The classic white shark attack on a seal is launched:
   - A. Horizontally along the surface
   - B. From the beach
   - C. From below, rising against the bright surface
   - D. Only at midday

4. Most bites on humans by white sharks are best described as:
   - A. Sustained predatory attacks with humans as preferred prey
   - B. Defensive venom strikes
   - C. Accidental collisions with no biting
   - D. Often single, exploratory "test" bites after which the shark leaves

5. "Ram ventilation" means the shark:
   - A. Pumps water while resting on the bottom
   - B. Breathes air at the surface
   - C. Drives water over its gills by swimming forward
   - D. Stops breathing while migrating

6. The white shark's countershading is:
   - A. Bright warning coloration
   - B. Dark above and pale below, camouflaging it from both sides
   - C. Identical top and bottom
   - D. Purely for temperature control

7. Oophagy in white sharks refers to:
   - A. Parental feeding of pups after birth
   - B. Filtering plankton
   - C. Scavenging whale carcasses
   - D. Embryos eating unfertilized eggs inside the mother

8. Which is a documented predator of white sharks?
   - A. The killer whale (orca)
   - B. The giant squid
   - C. The bottlenose dolphin
   - D. The manta ray

9. Which statement is TRUE about sharks and disease?
   - A. Sharks are immune to cancer
   - B. Sharks cannot get infections
   - C. Sharks can and do develop tumors and disease
   - D. Shark cartilage cures cancer

10. Why is the white shark classified as Vulnerable?
    - A. It reproduces so fast it exhausts its prey
    - B. Its slow life history and low output make losses hard to replace
    - C. It has no predators, so it overpopulates
    - D. It lives only in one small lagoon

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. C
4. D
5. C
6. B
7. D
8. A
9. C
10. B
