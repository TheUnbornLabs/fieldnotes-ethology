# Book 030 — The Great White Shark: Glossary

- **Lamnid** — a member of the family Lamnidae (mackerel sharks), which includes the white, mako, and porbeagle sharks; streamlined, fast, and regionally endothermic.
- **Regional endothermy** — the ability to keep certain tissues (swimming muscle, gut, brain) warmer than the surrounding water without being fully warm-blooded.
- **Rete mirabile** — a "wonderful net" of intertwined blood vessels that traps metabolic heat and warms specific tissues.
- **Ampullae of Lorenzini** — jelly-filled sensory pores concentrated around the snout that detect weak electric fields.
- **Electroreception** — the sense that detects the bioelectric fields generated by living animals; it guides the shark's final strike when vision is obscured.
- **Lateral line** — a row of motion- and pressure-sensitive organs along the body that detects water displacement, acting like "touch at a distance."
- **Countershading** — camouflage coloring (dark grey above, white below) that conceals the shark from viewers both above and below.
- **Ram ventilation** — driving water over the gills by swimming forward with the mouth open, rather than actively pumping it.
- **Breaching** — leaping clear of the water surface, seen in white sharks during high-speed vertical attacks on prey.
- **Ambush predation** — attacking by surprise from concealment rather than by prolonged pursuit.
- **Oophagy** — intrauterine egg-eating, in which developing embryos consume unfertilized eggs the mother produces inside the uterus.
- **Aplacental viviparity** — giving birth to live young that developed inside the mother without a placental connection, nourished by yolk and (in white sharks) oophagy.
- **Philopatry** — the tendency to return to, or remain faithful to, a particular area such as a nursery or feeding site.
- **Migration** — regular movement between areas; in white sharks this includes basin-scale journeys and offshore aggregations like the "White Shark Café."
- **Apex predator** — a predator at the top of its food web with few or no natural predators as an adult.
