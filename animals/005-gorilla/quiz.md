# Quiz — Book 005: The Gorilla

1. What is a "silverback"?
   A. A juvenile female gorilla
   B. A mature dominant male with silver back hair
   C. A subspecies of eastern gorilla
   D. A type of feeding call

2. Which best describes the typical gorilla diet?
   A. Mainly hunted meat
   B. Mainly herbivorous, with fruit important for western gorillas
   C. Exclusively insects
   D. Fish and shellfish

3. The chest-beat display mainly functions as:
   A. A digestive behaviour
   B. A guaranteed prelude to a lethal attack
   C. A signal advertising the male's size and status
   D. A method of building nests

4. Female transfer refers to:
   A. Males leaving to find food
   B. Females moving from their natal group to another group or male
   C. Infants being carried between trees
   D. Seasonal migration of whole groups

5. Which species is the main focus of this book?
   A. Eastern Gorilla, *Gorilla beringei*
   B. Western Gorilla, *Gorilla gorilla*
   C. Chimpanzee, *Pan troglodytes*
   D. Orangutan, *Pongo*

6. Infanticide risk in gorillas is highest when:
   A. Fruit is abundant
   B. A new male takes over a group with a dependent infant
   C. The group is resting at midday
   D. Juveniles begin to eat solid food

7. Compared with gorillas, chimpanzees are:
   A. Larger and more herbivorous
   B. More frugivorous, meat-hunting, and often more aggressive
   C. Led by a single silverback
   D. Entirely solitary

8. Wild gorilla tool use is best described as:
   A. Common and central to feeding
   B. Rare but documented, e.g. testing water depth with a stick
   C. Never observed
   D. Limited to captivity only

9. What is the IUCN Red List status of the Western Gorilla?
   A. Least Concern
   B. Near Threatened
   C. Vulnerable
   D. Critically Endangered

10. Which researcher is most associated with mountain gorilla study and conservation at Karisoke?
    A. George Schaller only
    B. Dian Fossey
    C. Jane Goodall
    D. Koko's handlers

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. D
10. B
