# References / Further Reading — Book 005: The Gorilla

Sources are separated into well-established works safe to name and specific claims that should be checked against a database before publication. No citations here are invented; uncertain items are flagged.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- George B. Schaller, *The Mountain Gorilla: Ecology and Behavior* — foundational close-range field study of gorilla behaviour and social organisation.
- Dian Fossey, *Gorillas in the Mist* — long-term observation and conservation account arising from the Karisoke Research Center.
- IUCN Red List — species assessments for *Gorilla gorilla* (Western Gorilla, Critically Endangered) and *Gorilla beringei* (Eastern Gorilla), including the Mountain Gorilla subspecies. Consult the current online assessment for up-to-date status and population figures.
- The Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund — recognised conservation organisation continuing daily monitoring, research, and anti-poaching work for mountain gorillas.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live web on 2026-07-02 and confirmed:

- **Scientific name & taxonomy:** Genus *Gorilla*; *Gorilla gorilla* (Western) and *Gorilla beringei* (Eastern), family Hominidae, order Primates — confirmed (IUCN Red List).
- **IUCN Red List status:** Western Gorilla (*Gorilla gorilla*) and Eastern Gorilla (*Gorilla beringei*) both **Critically Endangered**; the Mountain Gorilla subspecies (*Gorilla beringei beringei*) has been downlisted to **Endangered** (recent population ~1,000+) — confirmed via IUCN Red List. The book's Section 2 statement matches this.
