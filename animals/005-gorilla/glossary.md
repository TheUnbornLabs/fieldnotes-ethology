# Glossary — Book 005: The Gorilla

**Silverback** — A mature adult male gorilla with the characteristic silver-grey saddle of hair on his back, typically the dominant leader of a group.

**Blackback** — A younger sexually mature male gorilla that has not yet developed the silver back and usually remains subordinate.

**One-male group (harem)** — A group containing a single breeding male, several females, and their offspring; the common gorilla social unit.

**Folivory** — A feeding strategy based mainly on leaves and other foliage.

**Frugivory** — A feeding strategy based substantially on fruit; more pronounced in western than in mountain gorillas.

**Knuckle-walking** — Quadrupedal locomotion in which the animal bears its weight on the knuckles of the hands.

**Chest-beat display** — A male gorilla's rapid striking of cupped hands against the chest, producing a resonant sound that functions as a signal of body size and status.

**Infanticide** — The killing of an unrelated infant, a risk when a new male takes over a group or a female transfers while carrying a dependent infant.

**Female transfer** — The movement of a female gorilla from her natal group to another group or to a lone silverback.

**Hindgut fermentation** — Digestion of fibrous plant matter by microbes in the enlarged hindgut, releasing energy the gorilla cannot digest directly.

**Sexual dimorphism** — Marked physical difference between the sexes; adult male gorillas are much larger than females.

**Natal group** — The group into which an individual gorilla is born.

**Day range** — The distance a gorilla group travels in a single day, varying with the distribution of food.
