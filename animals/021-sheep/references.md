Per the collection's citation policy, entries are separated into well-established sources that are safe to name and specific claims that must be verified before publication. No citations have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List — species assessments for wild sheep and mouflon (*Ovis* spp.), for the conservation status of the domestic sheep's wild relatives.
- Keith Kendrick and colleagues — published research on face recognition and social cognition in sheep. Named here in general terms; specific papers to be confirmed before publication.
- John Webster — writings on farm animal welfare and the behavioural needs of livestock.
- *Animal Behavior* by John Alcock — standard ethology textbook, for general principles of social behaviour, anti-predator strategy, and communication.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan) — species profile for *Ovis aries*.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Scientific name *Ovis aries* and family/subfamily Bovidae, Caprinae — confirmed. Wild ancestor is the mouflon; the current accepted binomial is *Ovis gmelini* (formerly *Ovis orientalis*), confirmed via species literature (Wikipedia "Mouflon"; ResearchGate/NCBI phylogeny sources). Both names are acceptable; the book's "*Ovis orientalis / gmelini*" is correct.
- Domestic sheep not assessed as a wild species by IUCN — correct (no change needed).
- Sheep face recognition (Kendrick and colleagues): confirmed. Sheep can learn to distinguish ~50 individual sheep faces plus a familiar human face and retain them for up to two years, with processing in the temporal/medial frontal cortex. Verified primary source now identified: Kendrick, K. M., et al. (2001), "Sheep don't forget a face," *Nature* 414: 165–166. Sources: nature.com/articles/35102669; Nature 2001 (ADS 2001Natur.414..165K); Scientific American summary.
