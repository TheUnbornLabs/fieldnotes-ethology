# Quiz — Book 021: The Sheep

1. The wild ancestor of the domestic sheep is:
A) The ibex
B) The mouflon
C) The bighorn sheep
D) The gazelle

2. Sheep flocking behaviour is best understood as:
A) A sign of low intelligence
B) An adaptive anti-predator strategy
C) A result of domestication only
D) Random crowding

3. Research led by Keith Kendrick showed that sheep can:
A) Use simple tools
B) Echolocate in the dark
C) Recognise and remember many individual faces for long periods
D) Count to one hundred

4. The eyes of a sheep provide:
A) Excellent forward depth perception
B) A very wide, panoramic field of view
C) Colour vision like a bee
D) Night vision better than an owl

5. For a sheep, being isolated from the flock typically causes:
A) Calm and relaxation
B) Intense stress
C) No measurable change
D) Improved feeding

6. A ewe recognises her own lamb largely through:
A) The lamb's footprints
B) Random chance
C) The shepherd's guidance
D) Scent and the lamb's distinctive call

7. "Allelomimetic behaviour" refers to:
A) Fighting for dominance
B) Doing what one's neighbours do
C) Hiding young in vegetation
D) Solitary browsing

8. Compared with sheep, goats are more likely to be:
A) Tight-flocking grazers
B) Independent browsers and explorers
C) Nocturnal predators
D) Unable to climb

9. Lambs are described as precocial, meaning they:
A) Are born blind and helpless
B) Are hidden for weeks after birth
C) Can stand and follow their mother soon after birth
D) Cannot recognise their mother

10. Selective breeding of sheep has produced specialised types for:
A) Wool, meat, and milk
B) Racing and hunting
C) Guarding and tracking
D) Egg production

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. D
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. A
