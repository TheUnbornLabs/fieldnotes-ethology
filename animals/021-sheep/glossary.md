# Glossary — Book 021: The Sheep

- **Flocking** — the tendency to form and stay in a cohesive group, the sheep's primary anti-predator strategy.
- **Follower behaviour** — the strong tendency to move in the direction taken by other flock members.
- **Allelomimetic behaviour** — doing what neighbours do; coordinated, contagious group action such as grazing or fleeing together.
- **Mouflon** — the wild ancestor of the domestic sheep (*Ovis orientalis / gmelini*).
- **Face recognition** — the ability to identify and remember specific individuals by their faces, shown strongly in sheep.
- **Social hierarchy** — the loose set of dominance relationships that reduce conflict within a flock.
- **Flight zone** — the personal space around an animal; when crossed, the animal moves away, a principle used in low-stress handling.
- **Grazer** — an animal specialised to feed on low-growing grasses and forbs, as opposed to a browser.
- **Mother–lamb bond** — the specific, exclusive recognition and attachment formed between a ewe and her lamb soon after birth.
- **Vocal recognition** — identifying specific individuals by their distinctive calls, as ewes and lambs do.
- **Isolation stress** — the intense stress response a sheep shows when separated from its group.
- **Selective breeding** — human-directed breeding that shaped sheep into specialised wool, meat, and dairy types.
- **Ruminant** — a mammal that ferments plant food in a multi-chambered stomach and re-chews it as cud.
- **Precocial** — describing young that are mobile and well-developed at birth, like lambs.
- **Flehmen response** — a lip-curling behaviour that draws scent cues over a specialised sensory organ, used in reproduction.
