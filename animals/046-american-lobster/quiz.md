# Short Quiz — The American Lobster

1. The American lobster maintains dominance mainly by:
   A. Repeatedly re-fighting every rival it meets
   B. Recognizing former opponents by chemical (urine) signals and deferring
   C. Loud warning sounds
   D. Changing color to signal rank

2. A lobster releases its social urine signals from the:
   A. Tips of the walking legs
   B. Mouthparts
   C. Nephropores at the base of the antennae, below the eyes
   D. Underside of the tail

3. The lobster's main organs of distance chemoreception are the:
   A. Compound eyes
   B. Antennules, bearing aesthetasc hairs
   C. Statocysts
   D. Swimmerets

4. A lobster's two claws are best described as:
   A. A heavy crusher and a slender cutter, each specialized
   B. Identical mirror images
   C. Both used only for display
   D. Both used only for swimming

5. Lobster growth is called indeterminate because the animal:
   A. Stops growing at maturity
   B. Shrinks each winter
   C. Grows continuously throughout life via repeated molting
   D. Cannot grow once its shell hardens

6. The "meral spread" is:
   A. A feeding method
   B. A threat posture that makes the lobster look larger
   C. A type of larva
   D. A migration pattern

7. Why do wild lobsters turn red only when cooked?
   A. They blush when stressed
   B. They are painted by fishers
   C. Cold water bleaches them
   D. Heat frees a red pigment (astaxanthin) normally masked by protein

8. In lobster mating, the female typically:
   A. Mates while her shell is hard and she is solitary
   B. Fights the male to the death first
   C. Molts and mates in the protected shelter of a dominant male
   D. Provides no chemical signals

9. The claim that lobsters "scream" when boiled is false because they:
   A. Have no vocal apparatus; the sound is escaping gas/steam
   B. Are already dead
   C. Cannot be heated
   D. Only hiss underwater

10. Compared with the American lobster, the spiny lobster is:
    A. Clawed and solitary
    B. Clawless and known for single-file queuing migrations
    C. A freshwater animal
    D. Unable to sense chemicals

## Answer Key

1: B; 2: C; 3: B; 4: A; 5: C; 6: B; 7: D; 8: C; 9: A; 10: B
