# Glossary — The American Lobster

- **Benthic** — living on or near the bottom of a body of water; the lobster is a bottom-dwelling seafloor animal.
- **Agonistic behavior** — the range of behavior connected to conflict, including threat, aggression, submission, and retreat, seen in lobster contests.
- **Dominance hierarchy** — a ranking of individuals by competitive status that determines priority of access to resources such as shelters and mates.
- **Winner effect / loser effect** — the tendency for a recent winner to be more likely to win again, and a recent loser to lose again, because of lasting internal changes.
- **Chemical individual recognition** — the ability to identify a specific other individual by its unique chemical signature; lobsters recognize former opponents by urine cues.
- **Nephropore** — the excretory opening from which a lobster releases urine, located at the base of the antennae below the eyes; the source of social urine signals.
- **Antennule** — the short, forked first antenna, bearing dense chemosensory hairs; the lobster's main organ of distance "smell."
- **Aesthetasc** — a specialized chemosensory hair on the antennule that detects dissolved chemicals in the water.
- **Chemoreception** — the detection of chemical signals, the lobster's dominant sense for finding food, sensing predators, and reading other lobsters.
- **Molting / ecdysis** — the shedding of the old exoskeleton to grow a new, larger one; the only way a hard-shelled lobster can increase in size.
- **Indeterminate growth** — continued growth throughout life rather than stopping at a fixed adult size; a feature of lobsters achieved through repeated molting.
- **Meral spread** — a threat posture in which a lobster raises and opens its claws and spreads the leg segments to appear larger.
- **Crusher claw** — the heavy, blunt-toothed claw used to break hard shells.
- **Cutter claw** — the slender, sharp-toothed claw (also called ripper or pincer) used to seize and tear.
- **Autotomy** — the deliberate shedding of a claw or leg at a preformed breakage plane to escape, followed by regeneration over later molts.
- **Nociception** — the detection of harmful or potentially damaging stimuli; distinct from, and central to the debate over, whether lobsters feel pain.
