# Glossary — Ants (family Formicidae)

**Eusociality** — the highest level of social organisation, marked by overlapping generations, cooperative care of young, and a reproductive division of labour between reproducers and non-reproducing helpers.
**Superorganism** — a colony treated as a single biological unit, with reproductive members acting like a germline and workers like the body.
**Pheromone trail** — a chemical path laid by foraging ants to guide nestmates to food; it evaporates unless reinforced, so only useful routes persist.
**Stigmergy** — coordination through changes made to the shared environment, where one individual's modification cues the behaviour of others.
**Division of labour** — the partitioning of colony tasks among different individuals (e.g. reproduction, foraging, brood care, defence).
**Polymorphism** — the presence of workers of different sizes and body forms suited to different tasks within one colony.
**Caste** — a class of colony member with a distinct role and often form (queen, worker, soldier), largely determined by rearing conditions.
**Trophallaxis** — mouth-to-mouth (or anus-to-mouth) sharing of liquid food among colony members, distributing nutrients and chemical signals.
**Cuticular hydrocarbons (CHCs)** — waxy chemicals on an ant's body surface that carry its colony identity and enable nestmate recognition.
**Trophobiosis** — a mutualism in which ants tend honeydew-producing insects such as aphids, collecting sugary secretions in exchange for protection.
**Kin selection** — evolution of helping behaviour because it aids relatives who share the helper's genes.
**Haplodiploidy** — the genetic system in which females are diploid (from fertilised eggs) and males haploid (from unfertilised eggs), affecting relatedness in the colony.
**Nuptial flight** — the mating flight in which winged reproductives from many colonies leave to mate.
**Bivouac** — a temporary living "nest" formed from the interlinked bodies of army ant workers, sheltering the queen and brood.
**Formic acid** — a defensive chemical sprayed by some ants (such as wood ants); the acid is named after ants (Latin *formica*).
**Stridulation** — production of sound or vibration by rubbing body parts together, used by some ants for alarm or distress signalling.
