# References / Further Reading — Ants (family Formicidae)

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those that should be checked against a database before final publication. Following the collection's citation policy, no authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, or page numbers have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Bert Hölldobler and Edward O. Wilson, *The Ants* — the standard comprehensive scholarly reference on ant biology and behaviour.
- Bert Hölldobler and Edward O. Wilson, *The Superorganism: The Beauty, Elegance, and Strangeness of Insect Societies* — on colony-level organisation and the superorganism concept.
- Deborah M. Gordon, *Ant Encounters: Interaction Networks and Colony Behavior* — on decentralised colony behaviour and task allocation, especially in harvester ants.
- AntWiki — a collaborative, expert-curated reference on ant taxonomy, biology, and distribution (verify specific entries as needed).
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — profiles for many ant genera and species.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as eusociality, kin selection, communication, and self-organisation.
