# Short Quiz — The House Mouse (*Mus musculus*)

1. The house mouse is best described ecologically as:
   A. A commensal species living alongside humans
   B. A strictly wild species avoiding humans
   C. An aquatic rodent
   D. A strictly diurnal grassland grazer

2. Thigmotaxis in mice refers to:
   A. A preference for open spaces
   B. The tendency to hug walls and stay near surfaces
   C. A type of vocalization
   D. A form of caching

3. Major urinary proteins (MUPs) primarily function to:
   A. Digest cellulose
   B. Carry and release pheromones and encode individual identity
   C. Provide camouflage
   D. Store fat for winter

4. The Bruce effect is:
   A. Estrus induced by male odor in grouped females
   B. Pregnancy blocked by exposure to an unfamiliar male's odor
   C. A form of nest-building
   D. A predator-avoidance freeze

5. Male mouse "songs" are produced:
   A. In the audible range as loud squeaks
   B. In the ultrasonic range, above human hearing
   C. Only by pups
   D. Only during fights between males

6. The preferred natural foods of house mice are:
   A. Cheese above all else
   B. Fresh meat only
   C. Seeds, grains, and sweet high-calorie foods
   D. Leaves and grass exclusively

7. Which statement about laboratory mice is correct?
   A. They behave identically to wild mice
   B. Inbred lab strains differ behaviorally and physiologically from wild mice
   C. They are a different species from wild mice
   D. They cannot reproduce

8. The dominant sense in a mouse's world is:
   A. Vision
   B. Smell (and touch)
   C. Echolocation
   D. Electroreception

9. An inbred strain is produced by:
   A. Crossing distantly related wild mice
   B. Genetic engineering of a single gene
   C. Selecting only for coat color
   D. Many generations of sibling mating until animals are near-identical

10. Wild house mouse social groups (demes) typically consist of:
    A. Many equal, unrelated adults
    B. Solitary individuals that never interact
    C. A dominant male, several breeding females, and their offspring
    D. Pairs that mate for life with no offspring nearby

## Answer Key

1-A, 2-B, 3-B, 4-B, 5-B, 6-C, 7-B, 8-B, 9-D, 10-C
