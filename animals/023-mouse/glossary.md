# Glossary — The House Mouse (*Mus musculus*)

**Commensal** — living in close association with humans, benefiting from our structures and stored food, as the house mouse does.
**Territoriality** — defense of a space and its resources against rivals; in house mice, chiefly a behavior of dominant males.
**Scent-marking** — depositing urine or other secretions to communicate identity, status, and territory.
**Major urinary proteins (MUPs)** — proteins secreted in mouse urine that bind and release volatile pheromones and encode individual identity, functioning almost like a chemical signature.
**Bruce effect** — the blocking of a newly mated female's pregnancy by exposure to the odor of an unfamiliar male, after which she returns to estrus.
**Whitten effect** — synchronization or induction of estrus in grouped females by exposure to male odor.
**Ultrasonic vocalization (USV)** — a call produced above the human hearing range (roughly above 20 kHz), used by mice in social and courtship contexts, including song-like male courtship sequences.
**Thigmotaxis** — the tendency to stay in contact with surfaces and hug walls, avoiding open space; an anti-predator caution.
**Nocturnal** — primarily active at night.
**Vomeronasal organ** — an accessory olfactory structure specialized for detecting pheromones and social chemical signals.
**Inbred strain** — a population bred for many generations of sibling mating until individuals are genetically near-identical, used to standardize research.
**Knockout** — a mouse genetically engineered so that a specific gene is inactivated, used to study that gene's function.
**Nest-building** — construction of an insulated nest from gathered material; central to mouse maternal care and used as an index of maternal motivation.
**Model organism** — a species studied intensively to understand biological processes shared with other species, including humans.
**Altricial** — born in an undeveloped, helpless state (hairless, blind, deaf) requiring extended parental care.
**Deme** — a local breeding group of mice, typically a dominant male, several breeding females, and their young.
