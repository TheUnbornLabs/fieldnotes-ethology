# Short Quiz — The Tiger (*Panthera tigris*)

1. The tiger is best described as:
   A. A social cat that lives in prides
   B. A solitary, territorial ambush predator
   C. A cursorial pursuit hunter
   D. A cooperative pack hunter

2. A tiger's home range compared between the sexes is typically:
   A. Larger for females than males
   B. Identical for both sexes
   C. Larger for males, often overlapping several female ranges
   D. Shared equally within a group territory

3. The tiger's primary channel for communicating over time and distance is:
   A. Cooperative howling
   B. Scent-marking, scrapes, and long-distance calls
   C. Visual displays only
   D. Touch

4. Tiger cubs are raised:
   A. By both parents together
   B. Communally by several adults
   C. By the mother alone
   D. By the father after the mother leaves

5. A tiger's hunting method relies mainly on:
   A. A long endurance chase
   B. Stalking under cover and a short, explosive attack
   C. Cooperative encirclement
   D. Scavenging carcasses

6. Which statement about tiger stripes is correct?
   A. They are identical in every tiger
   B. They exist only on the fur
   C. They are individually unique and used to identify individuals
   D. They serve no camouflage function

7. Which claim about tigers and water is accurate?
   A. Tigers cannot swim
   B. Tigers avoid all water
   C. Tigers are strong, willing swimmers, unusual among cats
   D. Only cubs can swim

8. Man-eating by tigers is best described as:
   A. Normal, preferred tiger behavior
   B. Rare and context-specific
   C. Impossible
   D. Universal across all populations

9. The key ecological difference between the lion and the tiger is that:
   A. Only tigers hunt large prey
   B. The lion is social (pride-living) while the tiger is solitary
   C. Only lions can swim
   D. They compete directly in the wild

10. The tiger's IUCN Red List status is:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Endangered
   C. Extinct in the Wild
   D. Not assessed

## Answer Key

1-B, 2-C, 3-B, 4-C, 5-B, 6-C, 7-C, 8-B, 9-B, 10-B
