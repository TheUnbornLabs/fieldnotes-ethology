# References / Further Reading — The Tiger (*Panthera tigris*)

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those that should be checked against a database before final publication. Following the collection's citation policy, no authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, or page numbers have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Mel Sunquist and Fiona Sunquist, *Wild Cats of the World* — a standard scholarly reference on the biology and behavior of the world's wild cats, including the tiger.
- George B. Schaller, *The Deer and the Tiger* — a foundational field study of tiger ecology and predator–prey relationships in India.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Panthera tigris* (status: Endangered). Consult the current entry for range, population, subspecies, and threats.
- Panthera — a recognised wild-cat conservation organisation providing science-based information on tiger biology and conservation.
- WWF (World Wide Fund for Nature) — recognised conservation organisation with science-based tiger status and programme information.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for *Panthera tigris*.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as territoriality, communication, and mating systems.
