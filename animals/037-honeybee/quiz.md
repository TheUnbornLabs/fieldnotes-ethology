# Quiz — The Honeybee (*Apis mellifera*)

1. What does the *direction* of the straight run in a waggle dance encode?
   A. The distance to the resource
   B. The direction of the resource relative to the sun
   C. The identity of the flower species
   D. The number of bees needed

2. Who is credited with decoding the honeybee dance language?
   A. Thomas Seeley
   B. Charles Darwin
   C. Karl von Frisch
   D. Niko Tinbergen

3. Temporal polyethism means that workers divide labor by:
   A. Age
   B. Body size
   C. Genetic caste at birth
   D. Random assignment

4. In honeybees, what determines whether a female larva becomes a queen or a worker?
   A. The egg is genetically different
   B. How the larva is fed (notably royal jelly)
   C. The cell's location only
   D. The queen chooses each one

5. During nest-site selection, a swarm commits to a site when:
   A. The queen points to it
   B. The first scout returns
   C. Scout support passes a quorum threshold
   D. All scouts agree unanimously

6. Which signal recruits more bees to *receive and process* incoming nectar?
   A. Round dance
   B. Stop signal
   C. Alarm pheromone
   D. Tremble dance

7. Why does a honeybee worker usually die after stinging a mammal?
   A. Her barbed sting lodges in the skin and tears away
   B. She runs out of venom
   C. She is attacked by other bees
   D. Stinging exhausts her

8. The queen's main role in the colony is best described as:
   A. Directing foraging and defense
   B. Being the colony's reproductive, not its commander
   C. Choosing the nest site
   D. Building the comb

9. In hot weather, how do honeybees cool the nest?
   A. By clustering tightly together
   B. By shivering their flight muscles
   C. By fanning and evaporating collected water
   D. By leaving the nest entirely

10. Which is the most accurate statement about bees and honey?
    A. All bee species hoard honey
    B. Only honeybees and a few relatives hoard large honey stores
    C. No bees store honey
    D. Only queens make honey

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. A
4. B
5. C
6. D
7. A
8. B
9. C
10. B
