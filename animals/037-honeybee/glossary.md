# Glossary — The Honeybee (*Apis mellifera*)

- **Eusociality:** The highest level of social organization, defined by cooperative brood care, overlapping generations, and a reproductive division of labor between reproductive and non-reproductive individuals.
- **Superorganism:** A colony so integrated that it functions as a single organism, with the colony (not the individual) acting as the primary unit of behavior and selection.
- **Temporal polyethism:** Age-based division of labor, in which a worker progresses through a sequence of tasks (nursing, building, guarding, foraging) as she ages.
- **Waggle dance:** A figure-eight dance in which a forager encodes the direction (relative to the sun) and distance of a distant resource.
- **Round dance:** A simpler circling dance signaling that a resource is close to the hive, without precise directional information.
- **Tremble dance:** A dance that recruits additional bees to receive and process incoming nectar when foragers cannot be unloaded quickly enough.
- **Stop signal:** A brief vibrational signal that inhibits recruitment and helps resolve competition between options during nest-site selection.
- **Quorum sensing:** Reaching a decision once the number of individuals supporting an option passes a threshold, as scouts do when committing a swarm to a nest site.
- **Queen mandibular pheromone:** A pheromone blend produced by the queen that signals her presence, suppresses new-queen rearing, and helps cohere the colony.
- **Alarm pheromone:** A chemical signal released near the sting that marks a target and rapidly recruits defenders.
- **Drone:** A male honeybee, produced mainly in the reproductive season, whose role is to mate with queens from other colonies.
- **Forager / scout:** A worker in the final, food-collecting phase of life; a scout is a forager that searches for new resources or nest sites and recruits others.
- **Swarming:** Colony-level reproduction in which the old queen leaves with about half the workers to found a new nest, dividing one colony into two.
- **Thermoregulation:** The colony's active control of nest temperature by clustering and shivering for warmth, and fanning and evaporating water for cooling.
- **Haplodiploidy:** The Hymenopteran sex-determination system in which males develop from unfertilized eggs and females from fertilized eggs, affecting genetic relatedness.
- **Royal jelly:** A glandular secretion fed to larvae; abundant feeding of it directs a larva to develop into a queen rather than a worker.
