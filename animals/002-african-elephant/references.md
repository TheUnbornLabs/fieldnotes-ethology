# References / Further Reading — The African Elephant

Sources are separated into well-established works safe to name and items that should be checked against a database before final publication. No citations have been invented; uncertain entries are flagged.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Loxodonta africana* (African savanna elephant). The standard reference for conservation status and range.
- Cynthia Moss, *Elephant Memories: Thirteen Years in the Life of an Elephant Family* — foundational long-term field study of the Amboseli population.
- Amboseli Elephant Research Project (AERP) — one of the longest-running studies of wild elephants; a primary source on social structure, matriarchs, and life history.
- ElephantVoices (Joyce Poole and colleagues) — reference resource on elephant vocal and gestural communication, including rumbles and infrasound.
- Katy Payne, *Silent Thunder: In the Presence of Elephants* — accessible account of the discovery and study of elephant infrasound.
- IUCN and CITES documentation on the ivory trade and elephant conservation — for poaching, trade, and management context.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live web on 2026-07-02 and confirmed:

- **Scientific name & taxonomy:** *Loxodonta africana* (African savanna elephant), order Proboscidea, family Elephantidae; forest elephant *Loxodonta cyclotis* now treated as a distinct species — confirmed (IUCN Red List).
- **IUCN Red List status:** The 2021 IUCN reassessment split African elephants into two species and lists the African savanna elephant (*Loxodonta africana*) as **Endangered** — confirmed via IUCN Red List (iucnredlist.org, species 181008073). This resolves the item previously flagged in the "to verify" list below.
