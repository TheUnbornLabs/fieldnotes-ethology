# Quiz — The African Elephant

1. The African savanna elephant's scientific name is:
A. *Elephas maximus*
B. *Loxodonta cyclotis*
C. *Loxodonta africana*
D. *Mammuthus primigenius*

2. Elephant family units are led by:
A. The largest bull
B. An experienced adult female (matriarch)
C. A mated pair
D. Rotating leadership among calves

3. "Fission-fusion" society means:
A. Groups never change membership
B. Groups regularly split and merge depending on conditions
C. Males and females live entirely separately year-round
D. Only two elephants travel together at a time

4. Elephant long-distance vocal communication relies heavily on:
A. Ultrasound above human hearing
B. Infrasound below human hearing
C. Echolocation
D. Visual flashes

5. Besides air, elephant signals can travel through the ground as:
A. Electric fields
B. Seismic vibrations detected through the feet and trunk
C. Magnetic pulses
D. Chemical trails only

6. Musth is best described as:
A. A seasonal migration
B. A hormonally driven state in bulls linked to mating and aggression
C. A calf's developmental stage
D. A type of alarm call

7. Elephant gestation lasts approximately:
A. 9 months
B. 12 months
C. 22 months
D. 36 months

8. Elephant tusks are:
A. Horns made of keratin
B. Modified canine teeth
C. Elongated upper incisor teeth made of dentine
D. Fused nasal bones

9. Regarding responses to dead companions, the careful scientific view is that elephants:
A. Hold organized funerals
B. Feel grief identical to humans
C. Show heightened interest in and specific responses to dead individuals, best described without assuming human grief
D. Ignore dead elephants entirely

10. A key difference between African savanna and Asian elephants is:
A. Only African elephants are social
B. Asian elephants are larger with bigger ears
C. African savanna elephants generally have larger ears, two trunk-tip projections, and tusks common in both sexes
D. Only Asian elephants use infrasound

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. C
8. C
9. C
10. C
