# Glossary — The European Rabbit (*Oryctolagus cuniculus*)

- **Lagomorph** — a member of the order Lagomorpha (rabbits, hares, and pikas); distinct from rodents, with a second small pair of upper incisors.
- **Warren** — an interconnected network of burrows and chambers dug and shared by a rabbit colony, serving as its home and refuge.
- **Colonial** — living in a permanent, socially organized group; the European rabbit is the only truly colonial lagomorph.
- **Dominance hierarchy** — a ranked social order that determines priority of access to resources such as safe burrows and mates.
- **Crepuscular** — most active during the twilight hours of dawn and dusk.
- **Cecotrophy** — the strategy of reingesting soft, nutrient-rich droppings (cecotropes) to pass food through the gut a second time.
- **Cecotrope** — the soft, nutrient-rich dropping produced by hindgut fermentation that the rabbit re-eats, as opposed to the hard fecal pellet.
- **Chinning** — depositing scent by rubbing the chin gland on objects to mark territory and signal status.
- **Chin gland** — the scent gland beneath the rabbit's chin used in chinning.
- **Induced ovulation** — release of eggs triggered by the act of mating itself rather than on a fixed cycle.
- **Altricial** — describing young born helpless, blind, and undeveloped, requiring care in a nest (as in rabbit kits).
- **Form** — a shallow above-ground resting scrape used by hares (contrasted with the rabbit's underground nest).
- **Nest (nesting burrow / stop)** — the short, dead-end tunnel, lined with grass and fur and often plugged with soil, in which a doe raises her altricial kits.
- **Thumping** — striking the ground with a hind foot to produce an alarm sound and vibration warning the colony.
- **Keystone prey** — a prey species so central to an ecosystem that predators and the wider community depend on it.
- **Precocial** — describing young born well-developed and mobile, as in the hare's leverets (contrasted with altricial).
