# Short Quiz — The European Rabbit (*Oryctolagus cuniculus*)

1. To which order does the rabbit belong?
   A. Rodentia
   B. Lagomorpha
   C. Carnivora
   D. Insectivora

2. The European rabbit is unusual among lagomorphs because it is:
   A. Nocturnal and migratory
   B. The only truly colonial, warren-dwelling species
   C. A strict carnivore
   D. Unable to dig

3. Cecotrophy is best described as:
   A. Storing food for winter
   B. Eating other animals' droppings
   C. Reingesting the rabbit's own soft, nutrient-rich droppings
   D. Grinding food with a gizzard

4. Chinning is a form of:
   A. Vocal communication
   B. Scent-marking using the chin gland
   C. Aggressive biting
   D. Grooming a partner

5. A rabbit thumps its hind foot mainly to:
   A. Dig a burrow
   B. Attract a mate
   C. Signal alarm to the colony
   D. Cool itself down

6. Rabbit kits are described as altricial, meaning they are born:
   A. Fully furred and able to run
   B. Blind, naked, and helpless
   C. Already weaned
   D. In the open, not in a nest

7. "Induced ovulation" means the female rabbit releases eggs:
   A. Only once a year
   B. On a strict monthly cycle
   C. In response to the act of mating
   D. Without ever mating

8. The near-panoramic vision of a rabbit comes at the cost of:
   A. Any hearing ability
   B. Fine detail and a blind spot in front of the nose
   C. The ability to see movement
   D. Seeing overhead

9. In its native Iberia, the European rabbit is:
   A. Extinct in the wild
   B. Of Least Concern
   C. Endangered and a keystone prey species
   D. An introduced pest

10. Compared with the European rabbit, the European brown hare:
    A. Lives in larger warrens
    B. Is solitary, does not dig warrens, and has precocial young
    C. Has altricial young in burrows
    D. Cannot run fast

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. C
10. B
