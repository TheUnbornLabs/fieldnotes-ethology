# Short Quiz — The Goat (*Capra hircus*)

1. The domestic goat descends from:
   A. The domestic sheep
   B. The wild bezoar goat (*Capra aegagrus*)
   C. The ibex of the Alps only
   D. An extinct wild cattle species

2. Goats are best classified as:
   A. Grazers that prefer grass
   B. Selective browsers that prefer leaves, shoots, and twigs
   C. Carnivores
   D. Filter feeders

3. Goat domestication is generally placed in the:
   A. Industrial era in Europe
   B. Early Neolithic Fertile Crescent
   C. Ice Age tundra of Siberia
   D. Modern Americas

4. In the lever-and-cup research, goats:
   A. Failed to learn the task at all
   B. Learned the task and remembered the solution for many months
   C. Only succeeded when copying another goat
   D. Forgot the solution within a day

5. When a goat cannot solve a problem, research shows it tends to:
   A. Ignore any nearby humans completely
   B. Gaze toward a nearby person, as dogs do
   C. Fall asleep
   D. Attack the apparatus

6. The popular idea that goats "eat tin cans" is explained by:
   A. Goats actually digesting metal
   B. Goats investigating objects with their mobile prehensile lips, not eating metal
   C. A dietary need for iron
   D. Goats mistaking cans for grass

7. So-called "fainting" goats:
   A. Lose consciousness from fear
   B. Have myotonia congenita, a harmless muscle condition, and stay awake
   C. Are a separate species from other goats
   D. Faint from low blood sugar

8. Compared with sheep, goats are:
   A. Tighter-flocking and more grass-eating
   B. More independent, exploratory browsers inclined to climb
   C. Unable to form social bonds
   D. Strictly solitary animals

9. Goat "accents" refer to:
   A. Regional differences in horn shape
   B. Vocal plasticity, whereby goats raised together develop more similar calls
   C. A myth with no evidence
   D. Differences in walking gait

10. Goat dominance hierarchies are established mainly through:
   A. Cooperative food sharing
   B. Horn displays and head-to-head contests
   C. Vocal duets only
   D. Scent marking of territory boundaries

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. B
10. B
