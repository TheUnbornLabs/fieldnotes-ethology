## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live IUCN Red List and Animal Diversity Web:

- The domestic goat, *Capra hircus* (order Artiodactyla, family Bovidae, subfamily Caprinae), descended from the wild **bezoar goat** (*Capra aegagrus*) — confirmed as accepted taxonomy. Source: Animal Diversity Web.
- As a domesticated animal the goat is **not IUCN-assessed**; the bezoar goat *Capra aegagrus* is the IUCN-assessed wild progenitor (assessment by Weinberg et al.; category **Near Threatened**) — confirmed that the wild ancestor is the assessed taxon. Source: IUCN Red List, *Capra aegagrus* (iucnredlist.org). The book asserts only that the bezoar goat is the assessed taxon and that wild caprines face hunting/habitat pressure, which is consistent.
