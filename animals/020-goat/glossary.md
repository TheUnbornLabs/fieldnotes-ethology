# Glossary — The Goat (*Capra hircus*)

**Browser** — an herbivore that feeds selectively on the leaves, shoots, twigs, and bark of shrubs and trees rather than on grass; the goat's primary feeding mode.
**Grazer** — an herbivore that feeds mainly on grasses cropped close to the ground; the primary feeding mode of sheep and cattle, contrasted with browsing.
**Bezoar goat (*Capra aegagrus*)** — the wild mountain caprine of the Middle East and Central Asia from which the domestic goat descends; its behavior underlies the goat's agility, climbing, and browsing.
**Neolithic domestication** — the early-farming-era process, roughly ten to eleven thousand years ago in the Fertile Crescent, by which goats and other early livestock were domesticated from wild ancestors.
**Prehensile lips** — the goat's extremely mobile, dexterous lips, used to investigate objects by touch and to select individual plant parts with precision.
**Selective feeding** — choosing among available plants and plant parts for the most nutritious or palatable, rather than eating indiscriminately; characteristic of the goat.
**Ruminant** — a hoofed mammal with a multi-chambered stomach that ferments plant material and re-chews regurgitated cud, allowing use of tough, fibrous forage.
**Problem-solving** — the ability to work out how to obtain a goal, such as operating a lever-and-cup mechanism to release food; demonstrated in goats by trial-and-error learning.
**Long-term memory** — the retention of learned information over extended periods; goats have been shown to remember a learned foraging task for many months.
**Contact call** — a bleat used to maintain or re-establish contact between group members, especially between a mother and her kid, who recognize one another by voice.
**Vocal plasticity** — the capacity to modify vocalizations according to social environment; goats raised together develop more similar calls, sometimes called "accents."
**Referential / audience-directed behavior** — communication directed at a recipient about something in the environment; goats reorient between an unsolvable problem and a person.
**Human-directed gaze** — the goat's tendency to look toward a nearby human when it cannot solve a task, interpreted as soliciting help, comparable to behavior seen in dogs.
**Dominance hierarchy** — a ranked social order, in goats established mainly through horn displays and head-to-head contests, that determines priority of access to resources.
**Myotonia congenita** — the harmless inherited muscle condition of "fainting" (myotonic) goats, causing brief muscle stiffening when startled; not fear and not loss of consciousness.
**Precocial** — describing young born in an advanced state, able to stand and move soon after birth, as goat kids are.
