## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- **Scientific name / taxonomy:** *Chelonia mydas*, family Cheloniidae, sole living species of genus *Chelonia* — confirmed via IUCN Red List (species ID 4615) and Animal Diversity Web.
- **IUCN Red List status — CORRECTED:** Now assessed globally as **Least Concern**, not Endangered. The species was downlisted from Endangered (held since 1982) to Least Concern in the 2025 Red List update (assessment version 2025-2; announced at the 2025 IUCN World Conservation Congress, Abu Dhabi), reflecting a ~28% global population increase since the 1970s. Book text and summary corrected accordingly. Regional subpopulations remain threatened.
