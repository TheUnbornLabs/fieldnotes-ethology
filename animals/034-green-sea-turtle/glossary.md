# Glossary — Book 034: The Green Sea Turtle

- **Natal homing** — the behaviour by which adults return to breed at or near the location where they themselves were born or hatched.
- **Magnetoreception** — the ability to detect the Earth's magnetic field and use it for orientation and navigation.
- **Geomagnetic imprinting** — the hypothesis that a young animal learns the magnetic signature of its natal region and uses that memory to return there as an adult.
- **Temperature-dependent sex determination (TSD)** — a system in which incubation temperature, rather than sex chromosomes, decides an embryo's sex; in green turtles warmer sand yields more females.
- **Migration** — regular, often seasonal, long-distance movement between separated habitats, here between feeding grounds and nesting beaches.
- **Nest-site fidelity** — the tendency of a female to return to the same beach or region to lay eggs across breeding seasons.
- **Philopatry** — an animal's tendency to remain in, or return to, its home area or birthplace.
- **Hatchling frenzy** — the burst of synchronised emergence and vigorous crawling and swimming by which hatchlings leave the nest and reach open water quickly.
- **Seagrass grazing** — the cropping of seagrass and algae by adult green turtles; an ecologically important herbivory that shapes meadow health.
- **Salt gland** — a gland near the eye that excretes excess salt, producing the "tears" seen in nesting turtles.
- **Clutch** — the full set of eggs laid by a female in a single nesting.
- **Carapace** — the domed upper part of the shell; in turtles it is fused to the skeleton and cannot be shed.
- **Fibropapillomatosis** — a disease of sea turtles, associated with a herpesvirus, that causes tumours which can impair vision, feeding, and swimming.
- **Ontogenetic diet shift** — a change in diet across life stages, here from carnivorous juvenile to largely herbivorous adult.
