# Short Quiz — Book 034: The Green Sea Turtle

1. What behaviour is the green sea turtle most famous for in ethology?
   - A. Natal homing — returning to nest near where it hatched
   - B. Building communal nests
   - C. Cooperative hunting in packs
   - D. Year-round territorial defence

2. Which sense is central to the green turtle's long-distance navigation?
   - A. Echolocation
   - B. Electroreception
   - C. Magnetoreception
   - D. Infrared vision

3. In temperature-dependent sex determination, warmer incubation sand tends to produce:
   - A. More males
   - B. More females
   - C. Equal sex ratios
   - D. No viable eggs

4. The adult green turtle's diet is best described as:
   - A. Strictly carnivorous
   - B. Filter-feeding on plankton
   - C. Scavenging on carrion only
   - D. Largely herbivorous, grazing seagrass and algae

5. The "tears" of a nesting green turtle are actually:
   - A. Secretions of salt glands excreting excess salt
   - B. A sign of emotional distress
   - C. Fluid to keep the eggs moist
   - D. A response to predators

6. Which statement about the green turtle's shell is correct?
   - A. It can be shed and regrown
   - B. The turtle can crawl out of it
   - C. It is fused to the skeleton and cannot be left
   - D. It fully retracts the head inside

7. After laying eggs, the female green turtle:
   - A. Guards the nest until hatching
   - B. Provides no further parental care
   - C. Carries the eggs to the sea
   - D. Feeds the hatchlings

8. Hatchlings crossing the beach orient toward the sea mainly by:
   - A. The Earth's magnetic field alone
   - B. Their mother's scent trail
   - C. Light cues and beach slope
   - D. Sound of the waves only

9. A major reason green turtle populations recover slowly is:
   - A. They lay only one egg per year
   - B. They have no predators
   - C. They cannot migrate
   - D. Their slow maturation and infrequent breeding

10. Fibropapillomatosis in green turtles is:
    - A. A tumour-causing disease linked to a herpesvirus
    - B. A navigational disorder
    - C. A type of parasite in the gut
    - D. A behaviour seen during nesting

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. B
4. D
5. A
6. C
7. B
8. C
9. D
10. A
