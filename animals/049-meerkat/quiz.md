# Short Quiz — The Meerkat (*Suricata suricatta*)

1. To which mammal family does the meerkat belong?
   A. Felidae (cats)
   B. Herpestidae (mongooses)
   C. Canidae (dogs)
   D. Mustelidae (weasels)

2. In a meerkat group, most reproduction is carried out by:
   A. Every adult female equally
   B. Only subordinate males
   C. Visiting males from other groups
   D. A single dominant pair

3. Meerkat alarm calls encode information about:
   A. Only the caller's identity
   B. Predator type and urgency
   C. The time of day
   D. Food location only

4. A "sentinel" meerkat is one that:
   A. Digs the deepest burrow
   B. Leads the group to new territory
   C. Takes a raised position to watch for predators
   D. Always breeds first

5. Meerkat prey-provisioning of pups is scientifically notable as an example of:
   A. Teaching
   B. Tool use
   C. Echolocation
   D. Migration

6. When feeding very young pups, adults typically provide scorpions that are:
   A. Alive and fully intact
   B. Dead or with the sting disabled
   C. Frozen
   D. Replaced with plant food

7. Reproductive suppression in meerkats refers to:
   A. Helpers refusing to feed pups
   B. Males leaving the group
   C. Seasonal hibernation
   D. A dominant female preventing subordinates from breeding

8. "Sunning" in meerkats is best described as:
   A. A courtship display
   B. Thermoregulatory warming in the sun
   C. A territorial threat
   D. A hunting technique

9. Compared with the meerkat, the banded mongoose typically shows:
   A. More sharing of reproduction among females
   B. Stricter monopoly by one female
   C. No parental care at all
   D. Solitary living

10. Which statement about a lone meerkat is accurate?
    A. It thrives better alone than in a group
    B. It can rear pups by itself easily
    C. It faces very poor survival prospects without a group
    D. It gains nothing from group living

## Answer Key

1. B
2. D
3. B
4. C
5. A
6. B
7. D
8. B
9. A
10. C
