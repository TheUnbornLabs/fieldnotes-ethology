# Glossary — The Meerkat (*Suricata suricatta*)

- **Cooperative breeding** — a system in which individuals other than the parents help raise young.
- **Obligate cooperative breeder** — a species that depends on helpers to reproduce successfully; breeding pairs cannot reliably rear young alone.
- **Alloparental care** — care of young provided by individuals other than the genetic parents.
- **Helper** — a (usually subordinate, related) group member that assists in raising young rather than breeding itself.
- **Sentinel** — an individual that takes up a raised guard position to watch for predators while others forage.
- **Graded alarm call** — an alarm signal whose acoustic form varies continuously to encode information such as predator type and urgency.
- **Referential signalling** — communication in which a signal conveys information about a specific external object or event, such as a particular class of predator.
- **Reproductive suppression** — the prevention of breeding in subordinates by a dominant individual, through behaviour and physiological stress.
- **Eviction** — the forced expulsion of a subordinate (often a pregnant female) from the group by the dominant.
- **Infanticide** — the killing of young; in meerkats often of a subordinate's pups by the dominant female or others.
- **Teaching** — behaviour by which an experienced individual modifies its actions in the presence of a naive individual, at a cost, to help it acquire a skill.
- **Dominant pair** — the breeding female and male that monopolize most reproduction in a meerkat group.
- **Kalahari** — the arid to semi-arid region of southern Africa that is core meerkat habitat and the setting for major long-term study.
- **Sunning** — thermoregulatory behaviour in which meerkats stand and expose their bellies to the sun to warm up.
- **Mob (or gang)** — the common terms for a meerkat social group, typically three to thirty individuals.
