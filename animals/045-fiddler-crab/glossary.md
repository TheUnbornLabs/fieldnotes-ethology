# Glossary — The Fiddler Crab

- **Sexual dimorphism** — consistent differences in form between males and females of a species; in fiddler crabs, males have one giant claw while females have two small ones.
- **Cheliped** — a claw-bearing leg of a crustacean; fiddler crabs have a major (large) and minor (small) cheliped in males.
- **Major claw** — the enormously enlarged cheliped of a male fiddler crab, used for display and combat but not for feeding.
- **Minor claw** — the small cheliped that does all feeding in males (females use both small claws).
- **Claw-waving display** — the species-specific raising, circling, or bobbing of the major claw used to court females and challenge rival males.
- **Deposit feeding** — feeding by scooping sediment and sorting out microscopic organic matter (algae, bacteria, detritus) from mineral grains.
- **Pseudo-faeces** — rounded pellets of cleaned, rejected sediment left after feeding; not true faeces.
- **Tidal rhythm** — a behavioural cycle timed to the tides (roughly 12.4-hour period), such as emerging at low tide and plugging the burrow at high tide.
- **Circadian clock** — an internal timer of roughly 24 hours that keeps daily rhythms running even without external cues.
- **Endogenous clock** — any internally generated biological timer that continues to cycle in constant conditions; fiddler crabs have both circadian and tidal endogenous clocks.
- **Stalked compound eyes** — eyes raised on movable stalks and built of many units, giving fiddler crabs near-panoramic, horizon-tuned vision.
- **Polarised-light sensitivity** — the ability to detect the plane of polarisation of light, aiding orientation and object detection against sky and wet mud.
- **Burrow** — the excavated shelter each crab defends, used as refuge from predators, heat, and tide, and as a courtship site.
- **Bioturbation** — the reworking and aeration of sediment by burrowing animals, an ecosystem-engineering effect of fiddler crab digging.
- **Path integration** — continuously tracking direction and distance from a start point (the burrow) to compute a straight route home.
- **Leptochelous claw** — the slender, differently shaped claw a male often regrows after losing his original major claw.
