## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Fiddler crabs are in family **Ocypodidae** (Decapoda: Brachyura); close relatives of ghost crabs (*Ocypode*) — confirmed.
- **The 2016 revision (Shih et al.) raised the former subgenera of *Uca* to eleven full genera**, including *Leptuca*, *Austruca*, *Minuca*, *Gelasimus*, *Tubuca*, *Paraleptuca* — confirmed (Journal of Crustacean Biology 2019 "A fresh look at the biodiversity lexicon for fiddler crabs"; Wikipedia/Ocypodidae). About 100 species total.
- Deposit-feeding, intertidal (mudflat/mangrove/salt-marsh) habit and the enlarged male major cheliped used for signalling and combat — consistent with standard references.
- Species are largely group/genus-level for IUCN purposes; most fiddler crabs are not of conservation concern but depend on coastal wetlands. (Genus group — no single IUCN status applies.)
