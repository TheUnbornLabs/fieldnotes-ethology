# Short Quiz — The Fiddler Crab

1. In a male fiddler crab, which claw does the feeding?
   A. The major (large) claw
   B. The minor (small) claw
   C. Both claws equally
   D. Neither; it filters water

2. The rounded pellets left on a fiddler crab feeding flat are mostly:
   A. Eggs
   B. True faeces
   C. Pseudo-faeces (cleaned, rejected sediment)
   D. Larvae

3. Fiddler crab eyes are notable for being:
   A. Simple single-lens eyes
   B. Stalked compound eyes with near-panoramic, horizon-tuned vision
   C. Blind, relying on smell
   D. Sensitive only to red light

4. The main function of the male's giant major claw is:
   A. Feeding
   B. Digging burrows
   C. Courtship display and male-male contests
   D. Breathing

5. Fiddler crabs plug their burrows at high tide because they:
   A. Are startled by light
   B. Anticipate the incoming tide using internal clocks
   C. Are hibernating
   D. Store food there

6. A fiddler crab that loses its major claw will typically:
   A. Die within a day
   B. Never move again
   C. Regenerate a slighter "leptochelous" claw over moults
   D. Grow a second body

7. Female fiddler crabs often choose mates based on all of the following EXCEPT:
   A. Wave rate and vigour
   B. Claw size
   C. Burrow quality
   D. The male's true faeces output

8. Fiddler crabs are:
   A. Insects
   B. Crustaceans
   C. Molluscs
   D. Arachnids

9. Which relative provides a good contrast, sharing stalked eyes but lacking the waving display?
   A. The ghost crab
   B. The honeybee
   C. The octopus
   D. The sea turtle

10. Fiddler crab burrowing benefits coastal wetlands mainly through:
    A. Filtering seawater of salt
    B. Bioturbation — aerating and turning over sediment
    C. Pollinating marsh plants
    D. Producing fresh water

## Answer Key

1: B; 2: C; 3: B; 4: C; 5: B; 6: C; 7: D; 8: B; 9: A; 10: B
