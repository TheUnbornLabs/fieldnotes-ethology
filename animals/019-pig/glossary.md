# Glossary — The Pig (*Sus domesticus*)

- **Rooting** — the pig's core foraging behavior of pushing and levering the snout through soil to find and expose buried food; strongly motivated even in fully fed pigs.
- **Wild boar (*Sus scrofa*)** — the wild Eurasian ancestor of the domestic pig, whose behavioral repertoire the domestic pig largely retains.
- **Farrowing** — the act of a sow giving birth to a litter of piglets.
- **Nest-building** — strongly motivated pre-farrowing behavior in which a sow roots and arranges material into a nest before giving birth; still expressed despite domestication.
- **Wallowing** — rolling and lying in mud or water, primarily to lose heat and protect the skin, because pigs have few functional sweat glands.
- **Thermoregulation** — the maintenance of body temperature; in pigs achieved largely through wallowing and behavior rather than sweating.
- **Mirror food-finding task** — an experiment (Broom and colleagues) showing pigs can use reflected information to locate hidden food; a test of information-use, not of mirror self-recognition.
- **Mirror self-recognition (mark test)** — a separate, stronger test of self-directed response to a mirror; the pig mirror-and-food task does not by itself demonstrate this.
- **Operant conditioning** — learning in which an animal associates its own actions with consequences; the basis of lever, button, and joystick tasks used with pigs.
- **Contact call** — a vocalization used to maintain social cohesion; in pigs these carry individually recognizable signatures used by sows and piglets.
- **Dominance hierarchy** — a ranked social order that, once established through displays and recognition of individuals, reduces repeated conflict over resources.
- **Enrichment** — additions to an environment (rooting substrate, space, nest material, social stability) that allow natural behaviors and improve welfare.
- **Tactical use of information** — adjusting behavior according to what competitors know or can see; studied in pig foraging competition (Held and colleagues).
- **Sounder** — a matrilineal social group of sows and their young, the natural social unit in wild boar and pigs.
- **Teat order** — the stable assignment of piglets to particular teats, which reduces conflict during nursing.
- **Gestation (sow) stall** — a narrow crate confining a pregnant sow so she cannot turn around; a central welfare concern in intensive farming.
