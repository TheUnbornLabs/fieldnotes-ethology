# Short Quiz — The Pig (*Sus domesticus*)

1. What is the pig's core foraging behavior?
   A. Grazing on grass
   B. Rooting with the snout
   C. Ambush hunting
   D. Filter feeding

2. Why do pigs wallow in mud?
   A. Because they prefer dirt
   B. To attract mates
   C. To lose heat, since they have few functional sweat glands
   D. To digest food

3. The Broom mirror experiment showed that pigs can:
   A. Recognize themselves in a mirror (pass the mark test)
   B. Use reflected information to locate hidden food
   C. Communicate through mirrors
   D. Count reflected objects

4. Which task, associated with Curtis, Croney, and colleagues, demonstrated pig behavioral flexibility?
   A. Solving mazes underwater
   B. Using a joystick to move an on-screen cursor to targets
   C. Opening locked cages
   D. Imitating human speech

5. The wild ancestor of the domestic pig is the:
   A. Warthog
   B. Peccary
   C. Wild boar (*Sus scrofa*)
   D. Babirusa

6. Pre-farrowing nest-building in sows is:
   A. A learned trick taught by farmers
   B. A strongly motivated behavior still expressed despite domestication
   C. Unique to feral pigs
   D. Only seen in wild boar

7. "Teacup pigs" are best described as:
   A. A distinct tiny breed that stays small
   B. A marketing label for young miniature pigs that grow large
   C. A cross between pigs and dogs
   D. Wild boar piglets

8. Pig dominance hierarchies mainly function to:
   A. Increase constant fighting
   B. Reduce repeated conflict once rank is established
   C. Select mates only
   D. Organize migration

9. Which sense is most central to how a pig experiences its world?
   A. Vision
   B. Smell and touch, organized around the snout
   C. Echolocation
   D. Magnetoreception

10. Which is a recognized welfare concern in intensive pig farming?
    A. Excessive rooting substrate
    B. Overly large social groups of familiar pigs
    C. Gestation stalls and barren, rooting-free housing
    D. Too much nest material

## Answer Key

1-B, 2-C, 3-B, 4-B, 5-C, 6-B, 7-B, 8-B, 9-B, 10-C
