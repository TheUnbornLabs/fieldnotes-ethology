# Glossary — The Nile Crocodile (*Crocodylus niloticus*)

**Ambush predator** — a hunter that waits in concealment and attacks prey that comes within reach, rather than pursuing it; the Nile crocodile's core strategy.

**Death roll** — the spinning of the whole body along its long axis to dismember large prey or to subdue and drown struggling prey.

**Ectotherm** — an animal that relies on external heat sources rather than internal metabolism to regulate body temperature.

**Thermoregulation** — the behavioral control of body temperature, achieved in crocodiles by basking, seeking shade, entering water, and gaping.

**Gaping** — holding the mouth wide open while basking, thought to help release heat rather than being a threat display.

**Integumentary sensory organ** — a dome pressure receptor: a tiny pigmented pit in the skin, concentrated around the jaws, that detects pressure and surface ripples in the water.

**Temperature-dependent sex determination** — a system in which the incubation temperature of the eggs, not chromosomes, determines the sex of the offspring.

**Crèche** — a nursery group of hatchlings guarded by the mother in the weeks after hatching.

**Archosaur** — the reptile lineage that includes crocodilians, dinosaurs, and birds; crocodiles are more closely related to birds than to lizards or snakes.

**Bellow** — a deep, low-frequency vocalisation used by adults in social and reproductive contexts.

**Nest guarding** — the female's prolonged defence of the egg nest through the months of incubation.

**Tapetum lucidum** — a reflective layer behind the retina that improves night vision and produces the crocodile's eyeshine.

**Osteoderm** — a bony plate embedded in the skin forming the crocodile's protective dermal armour.

**Polygyny** — a mating system in which a dominant male mates with several females.

**Ambush queue** — the roughly size-ordered sequence in which crocodiles take turns feeding at a carcass under a dominance hierarchy.
