# Short Quiz — The Nile Crocodile (*Crocodylus niloticus*)

1. The Nile crocodile's primary hunting strategy is best described as:
   A. Long-distance pursuit
   B. Sit-and-wait ambush
   C. Cooperative pack hunting on land
   D. Filter feeding

2. The "death roll" is used mainly to:
   A. Cool the body down
   B. Attract a mate
   C. Dismember large prey and subdue struggling animals
   D. Dig a nest

3. Dome pressure receptors (integumentary sensory organs) allow a crocodile to:
   A. Detect ripples and pressure changes on the water surface
   B. See colour underwater
   C. Generate body heat
   D. Produce venom

4. Gaping — holding the mouth open while basking — is thought to:
   A. Always signal an imminent attack
   B. Help release heat as part of thermoregulation
   C. Trap insects
   D. Clean the teeth

5. In the Nile crocodile, the sex of offspring is determined by:
   A. Sex chromosomes at fertilisation
   B. The mother's diet
   C. The incubation temperature of the eggs
   D. The size of the clutch

6. Crocodilians are most closely related to:
   A. Lizards
   B. Snakes
   C. Turtles
   D. Birds

7. When hatchlings are ready to emerge, the mother is prompted to open the nest by:
   A. Rainfall alone
   B. Rising river levels
   C. The smell of the eggs
   D. The chirping calls of the young inside the eggs

8. At a shared carcass, access among crocodiles is chiefly ordered by:
   A. Age only
   B. Body size and dominance
   C. Sex only
   D. Random chance

9. A key visible difference between a Nile crocodile and an American alligator is that:
   A. Only alligators have teeth
   B. The crocodile's lower fourth tooth remains visible when the mouth is closed
   C. Crocodiles cannot swim
   D. Alligators are larger in every case

10. On the IUCN Red List, the Nile crocodile as a species is currently assessed as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Endangered
   C. Extinct in the Wild
   D. Not Evaluated

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. A
4. B
5. C
6. D
7. D
8. B
9. B
10. A
