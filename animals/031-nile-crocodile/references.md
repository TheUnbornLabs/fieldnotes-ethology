# References / Further Reading — The Nile Crocodile (*Crocodylus niloticus*)

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those that should be checked against a database before final publication. Following the collection's citation policy, no authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, or page numbers have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Gordon Grigg and David Kirshner, *Biology and Evolution of Crocodylians* — a comprehensive scholarly reference on crocodilian anatomy, physiology, behavior, and evolution, including thermoregulation, sensory biology, and parental care.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Crocodylus niloticus* (global status: Least Concern). Consult the current Red List entry for range, population trends, threats, and notes on regional variation.
- Crocodilian-behavior research associated with Adam Britton — a recognised source of science-based information on crocodilian biology, behavior, vocal communication, and conservation; a useful starting point for verifying species-specific behavioural claims.
- IUCN Crocodile Specialist Group — a recognised body producing science-based assessments and status reports on crocodilian species and their management.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general ethological concepts such as ambush predation, dominance hierarchies, communication, parental investment, and thermoregulation.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- **Scientific name / taxonomy:** *Crocodylus niloticus*, family Crocodylidae; crocodilians are archosaurs (closer to birds than to lizards/snakes) — confirmed via IUCN Red List and standard references.
- **IUCN Red List status:** **Least Concern** globally (assessed 2017), with acknowledged regional variation — book text confirmed correct. Sources: IUCN Red List / IUCN-SSC Crocodile Specialist Group; San Diego Zoo (ielc.libguides.com) fact sheet.
- Bite-force, sensory-organ, TSD, parental-communication, and human-conflict specifics remain flagged above; not individually re-verified here.
