# Glossary — The Wolf (*Canis lupus*)

**Pack** — a wolf social group, in the wild usually a family consisting of a breeding pair and their offspring.
**Dispersal** — the departure of a maturing wolf from its natal pack to seek a mate and territory of its own, often over long distances.
**Breeding pair** — the mated male and female whose reproduction and leadership organise a typical wolf pack.
**Allo-parenting** — care of pups by pack members other than the mother, especially the breeding male and older offspring.
**Scent-marking** — deposition of urine and scats at strategic sites to communicate identity, reproductive state, and territory.
**Rendezvous site** — an above-ground gathering area where pups wait and are cared for after leaving the den while adults hunt.
**Crepuscular** — most active around dawn and dusk.
**Dominance hierarchy** — a ranking of individuals; in wild wolf families this largely reflects the natural parent–offspring structure rather than combat among unrelated adults.
**Cursorial predator** — a hunter adapted for sustained pursuit and endurance running rather than ambush.
**Home range** — the total area a wolf pack uses and defends; a wolf territory.
**Howl chorus** — a group howl by several pack members that reinforces contact and territorial signalling.
**Trophic cascade** — an ecological chain of effects in which a predator influences prey, which in turn affects vegetation and other species.
**Pup** — a young wolf in its first year of life.
**Yearling** — a wolf roughly one year old, typically a non-breeding offspring that helps rear the current litter.
**Obligate carnivore** — an animal whose diet must consist primarily of animal prey.
**Tapetum lucidum** — a reflective layer behind the retina that improves low-light vision in wolves and other animals.
