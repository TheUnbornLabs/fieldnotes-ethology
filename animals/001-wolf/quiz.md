# Short Quiz — The Wolf (*Canis lupus*)

1. A typical wild wolf pack is best described as:
   A. A group of unrelated adults competing for rank
   B. A family of a breeding pair and their offspring
   C. A temporary hunting alliance that disbands after each kill
   D. A colony of many breeding pairs

2. The rigid "alpha wolf" model originally came largely from:
   A. Long-term study of wild packs
   B. Studies of captive, unrelated wolves in enclosures
   C. Observations of domestic dogs
   D. Fossil evidence

3. Which biologist publicly reconsidered the "alpha" terminology after studying wild wolves?
   A. Rudolph Schenkel
   B. L. David Mech
   C. Konrad Lorenz
   D. Charles Darwin

4. Wolves are best classified by activity pattern as:
   A. Strictly nocturnal
   B. Strictly diurnal
   C. Crepuscular with flexibility
   D. Active only in winter

5. The primary function of howling is to:
   A. React to the moon
   B. Reassemble the pack, keep contact, and advertise territory
   C. Frighten prey before a hunt
   D. Cool the body

6. Allo-parenting in wolves refers to:
   A. Pups raising younger pups
   B. Care of pups by pack members other than the mother
   C. Adoption of pups from other species
   D. The mother raising pups entirely alone

7. A "rendezvous site" is:
   A. The den where pups are born
   B. An above-ground area where pups wait while adults hunt
   C. A boundary scent post
   D. A winter shelter

8. The wolf's two most important senses for hunting and navigation are:
   A. Vision and touch
   B. Smell and hearing
   C. Taste and magnetoreception
   D. Echolocation and vision

9. Compared with wolves, domestic dogs typically:
   A. Hunt large prey more cooperatively
   B. Avoid humans more strongly
   C. Read human social cues such as pointing more readily
   D. Have larger territories

10. The scientifically honest description of the Yellowstone trophic cascade is that wolves:
   A. Single-handedly and simply fixed the entire ecosystem
   B. Had no measurable effect
   C. Genuinely influenced the ecosystem, though the magnitude is complex and debated
   D. Only affected other predators

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. B
4. C
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. C
