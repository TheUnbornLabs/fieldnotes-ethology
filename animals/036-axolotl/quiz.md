# Short Quiz — The Axolotl (*Ambystoma mexicanum*)

1. To which animal group does the axolotl belong?
   A. Fish
   B. Reptile
   C. Amphibian (salamander)
   D. Fish-amphibian hybrid

2. What is the term for retaining larval features while reaching sexual maturity?
   A. Metamorphosis
   B. Neoteny (paedomorphosis)
   C. Hibernation
   D. Estivation

3. Where is the wild axolotl found today?
   A. Throughout Mexico's rivers
   B. The remnant canals and wetlands of Xochimilco near Mexico City
   C. The Amazon basin
   D. Lakes across the southern United States

4. Which sense is NOT a major part of the axolotl's perception?
   A. Lateral line
   B. Electroreception
   C. Sharp, detailed vision
   D. Chemical detection

5. How does the axolotl capture prey?
   A. Active long-distance pursuit
   B. Filter feeding
   C. Sit-and-wait suction feeding
   D. Cooperative pack hunting

6. What does the male deposit during courtship for the female to take up?
   A. A nest of eggs
   B. A spermatophore
   C. A pheromone gland
   D. A gill filament

7. The familiar pink or white axolotl color is:
   A. The natural wild coloration
   B. A leucistic/albino captive morph
   C. A sign of illness in all cases
   D. Caused by cold water

8. What is the axolotl's IUCN Red List status?
   A. Least Concern
   B. Near Threatened
   C. Vulnerable
   D. Critically Endangered

9. Which close relative normally completes metamorphosis into a land-living adult?
   A. The tiger salamander (*Ambystoma tigrinum*)
   B. The common frog
   C. The mudpuppy
   D. The newt-eel

10. Which statement about axolotl regeneration is accurate?
    A. It makes the animal immortal
    B. It regrows limbs, tail, and some nerve and organ tissue, but the animal still ages and dies
    C. It works only on the tail
    D. It has never been scientifically observed

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. B
4. C
5. C
6. B
7. B
8. D
9. A
10. B
