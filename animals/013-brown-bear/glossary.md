# Glossary — Book 013: The Brown Bear

- **Solitary:** Living and foraging mostly alone rather than in permanent groups, as adult brown bears do.
- **Home range:** The area an animal uses for its normal activities; large in brown bears and not exclusively defended.
- **Home range overlap:** The condition in which neighbouring bears' ranges cover the same ground, because brown bears do not defend exclusive territories.
- **Hyperphagia:** The late-summer and autumn period of intense, near-continuous feeding by which bears build the fat reserves needed for winter.
- **Torpor:** The metabolic dormancy of winter denning, marked by reduced metabolism but only moderate temperature drop, from which bears can rouse.
- **Denning:** Spending winter in a sheltered den in a state of dormancy, during which females give birth.
- **Delayed implantation (embryonic diapause):** A reproductive strategy in which the fertilized embryo pauses development and implants only later, its continuation depending on the mother's condition.
- **Omnivory:** A diet including both plant and animal foods, allowing flexible tracking of seasonal resources.
- **Dominance queue:** The informal, situational ordering of access to a rich food source (such as a salmon run) by size, age, and experience.
- **Infanticide (sexually selected):** The killing of unrelated cubs by adult males, which can bring the mother into breeding condition sooner.
- **Food-conditioning:** The learned association between humans or settlements and food, which makes bears bolder and prone to conflict.
- **Nutrient transport:** The ecological movement of nutrients — as when bears carry salmon carcasses inland, fertilizing forests with marine-derived nutrients.
- **Olfaction:** The sense of smell; in brown bears, exceptionally acute and central to foraging, mating, and navigation.
- **Aggregation:** A temporary gathering of otherwise solitary bears at a concentrated food source.
