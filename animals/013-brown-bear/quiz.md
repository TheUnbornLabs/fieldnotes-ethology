# Quiz — Book 013: The Brown Bear

1. The brown bear's social system is best described as:
   A. Territorial and defended
   B. Solitary with overlapping, non-defended home ranges
   C. Permanent pack living
   D. Colonial nesting

2. Winter denning in brown bears is best described as:
   A. True deep hibernation with no possibility of waking
   B. Continuous activity through winter
   C. Torpor, a dormancy from which bears can rouse
   D. A brief nap of a few days

3. Hyperphagia refers to:
   A. A disease of captive bears
   B. Intense pre-winter feeding to build fat reserves
   C. A courtship display
   D. A form of scent marking

4. Delayed implantation means that:
   A. Cubs are born in summer
   B. The embryo pauses development and implants later, depending on the mother's condition
   C. Bears cannot reproduce in the wild
   D. Females mate only once in a lifetime

5. At a rich salmon run, access among bears is typically organized by:
   A. Random chance
   B. A defended territory system
   C. An informal dominance queue based on size, age, and experience
   D. Cooperative sharing among all bears

6. Sexually selected infanticide in brown bears refers to:
   A. Mothers abandoning weak cubs
   B. Adult males killing unrelated cubs to breed with the mother sooner
   C. Cubs competing lethally with siblings
   D. Predation by wolves

7. The brown bear's most acute sense is:
   A. Vision
   B. Hearing
   C. Smell
   D. Electroreception

8. A brown bear standing on its hind legs most often indicates:
   A. An imminent attack
   B. Gathering scent and information
   C. A mating display
   D. Illness

9. "Food-conditioning" describes:
   A. Bears learning to associate humans and garbage with food
   B. A special denning diet
   C. Training bears in captivity
   D. Seasonal changes in metabolism

10. Compared with the American black bear, the brown bear is:
    A. Smaller and a better climber
    B. Larger, a poor climber, and more likely to stand and defend when surprised
    C. Strictly nocturnal and arboreal
    D. Exclusively a fish-eater

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. A
10. B
