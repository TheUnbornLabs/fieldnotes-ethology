## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Herrero, Stephen. *Bear Attacks: Their Causes and Avoidance.* A standard reference on brown/grizzly bear behavior, defensive vs. predatory attacks, and human safety.
- IUCN Red List — *Ursus arctos* species assessment (global status Least Concern, with range and population detail). Check the current assessment page for up-to-date figures.
- Interagency Grizzly Bear Study Team (IGBST) — long-running U.S. research program on grizzly bear population dynamics and ecology. Name generally; verify specific reports, dates, and figures before citing.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan) — *Ursus arctos* species profile.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live IUCN Red List and Animal Diversity Web:

- Scientific name *Ursus arctos*, order Carnivora, family Ursidae — confirmed. Source: IUCN Red List; Animal Diversity Web.
- IUCN Red List status: **Least Concern** (assessed 2022; large secure northern populations, but many of ~44 separate populations are small, isolated, and threatened, especially at the southern range fringe) — confirmed current. Source: IUCN Red List, *Ursus arctos* assessment (iucnredlist.org).
- Most widely distributed bear species, spanning Eurasia and western North America, from sea level to ~5,000 m — confirmed. Source: IUCN Red List.

Delayed-implantation, infanticide, salmon-nutrient-transport, and denning-physiology specifics remain to be traced to their primary sources as noted above.
