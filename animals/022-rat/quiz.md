# Short Quiz — The Brown Rat (*Rattus norvegicus*)

1. The brown rat's scientific name is:
   A. *Mus musculus*
   B. *Rattus norvegicus*
   C. *Rattus rattus*
   D. *Sus scrofa*

2. "Bait shyness" in rats is best explained by:
   A. Poor eyesight
   B. Neophobia combined with learned taste aversion
   C. An inability to smell food
   D. Lack of memory

3. Whisking refers to a rat:
   A. Sweeping its vibrissae to actively touch its surroundings
   B. Wagging its tail
   C. Grinding its teeth
   D. Marking territory with urine

4. Rats emit roughly 50 kHz ultrasonic calls in situations of:
   A. Fear and defeat
   B. Play, reward anticipation, and tickling
   C. Extreme cold only
   D. Complete silence

5. In the Bartal, Decety, and Mason experiments, a free rat would:
   A. Ignore a trapped cage-mate
   B. Attack a trapped cage-mate
   C. Open a restrainer to free a trapped cage-mate and share food
   D. Flee the arena

6. Hippocampal "place cells," central to rat spatial memory, were discovered by:
   A. John Garcia
   B. Jaak Panksepp
   C. John O'Keefe
   D. Michael Meaney

7. Brown rats are socially best described as:
   A. Strictly solitary vermin
   B. Colonial, living in family groups with dominance relationships
   C. Living only in mated pairs
   D. Forming leaderless migrating swarms

8. The claim that rats "directly" caused the Black Death through filth is:
   A. Fully confirmed and simple
   B. An oversimplification; transmission involved fleas and the rat's role is even debated
   C. Proof rats cannot carry disease
   D. Unrelated to *Yersinia pestis*

9. Compared with the house mouse, the brown rat is generally:
   A. Smaller and more neophilic
   B. Larger, more neophobic, and a committed burrower
   C. Unable to use ultrasonic calls
   D. Not a laboratory model

10. Rat pups are born:
    A. Fully furred and independent
    B. Able to forage immediately
    C. Altricial — blind, hairless, and helpless
    D. Already weaned

## Answer Key

1-B, 2-B, 3-A, 4-B, 5-C, 6-C, 7-B, 8-B, 9-B, 10-C
