# Glossary — The Brown Rat (*Rattus norvegicus*)

- **Commensal** — an animal that lives in close association with humans, exploiting their food and shelter; the brown rat is a classic commensal.
- **Colony** — the social unit of brown rats, made up of overlapping family groups sharing a burrow system and recognizing one another as individuals.
- **Dominance relationship** — a ranked social arrangement, especially among males, that governs priority of access to resources and mates and reduces continual conflict once established.
- **Neophobia** — wariness toward novel objects and foods, a defensive adaptation in the brown rat that makes it cautious of new baits and traps.
- **Bait shyness / learned taste aversion** — the strong, often one-trial learned avoidance of a food that previously made the animal ill; a key reason acute poisons fail against rat colonies.
- **Vibrissae** — the long facial whiskers that rats use as a primary touch organ.
- **Whisking** — the rapid back-and-forth sweeping of the vibrissae by which rats actively palpate their surroundings, especially in the dark.
- **Ultrasonic vocalization (USV)** — a call above the range of human hearing; rats emit roughly 22 kHz calls in negative states and 50 kHz calls in positive ones.
- **Prosocial behavior** — behavior that benefits another individual, such as freeing a trapped cage-mate or sharing food, documented experimentally in rats.
- **Metacognition** — sensitivity to one's own knowledge or uncertainty; some rat studies suggest rats decline difficult tests when unsure, though the interpretation is debated.
- **Spatial memory** — the capacity to learn and remember the layout of an environment, supported in rats by hippocampal place cells and cognitive maps.
- **Play (rough-and-tumble)** — vigorous juvenile chasing and play-fighting, accompanied by 50 kHz calls and thought to develop social and motor skills.
- **Altricial** — born in a helpless, undeveloped state (blind, hairless, dependent), as rat pups are.
- **Model organism** — a species widely used in research to understand biology generally; the brown rat is a premier mammalian model in neuroscience and medicine.
- **Vomeronasal organ** — an accessory olfactory structure that detects pheromones important in rat social and reproductive behavior.
