- Jaak Panksepp — *Affective Neuroscience: The Foundations of Human and Animal Emotions* is a well-established book that is safe to name by title and author. - Inbal Ben-Ami Bartal, Jean Decety, and Peggy Mason — broadly associated with the rat prosocial-helping studies in which a free rat releases a trapped cage-mate from a restrainer and shares food, discussed in terms of empathy. - John O'Keefe — associated with the discovery of hippocampal "place cells" and the cognitive-map theory of spatial navigation, work later recognized with a Nobel Prize (shared with May-Britt and Edvard Moser). Safe to name in general terms; verify specifics before final publication.
- John Garcia — associated with foundational work on conditioned (learned) taste aversion, the "Garcia effect," relevant to rat bait shyness. Safe to name in general terms; verify specific studies before final publication.
- Michael Meaney — associated with research on natural variation in rat maternal licking and grooming and its lasting effects on offspring stress responses. Safe to name the general research program; verify specific articles before final publication.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for the brown rat (*Rattus norvegicus*), listed as Least Concern, useful for range, habitat, and conservation status.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profiles for *Rattus norvegicus* and *Mus musculus*, useful for taxonomy, natural history, and general behavior.
