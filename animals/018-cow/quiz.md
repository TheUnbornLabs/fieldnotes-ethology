# Quiz — Book 018: Cattle (*Bos taurus*)

1. The wild ancestor of domestic cattle is the:
   A. Bison
   B. Aurochs
   C. Water buffalo
   D. Yak

2. Humped, heat-adapted cattle of South Asian origin are called:
   A. Taurine cattle
   B. Indicine (zebu) cattle
   C. Aurochs
   D. Highland cattle

3. Bulls charge a matador's cape mainly because of:
   A. The red color
   B. The movement of the cape
   C. The smell of the cloth
   D. The sound it makes

4. A cow "chewing the cud" is engaged in:
   A. Grazing new grass
   B. Rumination, a second stage of digestion
   C. A sign of illness
   D. Threat behavior

5. The flight zone is best described as:
   A. A cow's preferred grazing area
   B. The distance a herd migrates
   C. An animal's personal space whose edge triggers movement away
   D. The area where calves are hidden

6. Moving behind an animal's point of balance (at the shoulder) tends to:
   A. Stop the animal
   B. Turn it back
   C. Drive it forward
   D. Have no effect

7. Cattle color vision is:
   A. Full-color like humans
   B. Dichromatic, poor at distinguishing red from green
   C. Completely colorblind
   D. Best in the red range

8. The "eureka" findings in cattle research suggest that heifers:
   A. Feel nothing while learning
   B. Show excitement at solving a problem, not just at the reward
   C. Cannot learn tasks
   D. Only learn by trial and error

9. In the early "hider" phase, a cow typically:
   A. Keeps the calf constantly at her side
   B. Abandons the calf entirely
   C. Hides the calf and grazes nearby, returning to nurse
   D. Leaves the calf with the bull

10. Which statement is accurate about cattle safety?
    A. All cattle are always safe to approach
    B. Cows with calves and bulls can be dangerous
    C. Only sick cattle are ever dangerous
    D. Cattle cannot injure a person

## Answer Key

1-B, 2-B, 3-B, 4-B, 5-C, 6-C, 7-B, 8-B, 9-C, 10-B
