# Quiz — Book 028: The Octopus

1. To which animal group does the octopus belong?
   A. Fish
   B. Crustaceans
   C. Molluscs (cephalopods)
   D. Marine mammals

2. Where are most of an octopus's neurons located?
   A. In the arms
   B. In the central brain
   C. In the eyes
   D. In the funnel

3. With respect to color vision, octopuses are:
   A. Able to see more colors than humans
   B. Almost certainly colorblind
   C. Sensitive only to red light
   D. Completely blind

4. Which cells change color to produce camouflage patterns?
   A. Chromatophores
   B. Leucophores only
   C. Neurons
   D. Radula cells

5. The common octopus's reproductive strategy is best described as:
   A. Repeated breeding over many years
   B. Live birth of a few large young
   C. Cooperative group breeding
   D. Semelparous — a single terminal brood

6. What is the "coconut-carrying" octopus an example of?
   A. Social cooperation
   B. Tool-like behavior
   C. Color vision
   D. Parental care

7. How does an octopus achieve rapid escape from a predator?
   A. Running on its arms across land
   B. Inflating with air
   C. Jet propulsion by expelling water through the funnel
   D. Sprouting spines

8. Which is a key behavioral difference between octopuses and cuttlefish?
   A. Only octopuses have chromatophores
   B. Cuttlefish use their skin extensively for visual signaling, while octopuses use it mainly for camouflage
   C. Cuttlefish cannot change color
   D. Octopuses live in large cooperative herds

9. What happens to a female common octopus after her eggs hatch?
   A. She raises the hatchlings for months
   B. She mates again immediately
   C. She dies, having brooded the clutch and stopped feeding
   D. She joins a group of other females

10. Which statement about the octopus arms is correct?
    A. They move only on direct, moment-to-moment command from the brain
    B. They contain no neurons
    C. They cannot taste or touch
    D. They have substantial semi-autonomous local control

## Answer Key

1. C
2. A
3. B
4. A
5. D
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. C
10. D
