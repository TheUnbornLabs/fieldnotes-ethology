# Book 048 — The Red Kangaroo: Glossary

- **Marsupial** — a mammal that gives birth to a very undeveloped young which typically completes its growth attached to a teat, usually within a pouch.
- **Macropod** — a member of the family Macropodidae ("big-footed" marsupials): the kangaroos, wallaroos, and wallabies.
- **Saltation (hopping)** — locomotion by bounding leaps; the red kangaroo's characteristic two-legged hopping gait.
- **Elastic energy storage** — the storing of energy in stretched tendons during landing and its return on take-off, making hopping more energy-efficient at speed.
- **Pentapedal walk** — the slow five-limbed gait in which the muscular tail acts as a fifth limb together with the forelimbs and hind legs.
- **Mob** — the loose aggregation in which red kangaroos live.
- **Fission-fusion** — a social pattern in which group size and membership change constantly as individuals split apart and merge.
- **Sexual dimorphism** — consistent differences in size or form between the sexes; in red kangaroos, males are much larger and more muscular than females.
- **Embryonic diapause** — a pause in the development of a fertilised embryo until conditions (such as an available pouch) allow it to resume.
- **Joey** — a young kangaroo, especially the newborn and pouch-dwelling stages.
- **Behavioral thermoregulation** — controlling body temperature through behaviour, such as resting in shade, timing activity to cool hours, and licking the forearms to cool.
- **Crepuscular** — active mainly at twilight, around dusk and dawn.
- **Grazer** — an animal that feeds mainly on grasses and low herbage.
- **Nomadic** — ranging widely without a fixed defended territory, here tracking rainfall and green feed.
- **Diprotodont** — a marsupial with two prominent forward-pointing lower incisors, as in kangaroos.
