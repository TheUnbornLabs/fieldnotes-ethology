# Book 048 — The Red Kangaroo: Short Quiz

1. The red kangaroo is best described as:
   A. The largest living rodent
   B. The largest living marsupial
   C. A placental grazer
   D. A member of the deer family

2. Hopping becomes more energy-efficient at higher speeds mainly because:
   A. The kangaroo holds its breath
   B. The tail acts as a propeller
   C. The animal loses weight as it runs
   D. Elastic energy is stored and returned in the leg tendons

3. In the slow "pentapedal" walk, the tail functions as:
   A. A fifth limb that bears weight and helps propel the body
   B. A rudder only
   C. A purely decorative structure
   D. A store of fat

4. Red kangaroo mobs are characterised by:
   A. A strict, stable dominance hierarchy
   B. Loose, fission-fusion membership with no rigid hierarchy
   C. Permanent membership and defended group territories
   D. Lifelong monogamous pairs

5. A newborn red kangaroo is:
   A. A fully formed miniature adult
   B. Born already able to hop
   C. A tiny, embryo-like joey that crawls unaided to the pouch
   D. Raised entirely by the father

6. Embryonic diapause allows a female to:
   A. Grow two joeys in one pouch fused together
   B. Pause a fertilised embryo's development until conditions allow it to resume
   C. Change the sex of her young
   D. Skip lactation entirely

7. Male "boxing" contests in red kangaroos are mainly about:
   A. Defending a fixed territory year-round
   B. Play with no function
   C. Access to receptive females
   D. Establishing feeding order at water points

8. Which is a genuine part of the red kangaroo's behavioural thermoregulation?
   A. Sweating heavily across the whole body
   B. Burrowing deep underground each day
   C. Migrating to the coast every summer
   D. Licking the forearms to cool, resting in shade, and being active in cool hours

9. Red kangaroos are most active:
   A. In the full midday heat
   B. Only during rainstorms
   C. Around dusk, through the night, and near dawn (crepuscular/nocturnal)
   D. Exactly at noon

10. Compared with a similar-sized placental grazer such as an antelope, the red kangaroo:
    A. Needs far more water and drinks constantly
    B. Runs on four legs with a galloping gait
    C. Gives birth to a precocial calf that runs within hours
    D. Uses a hopping gait and a staggered, pausable reproductive strategy

## Answer Key

1. B
2. D
3. A
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. C
8. D
9. C
10. D
