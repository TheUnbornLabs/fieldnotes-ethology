# References / Further Reading — The Domestic Cat

## Well-established sources

- Bradshaw, John. *Cat Sense: How the New Feline Science Can Make You a Better Friend to Your Pet.* A widely read, science-based account of cat behavior and domestication.
- Turner, Dennis C., and Patrick Bateson (eds.). *The Domestic Cat: The Biology of its Behaviour.* Cambridge University Press. The standard scholarly reference on feline behavior.
- IUCN Red List — *Felis lybica* (African wildcat) species assessment (Least Concern). The authoritative conservation status source for the cat's wild ancestor.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live IUCN Red List:

- The domestic cat, *Felis catus* (family Felidae, genus *Felis*), is a **domesticated animal and is not IUCN-assessed** — confirmed.
- Its wild ancestor, the African wildcat, currently treated as *Felis lybica* (also written *Felis silvestris lybica*), is assessed **Least Concern** on the IUCN Red List — confirmed current. Source: IUCN Red List, African wildcat assessment (iucnredlist.org). The book's framing of the ancestry and the hybridization threat to wildcats is consistent with the IUCN account.

Domestication timing/geography, cat–wildlife impact, human-directed communication, and TNR-effectiveness specifics remain to be traced to their primary sources as noted above.
