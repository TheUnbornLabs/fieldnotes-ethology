# Quiz — The Domestic Cat

1. The domestic cat is descended from which wild ancestor?
   A. The European wildcat only
   B. The African wildcat (*Felis lybica*)
   C. The sand cat
   D. The jungle cat

2. "Obligate carnivore" means the cat:
   A. Prefers meat but can live on plants
   B. Must eat meat to obtain essential nutrients
   C. Only eats large prey
   D. Eats meat only in winter

3. Cats are described as facultatively social because they:
   A. Always live in large packs
   B. Are always strictly solitary
   C. Live alone or in colonies depending on food distribution
   D. Only group during migration

4. The matrilineal colonies cats form are built around:
   A. A single dominant male
   B. Related females across generations
   C. Unrelated adult males
   D. Mixed-species groups

5. The tapetum lucidum is:
   A. A whisker on the muzzle
   B. A reflective layer that aids night vision
   C. A scent gland on the paw
   D. An organ for tasting

6. Adult cats primarily use the meow to communicate with:
   A. Other adult cats
   B. Humans
   C. Kittens only
   D. Rival males

7. Purring in cats:
   A. Always signals contentment
   B. Occurs only during sleep
   C. Can also occur during stress, illness, or injury
   D. Is used only by kittens

8. A cat rolling over to expose its belly is usually:
   A. Requesting a belly rub
   B. Showing trust and relaxation
   C. Preparing to attack
   D. Marking territory

9. Trap-neuter-return (TNR) is a method used to:
   A. Increase feral cat numbers
   B. Sterilize feral cats and return them to their colony
   C. Train cats to hunt
   D. Relocate cats to new territories

10. The cat's righting reflex means that cats:
    A. Never get injured in falls
    B. Can rotate in mid-air to orient their feet downward, within limits
    C. Have nine literal lives
    D. Cannot fall from heights

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. B
10. B
