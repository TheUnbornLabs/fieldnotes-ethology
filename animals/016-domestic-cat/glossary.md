# Glossary — The Domestic Cat

- **Obligate carnivore** — an animal that must eat meat because it cannot synthesize certain essential nutrients (such as taurine) from plant material.
- **Facultatively social** — able to live either alone or in groups depending on conditions, rather than being obligately solitary or obligately social.
- **Matrilineal colony** — a group organized around related females across generations, as seen in cat colonies where food is concentrated.
- **Commensal** — a relationship in which one species benefits while the other is largely unaffected; cats began as commensals exploiting rodents around human grain stores.
- **Crepuscular** — most active during twilight, around dawn and dusk.
- **Tapetum lucidum** — a reflective layer behind the retina that improves night vision and causes eyeshine.
- **Vibrissae** — the specialized touch-sensitive whiskers on the face and forelegs used to sense surroundings at close range.
- **Allorubbing / bunting** — rubbing the body, head, or cheeks against another individual or object to exchange scent and reinforce social bonds.
- **Tail-up signal** — an upright tail posture used as a friendly greeting and an intention to approach amicably.
- **Slow-blink** — a slow, relaxed narrowing of the eyes that functions as a non-threatening, affiliative signal.
- **Spraying** — depositing urine on vertical surfaces to scent-mark territory and advertise reproductive status.
- **Trap-neuter-return (TNR)** — a management method of humanely trapping feral cats, sterilizing them, and returning them to their colony to stabilize numbers.
- **Induced ovulator** — a female in which ovulation is triggered by the act of mating rather than occurring on a fixed cycle.
- **Flehmen response** — an open-mouthed grimace that draws scent over the vomeronasal organ to analyze chemical signals.
- **Righting reflex** — the innate ability to rotate the body in mid-air to orient the feet downward during a fall.
