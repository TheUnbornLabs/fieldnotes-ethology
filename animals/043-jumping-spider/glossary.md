# Glossary — Book 043: The Jumping Spider

- **Salticidae** — the jumping-spider family; the largest spider family, defined by acute vision and active hunting rather than prey-catching webs.
- **Principal (anterior median) eyes** — the large, forward-facing pair of high-resolution eyes that provide the jumping spider's sharp, detailed vision.
- **Secondary eyes** — the fixed, wide-field, motion-sensitive eyes (lateral and rear) that give near-panoramic coverage and trigger the spider to turn and look.
- **Telephoto retina** — the long, layered retina at the back of the principal eye's narrow tube, giving high spatial acuity in a very small body.
- **Scanning retina** — a principal-eye retina that is moved by muscles to sweep its narrow high-acuity zone across a scene, like a searchlight.
- **Dragline silk** — the safety line a jumping spider trails behind it, anchoring leaps and enabling controlled falls; it is not used to catch prey.
- **Active hunter** — a predator that finds, stalks, and attacks mobile prey rather than trapping it in a web.
- **Detour (route) planning** — working out a roundabout path to prey in advance from visual information, then following it even when the prey is out of sight.
- **Aggressive mimicry** — producing deceptive signals (here, web vibrations) that lure prey within striking range, as *Portia* does on other spiders' webs.
- **Substrate-borne vibration** — signals sent through a leaf or the ground and sensed via organs in the legs; used in courtship songs and in *Portia*'s deception.
- **Hydraulic leg extension** — straightening the legs by raising internal blood (haemolymph) pressure, the mechanism that powers the jumping spider's leap.
- **Palps (pedipalps)** — the small paired appendages near the mouth; used in courtship display and, in males, in transferring sperm.
- **Courtship display** — the stereotyped visual and vibratory performance a male uses to attract and be assessed by a female.
- **Araneophage** — a predator that eats other spiders, as *Portia* does.
- **Ant mimicry** — resembling an ant in shape, colour, and movement as a defence against predators that avoid ants.
- **Ultraviolet (UV) vision** — sensitivity to ultraviolet light, used by some salticids to see UV-reflecting courtship ornaments.
