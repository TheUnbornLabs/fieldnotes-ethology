# Quiz — Book 043: The Jumping Spider

1. What is the primary function of the jumping spider's large anterior median eyes?
   A. Detecting vibration in webs
   B. High-resolution, detailed vision
   C. Producing silk
   D. Sensing chemicals

2. What is dragline silk used for in jumping spiders?
   A. A safety line for leaps and controlled falls
   B. Building sticky prey-catching webs
   C. Wrapping trapped insects in a snare
   D. Filtering water

3. The genus *Portia* is best known for which ability?
   A. Spinning the largest orb webs
   B. Living in large cooperative colonies
   C. Detour route planning and trial-and-error problem-solving
   D. Producing deadly venom for humans

4. How do jumping spiders power their jumps?
   A. Large extensor muscles
   B. Spring-loaded silk
   C. Compressed air sacs
   D. Hydraulic (blood-pressure) leg extension

5. The scanning behaviour of the principal eyes is achieved by:
   A. Turning the whole body constantly
   B. Moving the retinas behind fixed lenses
   C. Rotating the front lenses
   D. Closing and opening eyelids

6. Which group is famous for spectacular multi-part courtship displays combining colour and vibration?
   A. Orb-weavers (Araneidae)
   B. Trapdoor spiders
   C. Peacock spiders (*Maratus*)
   D. House spiders

7. How does *Portia* typically catch web-building spiders?
   A. By spinning a bigger competing web
   B. By spraying venom from a distance
   C. By waiting motionless for months
   D. By vibrating the victim's web to mimic prey or a mate

8. Compared with a web-building orb-weaver, a jumping spider:
   A. Invests more in silk and less in vision
   B. Invests more in vision and cognition and builds no capture web
   C. Cannot move once it settles
   D. Relies mainly on smell to find prey

9. Are jumping spiders dangerous to humans?
   A. Yes, they are highly venomous to people
   B. No, they are harmless; their venom suits small insect prey
   C. Only the peacock spiders are dangerous
   D. Only if they build a web on a person

10. Which sense is most central to the jumping spider's lifestyle?
    A. Echolocation
    B. Magnetoreception
    C. Vision
    D. Electroreception

## Answer Key

1. B
2. A
3. C
4. D
5. B
6. C
7. D
8. B
9. B
10. C
