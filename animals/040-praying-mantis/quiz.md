# Quiz — The Praying Mantis

1. The praying mantis is the only insect experimentally shown to have:
   A. Echolocation
   B. True stereoscopic 3-D vision
   C. Magnetic navigation
   D. Ultraviolet-only vision

2. Roughly how many species make up the order Mantodea?
   A. About 24
   B. About 240
   C. About 2,400
   D. About 24,000

3. The mantis's "praying" posture is actually:
   A. A resting position
   B. A cooling behavior
   C. The cocked, ready position of the raptorial forelegs
   D. A courtship signal to females

4. How was the mantis's 3-D vision demonstrated?
   A. By fitting mantises with tiny 3-D glasses and showing them illusory prey
   B. By removing one eye
   C. By recording brain waves during flight
   D. By training them to press levers

5. A mantis's single mid-line "cyclopean" ear is mainly tuned to detect:
   A. Mating calls of other mantises
   B. The ultrasound of hunting bats
   C. Low-frequency ground vibration
   D. Human speech

6. Sexual cannibalism in mantises is best described as:
   A. Universal and inevitable in all species
   B. Entirely a myth that never occurs
   C. Real but context-dependent and often overstated
   D. Performed only by males

7. The frothy, hardened case a female makes to hold her eggs is called the:
   A. Ootheca
   B. Fovea
   C. Ommatidium
   D. Pseudopupil

8. The orchid mantis's flower-like appearance appears to function as:
   A. Simple hiding only
   B. Aggressive mimicry that lures pollinating prey
   C. A warning to predators
   D. A cooling adaptation

9. Compared with the ambush-hunting mantis, the dragonfly is:
   A. A sit-and-wait predator
   B. A high-speed aerial pursuit predator
   C. A filter feeder
   D. A scavenger

10. When a flying mantis detects bat ultrasound, it typically:
    A. Freezes in place
    B. Displays bright colors
    C. Performs a rapid evasive dive or spiral
    D. Lands and folds its forelegs

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. C
4. A
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. B
9. B
10. C
