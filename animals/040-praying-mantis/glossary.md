# Glossary — The Praying Mantis

- **Ambush predator** — an animal that captures prey by waiting, hidden and motionless, until the prey comes within reach, rather than by actively chasing it.
- **Raptorial forelegs** — the mantis's grasping front legs, held folded and cocked, lined with spines, and snapped shut on prey in a fraction of a second.
- **Stereoscopic (3-D) vision** — depth perception produced by comparing the slightly different images from two forward-facing eyes; the mantis is the only insect experimentally shown to have true stereopsis.
- **Fovea** — a central region of an eye packed with high-resolution visual units, which the mantis aims at prey for sharp inspection.
- **Compound eye** — an eye made of many individual optical units (ommatidia); mantises have large, forward-facing compound eyes.
- **Crypsis** — camouflage; blending into the background (leaves, bark, grass, flowers) to avoid being detected by predator or prey.
- **Aggressive mimicry** — resembling something attractive (such as a flower) in order to lure prey, as the orchid mantis appears to do with pollinating insects.
- **Sexual cannibalism** — a female consuming her mate during or after mating; in mantises it is context-dependent and often overstated, not universal.
- **Cyclopean ear** — a single auditory organ on the body's midline; the mantis's ear detects bat ultrasound but cannot localize sound direction.
- **Ultrasound evasion** — a rapid evasive dive or spiral performed by a flying mantis when its ear detects the high-frequency calls of a hunting bat.
- **Ootheca** — the frothy, hardened egg case in which a female mantis lays and protects dozens to hundreds of eggs.
- **Deimatic display** — a startle threat display in which the mantis rears up, spreads its wings and forelegs, and flashes colors to intimidate a predator.
- **Ommatidium** — a single optical unit of a compound eye.
- **Incomplete metamorphosis** — development from nymph to adult through a series of molts without a pupal stage, as in mantises.
- **Pseudopupil** — the dark spot that appears to follow an observer, an optical effect of looking into the ommatidia aimed back at the viewer.
