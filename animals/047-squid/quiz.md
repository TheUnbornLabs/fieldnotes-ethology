# Short Quiz — Book 047: The Squid

1. How many appendages does a squid have?
   A. Six
   B. Eight arms only
   C. Eight arms plus two feeding tentacles (ten total)
   D. Twelve

2. The internal support structure of a squid is called the:
   A. Radula
   B. Gladius (pen)
   C. Funnel
   D. Hectocotylus

3. Which species is the large eastern-Pacific pack-hunting squid emphasized in this book?
   A. *Doryteuthis opalescens*
   B. *Architeuthis dux*
   C. *Dosidicus gigas*
   D. *Octopus vulgaris*

4. Squid change color and pattern using:
   A. Chromatophores in the skin
   B. Pigmented blood
   C. Shell plates
   D. Muscular fins only

5. "Semelparity" in squid means they:
   A. Live for many years
   B. Reproduce once and then die
   C. Care for their young for months
   D. Never reproduce

6. Counter-illumination is a form of camouflage that uses:
   A. Ink clouds
   B. Burrowing into sand
   C. Self-produced light to erase the silhouette from below
   D. Bright warning colors

7. The squid giant axon is famous because it:
   A. Is the largest muscle in the ocean
   B. Produces the squid's ink
   C. Controls only the eyes
   D. Was central to classic discoveries about how nerves fire

8. Diel vertical migration in Humboldt squid typically means:
   A. Staying in one spot year-round
   B. Rising to feed at night and descending by day
   C. Descending to feed by day and rising at night
   D. Migrating between oceans annually

9. Which statement about giant squid is accurate?
   A. They routinely attack ships
   B. They are deep-living and rarely seen, not aggressive monsters
   C. They are the smallest squid
   D. They live only in freshwater

10. The claim that Humboldt squid signaling is a proven language is best described as:
    A. An established scientific fact
    B. An overstatement; turn-taking and structure are active research questions
    C. Completely disproven
    D. Irrelevant to squid behavior

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. C
4. A
5. B
6. C
7. D
8. B
9. B
10. B
