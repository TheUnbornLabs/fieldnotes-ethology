# Glossary — Book 047: The Squid

- **Cephalopod** — a member of the mollusk class Cephalopoda, including squid, octopuses, cuttlefish, and nautiluses; characterized by a prominent head, arms or tentacles, and (usually) a well-developed nervous system.
- **Jet propulsion** — movement produced by drawing water into the mantle cavity and forcefully expelling it through the funnel, allowing squid to accelerate and escape rapidly.
- **Gladius (pen)** — the internal, feather-shaped support structure of a squid, the reduced and internalized remnant of the ancestral shell.
- **Chromatophore** — a pigment-filled sac in the skin, surrounded by muscles, that expands or contracts under nervous control to change color and pattern almost instantly.
- **Dynamic signaling** — the rapid, changeable use of skin patterns to communicate, as when Humboldt squid flicker patterns during group hunting.
- **Schooling** — coordinated group swimming in which individuals maintain spacing and a common direction, seen strongly in market squid.
- **Counter-illumination** — camouflage in which an animal produces its own light on its underside to match the light from above and erase its silhouette when seen from below.
- **Photophore** — a light-producing organ, which in some squid hosts light-emitting bacteria, used in counter-illumination and signaling.
- **Bioluminescence** — the production of light by living organisms; squid both produce it (via photophores) and use their large eyes to detect the bioluminescence of predators and prey.
- **Semelparity** — a life history in which an animal reproduces only once and then dies, characteristic of the focus squid species.
- **Giant axon** — the unusually large nerve fiber of squid, which conducts signals fast for rapid escape and which was central to classic discoveries about how nerve cells generate electrical signals.
- **Diel vertical migration** — the daily up-and-down movement of animals through the water column, typically rising to feed at night and descending by day.
- **Countershading** — coloration that is darker on top and lighter underneath, reducing visibility from above and below.
- **Mantle** — the muscular body of the squid that houses the internal organs and generates the water jet.
- **Hectocotylus** — a specialized arm used by male cephalopods to transfer sperm packets (spermatophores) to the female during mating.
- **Paralarva** — the tiny, independent early life stage of a squid that hatches from the egg and lives in the plankton without parental care.
