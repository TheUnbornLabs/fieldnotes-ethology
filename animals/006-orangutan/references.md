# References / Further Reading — Book 006: The Orangutan

Sources are separated into well-established works safe to name and specific claims that should be checked against a database before publication. No citations here are invented; uncertain items are flagged.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Carel van Schaik, *Among Orangutans: Red Apes and the Rise of Human Culture* — accessible account of Sumatran orangutan behaviour, ecology, tool use, and culture by a leading field researcher.
- The published fieldwork and rehabilitation writing of Biruté Galdikas — pioneering long-term study of Bornean orangutans at Tanjung Puting, Kalimantan.
- IUCN Red List — species assessments for *Pongo pygmaeus* (Bornean), *Pongo abelii* (Sumatran), and *Pongo tapanuliensis* (Tapanuli), all assessed as Critically Endangered. Consult the current online assessments for up-to-date status and population figures.
- The Orangutan Foundation — recognised conservation organisation supporting habitat protection, rehabilitation, and release.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following were checked against the live web on 2026-07-02 and confirmed:

- **Scientific name & taxonomy:** Genus *Pongo*, three species — *Pongo pygmaeus* (Bornean), *Pongo abelii* (Sumatran), *Pongo tapanuliensis* (Tapanuli); family Hominidae, order Primates — confirmed (IUCN Red List; species described 2017).
- **IUCN Red List status:** All three *Pongo* species assessed as **Critically Endangered** — confirmed via IUCN Red List.
- **Tapanuli population:** Book states "under 800 individuals" and "most threatened great ape"; confirmed — fewer than 800 individuals restricted to the Batang Toru ecosystem, the rarest great ape (IUCN).
