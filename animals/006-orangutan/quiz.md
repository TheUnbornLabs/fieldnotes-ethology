# Quiz — Book 006: The Orangutan

1. To which group do orangutans belong?
   A. Monkeys
   B. Great apes
   C. Lemurs
   D. Gibbons only

2. How many living species of orangutan are recognised?
   A. One
   B. Two
   C. Three
   D. Five

3. Orangutan social organisation is best described as:
   A. Large cohesive troops
   B. Semi-solitary, with a dispersed community
   C. One-male harems
   D. Monogamous pairs

4. "Bimaturism" in male orangutans refers to:
   A. Two breeding seasons a year
   B. Two adult male forms, flanged and unflanged
   C. Males maturing twice as fast as females
   D. Two separate species of male

5. The flanged male's long call mainly functions to:
   A. Attract prey
   B. Space out rival males and advertise to females
   C. Locate ripe fruit
   D. Signal the start of migration

6. Which best describes the orangutan interbirth interval?
   A. About one year
   B. About two to three years
   C. The longest of any mammal, roughly six to eight years
   D. There is no gap between births

7. Orangutan tool use for extracting insects and Neesia seeds is best documented in:
   A. Bornean peat swamps only
   B. Sumatran populations such as Suaq Balimbing
   C. Captivity only
   D. The Tapanuli population only

8. The main threats to wild orangutans today are:
   A. Predation by tigers and leopards
   B. Deforestation for oil palm, plus the pet trade
   C. Competition with gibbons
   D. Disease from other apes

9. What is the IUCN Red List status of all three orangutan species?
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Endangered
   D. Critically Endangered

10. Compared with chimpanzees, orangutans are:
    A. More social, living in fission-fusion communities
    B. Less intelligent because they live alone
    C. More arboreal and semi-solitary, yet also tool-using and cultural
    D. Ground-dwelling and non-tool-using

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. C
7. B
8. B
9. D
10. C
