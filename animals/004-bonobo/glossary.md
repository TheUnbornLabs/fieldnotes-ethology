# Glossary — The Bonobo (*Pan paniscus*)

**Fission-fusion** — a social system in which a community splits into smaller parties that change in size and membership over time, then merge again.
**Female coalition** — an alliance of females that cooperate, especially to support one another and collectively counter male aggression.
**Philopatry** — remaining in one's birth community for life; in bonobos, males are philopatric.
**Dispersal** — leaving the birth community to join another; in bonobos, females disperse at adolescence.
**Reconciliation** — affiliative contact between former opponents soon after a conflict, which repairs the relationship and limits escalation.
**Socio-sexual behavior** — sexual or genital contact used for social functions such as reducing tension and reconciling, rather than for reproduction.
**Self-domestication** — the hypothesis that selection against aggression produced correlated changes in bonobo development, physiology, and appearance.
**Social tolerance** — the capacity to remain in close proximity and share resources with reduced aggression.
**Matrifocal** — a social organization centered on mothers and female relationships.
**Interbirth interval** — the time between one birth and the next for a female; long in bonobos, commonly four to five years or more.
**Food-sharing** — allowing others, including non-relatives, access to food.
**GG-rubbing** — genito-genital rubbing between two female bonobos, a socio-sexual behavior that reduces tension and strengthens female bonds.
**Provisioning** — the practice of supplying food to wild animals, historically used at some field sites to habituate and observe bonobos.
**Frugivore** — an animal whose diet is dominated by fruit; bonobos are primarily frugivorous.
**Sexual swelling** — the swelling of a female's genital region across the reproductive cycle; in bonobos it is present over a longer portion of the cycle than in chimpanzees.
