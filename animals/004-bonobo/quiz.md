# Quiz — The Bonobo (*Pan paniscus*)

1. Bonobos are found naturally in only one country. Which is it?
A. Uganda
B. Democratic Republic of the Congo
C. Tanzania
D. Cameroon

2. What geographic feature marks the northern boundary of the bonobo's range?
A. The Congo River
B. The Nile River
C. The Great Rift Valley
D. Lake Victoria

3. In bonobo society, which individuals form the strongest, most influential alliances?
A. Related males
B. Immigrant females
C. Juveniles
D. Solitary old males

4. Which term describes a community that splits into changing subgroups and later merges?
A. Eusocial
B. Territorial
C. Fission-fusion
D. Solitary

5. Compared with chimpanzees, bonobos show:
A. More frequent lethal coalitionary killing
B. Higher social tolerance and lower lethal aggression
C. Strict male dominance over females
D. No sexual behavior

6. The self-domestication hypothesis for bonobos is associated with:
A. Selection *against* aggression producing correlated changes
B. Deliberate breeding by humans
C. Loss of all social behavior
D. Increased male violence over time

7. Bonobo socio-sexual behavior primarily functions to:
A. Only produce offspring
B. Reduce tension, reconcile, and maintain bonds
C. Mark territory chemically
D. Signal alarm to predators

8. Which of the following is TRUE of the bonobo?
A. It is a subspecies of chimpanzee
B. It is a distinct species, *Pan paniscus*
C. It lives across most of Africa
D. It reproduces very rapidly

9. What is a major reason bonobos are vulnerable to decline?
A. Extremely fast reproduction
B. Long interbirth intervals and slow reproduction
C. Immunity to hunting
D. A vast continental range

10. Which is a well-known bonobo sanctuary in the DRC?
A. Gombe
B. Lola ya Bonobo
C. Ngorongoro
D. Serengeti

## Answer Key

1. B
2. A
3. B
4. C
5. B
6. A
7. B
8. B
9. B
10. B
