# Book 025 — The Bottlenose Dolphin: Quiz

1. To which biological group does the bottlenose dolphin belong?
   A. A fish
   B. A cetacean (marine mammal)
   C. An amphibian
   D. A reptile

2. Echolocation in dolphins is best described as:
   A. A form of extrasensory perception
   B. A magnetic sense
   C. Biosonar — sensing by emitting clicks and hearing echoes
   D. Vision enhanced by the melon

3. The fatty structure that focuses a dolphin's echolocation clicks is the:
   A. Blowhole
   B. Melon
   C. Fluke
   D. Rostrum gland

4. A signature whistle is:
   A. A warning call shared by the whole group
   B. An individually distinctive, learned whistle that broadcasts identity
   C. A sound used only during hunting
   D. An innate call identical in all dolphins

5. The nested, multi-level male alliances of bottlenose dolphins are best known from:
   A. The Mediterranean Sea
   B. Shark Bay, Western Australia
   C. The Arctic Ocean
   D. The Amazon River

6. "Sponging" in Shark Bay dolphins is an example of:
   A. A play behaviour with no function
   B. A culturally transmitted foraging tradition using a tool
   C. An innate reflex present in all dolphins
   D. A courtship display

7. Dolphins rest by:
   A. Sleeping fully like land mammals
   B. Not sleeping at all
   C. Unihemispheric slow-wave sleep, one hemisphere at a time
   D. Burrowing into the seabed

8. The dolphin's fixed "smile" is:
   A. A reliable sign of happiness
   B. The fixed shape of its jaw anatomy, not an emotion
   C. A signal used only in captivity
   D. A response to human presence

9. Which statement is most accurate about dolphin behaviour?
   A. Dolphins are always gentle and altruistic
   B. Dolphins never compete with one another
   C. Dolphins show cooperation but also aggression, infanticide, and coercive mating
   D. Dolphins have a full language with grammar

10. A major human-caused threat to wild bottlenose dolphins is:
    A. Fisheries bycatch in nets and gear
    B. Loss of body hair
    C. Competition with fish for oxygen
    D. Excessive echolocation

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. C
8. B
9. C
10. A
