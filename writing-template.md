# Writing Template — Book Structure

Every `book.md` follows this exact 20-section structure. Keep section headings and numbering consistent so the website build can parse them. Target 2,500–5,000 words; write species-specific content in every section and include only the sub-topics relevant to that species.

## Title line

`# Book NNN — [Species]: [Short Thematic Subtitle]`

Example: `# Book 001 — The Wolf: Pack Life, Cooperation, and Survival Intelligence`

## Sections

1. **Opening Summary** — why this species matters in ethology.
2. **Scientific Identity** — common name, scientific name, taxonomic group, habitat, geographic range, diet, lifespan, conservation status (if available).
3. **Evolutionary Background** — the selective pressures that shaped the species' behavior.
4. **Daily Life and Activity Pattern** — diurnal / nocturnal / crepuscular; migratory, territorial, nomadic, colonial, solitary, or social.
5. **Brain, Senses, and Perception** — how the species perceives its world. Include only relevant senses (vision, smell, hearing, touch, vibration, magnetoreception, echolocation, electroreception, chemical detection).
6. **Feeding Behavior** — only the relevant modes (hunting, foraging, grazing, scavenging, filtering, caching, cooperative feeding, food competition).
7. **Social Behavior** — solitary/pairs/packs/herds/troops/colonies/flocks; dominance, cooperation, conflict, bonding, kinship, social learning.
8. **Communication** — vocal, body language, visual displays, chemical signals, touch, alarm calls, courtship signals, territorial signals.
9. **Reproduction and Courtship** — mating systems, mate choice, competition, courtship displays, breeding seasons, reproductive strategy.
10. **Parenting and Early Development** — birth/hatching, juvenile behavior, parental care, independence, learning, survival risks.
11. **Learning and Intelligence** — problem-solving, memory, social learning, tool use, adaptability, exploration, cognitive limits.
12. **Fear, Stress, and Defense** — predator avoidance, threat displays, hiding, fleeing, freezing, group defense, camouflage, venom, armor, alarm behavior.
13. **Human Interaction** — domestication, captivity, urban life, conservation, human conflict, cultural symbolism, exploitation, ethical concerns.
14. **Common Myths vs Scientific Reality** — at least 5 myths, each with a calm scientific correction. Format:

    ```text
    Myth 1:
    Scientific reality:
    ```

15. **Comparison With Another Species** — compare against one related or contrasting species.
16. **Ethology Lessons** — what the species teaches about behavior, adaptation, survival, cognition, society, ecology, or evolution. No fake moral lessons.
17. **Glossary** — 10–20 key terms (mirrored into `glossary.md`).
18. **Study Questions** — 10 open-ended questions.
19. **Short Quiz** — 10 multiple-choice questions with an answer key (mirrored into `quiz.md`).
20. **References / Further Reading** — credible sources only; mirrored into `references.md`. Never invent citations.

## Companion files

- `summary.md` — 300–500 word standalone overview.
- `glossary.md` — the section-17 terms as a clean list with definitions.
- `quiz.md` — the section-19 quiz with answer key.
- `references.md` — the section-20 list, with any uncertain source marked *"Further reading to verify before final publication."*

## Style reminders

Serious, clear, public-friendly, species-specific, well-structured, useful, filler-free. Avoid generic AI phrasing, cross-book repetition, fake emotion, overdramatization, motivational filler, unsupported claims, fake citations, "top 10 facts" listicles, and excess anthropomorphism. Write compact public science education, not a children's encyclopedia.
