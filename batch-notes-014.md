## Key Ethological Themes

**Sexual Selection & Display (066, 068, 069):** Three books feature elaborate male displays driven by female choice. The Indian peafowl's train is a textbook example of an expensive signal revealing male quality. The wild turkey's gobbling and strut displays serve both male-male signaling and female attraction. The ostrich's territorial displays are more muted but still central to mating.

**Mating Systems (066, 067, 068, 069, 070):** Covers spectrum from lek-mating (peafowl) to polygyny (turkey, ostrich) to monogamy (emu). The contrast between peafowl and emu is instructive: both large, flightless birds, but opposite social systems.

**Parental Care Variation (067, 068, 069, 070):** Ranges from male-only (emu), to female-only (peafowl, ostrich), to both parents assisting (chicken, turkey to lesser extent). Illustrates diversity of parental investment strategies.

**Domestication & Human Interaction (066, 067, 069, 070):** All five books include substantial sections on human-animal relationships, from ancient sacred symbolism (peafowl) to modern farming (chicken, ostrich) to conservation recovery (turkey).
