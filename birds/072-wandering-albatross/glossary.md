# Glossary

**Altricial** — of chicks, helpless at hatching and requiring extended parental care.
**Biennial breeding** — breeding once every two years, as in successful wandering albatross pairs.
**Bycatch** — the incidental capture of non-target animals (such as albatrosses on longline hooks) during fishing.
**Diomedea exulans** — the scientific name of the wandering albatross.
**Dynamic soaring** — a flight technique that extracts energy from the gradient of wind speed above the sea, allowing sustained travel with minimal flapping.
**Fledging** — the stage at which a young bird completes development and departs the nest site; in albatrosses this takes about ten months or more.
**Life history** — the pattern of growth, reproduction, and survival characterizing a species; albatrosses have a slow, high-investment life history.
**Longevity** — length of life; wandering albatrosses commonly live beyond 40 to 50 years.
**Longline fishing** — a fishing method using long lines of baited hooks, a major source of albatross mortality.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which individuals form long-term pair bonds.
**Pelagic** — living primarily on the open ocean.
**Procellariiformes** — the order of tube-nosed seabirds that includes albatrosses, petrels, and shearwaters.
**Ritualized display** — a stereotyped courtship behavior used to establish and maintain pair bonds.
**Sub-Antarctic islands** — remote southern islands where albatrosses breed.
**Wingspan** — the distance between wingtips; the wandering albatross has the largest of any living bird.
