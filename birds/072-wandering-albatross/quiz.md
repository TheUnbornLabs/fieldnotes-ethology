# Short Quiz

1. Wandering albatrosses typically breed: a) Every two years  b) Annually  c) Continuously  d) Only once in a lifetime

2. The largest wandering albatross wingspans approach or slightly exceed: a) 1.5 meters  b) 2 meters  c) 3.5 meters  d) 6 meters

3. The albatross's primary feeding behavior is: a) Deep pursuit diving  b) Filter-feeding  c) Taking squid at the surface  d) Cooperative fish herding

4. Pair bonds in wandering albatrosses are usually: a) Long-term, often lasting many years  b) One season only  c) Absent  d) Formed anew each week

5. Fledging of an albatross chick typically takes about: a) 2–3 months  b) 6 months  c) 10 or more months  d) 2 years

6. Albatrosses are thought to navigate using: a) Visual landmarks only  b) A combination of cues including celestial and olfactory information  c) Sound alone  d) No orientation ability

7. The lifespan of wandering albatrosses commonly reaches: a) 10–15 years  b) 20–30 years  c) 40–50 years or more  d) Under 5 years

8. The most severe modern threat to wandering albatrosses is: a) Longline fishing bycatch  b) Habitat loss  c) Oil spills  d) Overhunting

9. Dynamic soaring relies chiefly on: a) Continuous active flapping  b) Deep diving  c) Thermal updrafts over land  d) Extracting energy from wind gradients over waves

10. Wandering albatrosses are: a) Solitary and non-bonding  b) Socially monogamous with long-term pair bonds  c) Polygamous  d) Anonymous colonial breeders

**Answer Key:** 1-a, 2-c, 3-c, 4-a, 5-c, 6-b, 7-c, 8-a, 9-d, 10-b
