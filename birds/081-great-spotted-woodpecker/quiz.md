# Short Quiz — The Great Spotted Woodpecker (*Dendrocopos major*)

1. The great spotted woodpecker's rapid, rhythmic hammering in spring is primarily:
   A. A communication signal (drumming)
   B. A way of digging out insect larvae
   C. A method of sharpening the bill
   D. A cooling behaviour

2. The bird's foot arrangement, with two toes forward and two back, is called:
   A. Anisodactyl
   B. Syndactyl
   C. Zygodactyl
   D. Palmate

3. The long, barbed, sticky tongue is supported by the:
   A. Sternum
   B. Furcula
   C. Tarsus
   D. Hyoid apparatus

4. An "anvil," as used by this woodpecker, is:
   A. The nest chamber
   B. A resonant drumming branch
   C. A crevice for wedging and opening cones or nuts
   D. A communal roost

5. Which statement about the woodpecker's skull is most accurate?
   A. It is a proven perfect shock absorber
   B. It has features plausibly aiding impact tolerance, but the mechanism is not fully settled
   C. It provides no protection at all
   D. It is identical to other songbirds' skulls

6. Regarding drumming and sex:
   A. Only males drum
   B. Only females drum
   C. Both sexes drum, with males generally drumming more
   D. Drumming is unrelated to sex or season

7. The great spotted woodpecker's diet is best described as:
   A. Broadly omnivorous, including insects, seeds, and other birds' young
   B. Strictly insectivorous
   C. Strictly seed-eating
   D. Nectar-feeding

8. Woodpecker chicks hatch:
   A. Altricial — naked, blind, and helpless
   B. Fully feathered and able to fly
   C. Precocial and mobile
   D. Outside the nest

9. Woodpeckers are called ecological engineers because they:
   A. Build dams
   B. Excavate cavities later used by other species
   C. Pollinate flowers
   D. Disperse large seeds

10. Compared with the great spotted woodpecker, the green woodpecker:
   A. Drums far more and rarely feeds on the ground
   B. Is a nectar specialist
   C. Lacks a long tongue
   D. Feeds heavily on ants on the ground and drums little

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. D
4. C
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. A
9. B
10. D
