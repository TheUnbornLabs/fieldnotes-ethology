# Glossary — The Carrion Crow (*Corvus corone*)

- **Corvid** — a member of the crow family (Corvidae), which includes crows, ravens, rooks, jackdaws, magpies, and jays; known for large brains and flexible behavior.
- **Generalist** — a species that exploits a wide range of foods and habitats rather than specialising narrowly.
- **Urban adapter (synanthrope)** — an animal that thrives in close association with humans and human-built environments.
- **Caching** — hiding surplus food to recover and eat later; relies on spatial memory.
- **Aesop's fable task (water displacement)** — an experimental problem in which an animal raises a water level by dropping in objects to bring a floating reward within reach; used to probe understanding of physical cause and effect.
- **Face recognition** — the ability to identify and remember specific individual human faces or forms.
- **Social learning** — acquiring information or behavior by observing others rather than by individual trial and error.
- **Mobbing** — coordinated harassment of a predator or threat by several birds, often with alarm calls, to drive it off and advertise its presence.
- **Communal roost** — a large gathering of birds that assemble to spend the night together, often numbering hundreds.
- **Hybrid zone** — a geographic band where two closely related forms (here the carrion crow and the hooded crow) meet and interbreed.
- **Innovation** — the invention or first performance of a new behavior, such as a novel way of obtaining food.
- **Neophobia** — wariness of, or avoidance of, unfamiliar objects and situations.
- **Altricial** — describing young that hatch helpless, blind, and dependent on parental care.
- **Cooperative breeding** — a system in which individuals other than the parents (often grown offspring) help raise the young.
