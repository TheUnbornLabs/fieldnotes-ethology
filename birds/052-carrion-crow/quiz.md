# Short Quiz — The Carrion Crow (*Corvus corone*)

1. The scientific name of the carrion crow is:
   A. *Corvus corone*
   B. *Corvus corax*
   C. *Corvus cornix*
   D. *Corvus monedula*

2. The carrion crow is best described ecologically as a:
   A. Narrow dietary specialist
   B. Obligate scavenger only
   C. Flexible generalist and synanthrope
   D. Nocturnal hunter

3. In water-displacement ("Aesop's fable") tasks, a corvid obtains a floating reward by:
   A. Dropping stones to raise the water level
   B. Blowing on the water
   C. Tipping the tube over
   D. Waiting for evaporation

4. The claim that crows deliberately use car traffic to crack nuts is best described as:
   A. Fully confirmed by controlled experiments
   B. Physically impossible
   C. Largely anecdotal and debated
   D. Unique to the New Caledonian crow

5. Much of the strongest experimental work on crows recognising individual human faces was done on:
   A. The common raven
   B. The American crow (by Marzluff and colleagues)
   C. The hooded crow
   D. The jackdaw

6. "Crow funerals" — gatherings around a dead crow — are best explained as:
   A. Ritual mourning
   B. Feeding aggregations
   C. Courtship displays
   D. Information gathering about danger

7. The carrion crow differs from the New Caledonian crow in that it:
   A. Is a habitual manufacturer of foraging tools
   B. Is not a habitual tool-user but a flexible problem-solver
   C. Cannot cache food
   D. Lacks colour vision

8. The hybrid zone involving the carrion crow is shared with the:
   A. Rook
   B. Common raven
   C. Magpie
   D. Hooded crow

9. Compared with the common raven, the carrion crow is:
   A. Larger, with a wedge-shaped tail
   B. Smaller and more of an urban/farmland generalist
   C. A wilderness specialist of remote uplands
   D. Unable to vocalise

10. Neophobia in crows refers to:
    A. Fear of other crows
    B. Attraction to novel foods
    C. Wariness of unfamiliar objects and situations
    D. Inability to learn

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. A
4. C
5. B
6. D
7. B
8. D
9. B
10. C
