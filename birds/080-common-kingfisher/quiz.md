# Quiz — Book 080: The Common Kingfisher

1. The Common Kingfisher catches fish mainly by:
   A. Filtering water like a flamingo
   B. Plunge-diving, usually from a perch
   C. Wading in the shallows
   D. Scavenging dead fish

2. The kingfisher's brilliant blue colour is produced mainly by:
   A. Blue pigment
   B. Diet
   C. The microscopic structure of its feathers (structural colour)
   D. Age

3. A major visual challenge the kingfisher must overcome when hunting is:
   A. Total darkness
   B. Refraction displacing the apparent position of fish
   C. Its own reflection frightening prey
   D. Colour-blindness

4. The Common Kingfisher nests:
   A. On the open sea
   B. In a cup nest in a tree
   C. In a tunnel dug into an earth bank
   D. On bare rock

5. Outside the breeding season, the Common Kingfisher is typically:
   A. Highly social, living in flocks
   B. Solitary and territorial
   C. Cooperative with several helpers
   D. Nomadic in large groups

6. Common Kingfisher chicks hatch:
   A. Precocial, able to fish within a day
   B. Altricial — naked, blind, and helpless
   C. Fully feathered
   D. Able to leave the nest immediately

7. "Courtship feeding" in the kingfisher refers to:
   A. The female feeding the chicks
   B. The male presenting fish to the female
   C. Chicks feeding one another
   D. Feeding on courtship displays

8. The Common Kingfisher is considered a good indicator species because it:
   A. Tolerates any water conditions
   B. Depends on clean, clear, fish-bearing water
   C. Lives only in cities
   D. Migrates enormous distances

9. Compared with the Common Kingfisher, the Pied Kingfisher is notable for:
   A. Not eating fish
   B. Habitual hovering and greater sociality
   C. Nesting in trees
   D. Being nocturnal

10. The word "halcyon" comes from:
   A. A modern scientific term for diving
   B. An ancient Greek legend about the kingfisher calming the sea
   C. The bird's call
   D. The colour of its feathers

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. C
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. B
10. B
