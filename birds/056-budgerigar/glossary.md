# Glossary — The Budgerigar

- **Nomadic / irruptive** — moving unpredictably in response to conditions (such as rainfall and food) rather than following fixed territories or set migration routes, with populations that can surge and disperse.
- **Flock** — a coordinated social group in which budgerigars forage, drink, roost, and fly together, often in very large, synchronised numbers.
- **Open-ended vocal learning** — the capacity to acquire and modify vocalisations throughout life, rather than fixing them once early in development.
- **Contact call** — a short, learned vocal signal used to keep in touch, coordinate movement, and identify individuals.
- **Call convergence** — the process by which social partners' calls become more alike over time through learning, so that mates and flockmates come to share call types.
- **Warble song** — the long, quiet, varied stream of notes produced mainly by male budgerigars, especially in courtship; learned and modifiable.
- **Contagious yawning** — yawning triggered by seeing another individual yawn; budgerigars were the first non-mammal shown to exhibit it.
- **Mate choice** — selection of a mate based on assessable traits; in budgerigars influenced by ultraviolet plumage signals and by demonstrated problem-solving ability.
- **Tetrachromacy** — colour vision based on four cone types, including an ultraviolet-sensitive cone, giving a colour space richer than human trichromacy.
- **Ultraviolet plumage** — feather regions (crown and cheeks) that reflect and fluoresce in ultraviolet light, serving as social and sexual signals visible to tetrachromatic eyes.
- **Cavity nester** — a bird that nests inside enclosed spaces such as tree hollows rather than in open nests.
- **Opportunistic breeding** — reproducing when conditions (here, rainfall and the resulting seed flush) are favourable, rather than on a fixed seasonal schedule.
- **Altricial** — hatching in a helpless, undeveloped state requiring extended parental care.
- **Granivore** — an animal whose diet is dominated by seeds.
- **Social contagion** — the spread of a behaviour or state (such as yawning or alarm) through a group by observation of others.
