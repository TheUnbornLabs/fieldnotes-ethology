# Book 094 — The European Robin: Quiz

1. The European robin belongs to which family?
   A. Muscicapidae (Old World flycatchers and chats)
   B. Turdidae (thrushes)
   C. Fringillidae (finches)
   D. Passeridae (Old World sparrows)

2. Classic experiments (associated with David Lack) showed that a territorial robin will attack:
   A. Any bird of any colour
   B. A mere tuft of red feathers
   C. Only a complete, living rival
   D. A blue model bird

3. The robin's red breast functions mainly as:
   A. Camouflage
   B. A cooling structure
   C. A mate-only signal with no role in aggression
   D. A signal that releases territorial aggression

4. Unusually for a small songbird, European robins:
   A. Defend individual feeding territories even in winter
   B. Never defend territories
   C. Live only in large flocks
   D. Migrate as a single flock

5. Evidence associated with the Wiltschkos indicates the robin's magnetic compass is:
   A. A polarity compass like a bar magnet
   B. An inclination compass that appears light-dependent
   C. Based on smell
   D. Nonexistent

6. The current scientific status of the robin's magnetoreception mechanism is:
   A. Fully settled and understood
   B. Disproven
   C. Strong behavioural evidence exists, but the mechanism is still debated
   D. Never studied

7. The European robin and the American robin are:
   A. The same species
   B. Close relatives in the same genus
   C. Not closely related (a chat and a thrush respectively)
   D. Both thrushes

8. Robins recognise their territorial neighbours mainly by:
   A. Scent
   B. Individual song
   C. Nest location alone
   D. Bill colour

9. Juvenile robins:
   A. Have a full red breast
   B. Are brighter red than adults
   C. Are spotted brown and lack the red breast, avoiding adult aggression
   D. Are blue

10. The robin's diet is:
    A. Strictly seeds year-round
    B. Fish
    C. Nectar only
    D. Mainly invertebrates, with more fruit and seeds in autumn and winter

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. D
4. A
5. B
6. C
7. C
8. B
9. C
10. D
