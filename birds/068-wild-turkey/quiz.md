# Book 068 — The Wild Turkey: Quiz

1. Adult male turkeys are called: a) Toms  b) Gobblers  c) Jakes  d) Struts

2. Young male turkeys in their first year are called: a) Poults  b) Jakes  c) Hens  d) Chicks

3. Wild turkeys breed most intensely in: a) Summer  b) Fall  c) Winter/early spring  d) All seasons equally

4. The gobble's primary function is: a) Courtship only  b) Alarm calling  c) Feeding coordination  d) Long-distance advertisement of presence and dominance

5. Female turkeys with chicks form: a) Permanent territories  b) Individual nesting pairs  c) Loose flocks, sometimes with alloparenting  d) Solitary nests

6. Turkey chicks are: a) Altricial (helpless)  b) Semi-precocial  c) Nidicolous  d) Precocial (mobile, foraging independently)

7. The near-extinction and recovery of wild turkeys was due to: a) Climate change  b) Disease  c) Introduction of invasive predators  d) Intensive hunting followed by habitat restoration and management

8. Wild turkeys roost: a) In trees for predator avoidance  b) Only on the ground  c) In burrows  d) Underground

9. Domestic turkeys differ from wild turkeys in: a) Size and white plumage color  b) Lack of flight ability  c) Shorter lifespan  d) No social behavior

10. Wild turkeys now inhabit: a) Only wilderness  b) Temperate forests in eastern North America and Mexico  c) Suburban areas and mixed landscapes across a broad range  d) Only agricultural areas

**Answer Key:** 1-b, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d, 5-c, 6-d, 7-d, 8-a, 9-a, 10-c
