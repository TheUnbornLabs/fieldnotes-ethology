## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Sagittarius serpentarius*; monotypic family Sagittariidae; order Accipitriformes — confirmed.
- **IUCN Red List status: Endangered** — confirmed. The species was uplisted to Endangered (from Vulnerable) because of rapid population declines from habitat degradation, disturbance, hunting, and capture for trade; population estimated ~6,700–67,000 mature individuals and decreasing (IUCN Red List / BirdLife DataZone; Animal Diversity Web).
- **Kick-strike biomechanics** — confirmed. Portugal et al., "The fast and forceful kicking strike of the secretary bird," *Current Biology* 26(1), 2016 (published online Jan 2016). Measured strikes averaged 5.1 ± 0.9 body weights (195 ± 34 N) with foot-contact duration of 15 ± 4.4 ms, using a captive male ("Madeleine"/"Madeline", ~3.96 kg) at the Hawk Conservancy Trust striking a target. The book's "roughly five times body weight... about fifteen milliseconds" is accurate. (This is the specific paper the "to verify" note requested; authors/journal/year now confirmed.)
- Diet-composition proportions and name-etymology debate remain flagged (genuinely disputed / not settled).
