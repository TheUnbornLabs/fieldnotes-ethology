# Quiz — Book 065: The Secretary Bird

1. To which order of birds does the secretary bird belong?
   - A. Accipitriformes (birds of prey)
   - B. Gruiformes (cranes)
   - C. Ciconiiformes (storks)
   - D. Passeriformes (perching birds)

2. The secretary bird is the only living member of which family?
   - A. Accipitridae
   - B. Sagittariidae
   - C. Falconidae
   - D. Cathartidae

3. Roughly how much force does the secretary bird's kick strike deliver?
   - A. About one-tenth of its body weight
   - B. About equal to its body weight
   - C. About five times its body weight
   - D. About fifty times its body weight

4. Approximately how long is the foot in contact with the target during a strike?
   - A. About 15 milliseconds
   - B. About 1 second
   - C. About 5 seconds
   - D. About 1 minute

5. The secretary bird's diet is best described as:
   - A. Exclusively snakes
   - B. Exclusively insects
   - C. Fruit and seeds
   - D. A broad generalist diet of insects, small mammals, reptiles, and more

6. How does the secretary bird avoid being bitten when attacking venomous snakes?
   - A. It is fully immune to all snake venom
   - B. Fast, accurate kicks, long legs, and tough scaled leg skin
   - C. It only attacks dead snakes
   - D. It coats its legs in a protective mucus

7. Where does the secretary bird typically build its nest?
   - A. On the bare ground in the open
   - B. In burrows
   - C. In the flat crown of a tree such as an acacia
   - D. On cliff ledges

8. Which statement about the secretary bird's flight is correct?
   - A. It is flightless
   - B. It can barely glide
   - C. It only flies at night
   - D. It flies and soars well and performs aerial courtship displays

9. What is the secretary bird's current IUCN Red List status?
   - A. Least Concern
   - B. Near Threatened
   - C. Endangered
   - D. Extinct

10. Which is the most accurate statement about the origin of the name "secretary bird"?
    - A. It is definitely from carrying quill pens like a clerk
    - B. Its origin is uncertain and disputed
    - C. It is named after its discoverer
    - D. It comes from its call

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. C
4. A
5. D
6. B
7. C
8. D
9. C
10. B
