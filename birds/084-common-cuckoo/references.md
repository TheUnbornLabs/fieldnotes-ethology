# References / Further Reading — The Common Cuckoo (*Cuculus canorus*)

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those that should be checked against a database before final publication. Following the collection's citation policy, no authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, or page numbers have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Cuculus canorus* (global status: Least Concern). Consult the current Red List entry for range, population, and threats.
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology, *Birds of the World* — species account for the common cuckoo, covering identification, behavior, brood parasitism, and distribution.
- N. B. Davies, *Cuckoos, Cowbirds and Other Cheats* — a standard scholarly treatment of avian brood parasitism and the cuckoo–host arms race.
- Standard field guides and handbooks to the birds of Europe and the Western Palearctic — for identification, range, and life-history summaries.
- Standard animal-behavior textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for coevolution, arms races, supernormal stimuli, and innate behavior.
