# Short Quiz — The Common Cuckoo (*Cuculus canorus*)

1. The common cuckoo is best described as:
   A. A cooperative breeder
   B. An obligate brood parasite
   C. A colonial nester
   D. A bird that raises its own young

2. Egg mimicry in cuckoos evolved because:
   A. It makes the eggs stronger
   B. It helps the eggs stay warm
   C. It attracts mates
   D. It reduces the chance that hosts detect and reject the egg

3. When a newly hatched cuckoo chick ejects host eggs and chicks, it is:
   A. Executing an evolved, largely innate behavior that removes competitors
   B. Acting out of cruelty
   C. Playing
   D. Following its foster parents' instructions

4. A host-specific lineage of female cuckoos whose eggs match a particular host is called a:
   A. Colony
   B. Flock
   C. Gens
   D. Pair

5. The familiar two-note "cuck-oo" call is given by:
   A. The female
   B. The male
   C. Both sexes equally
   D. The chick

6. Host birds defend against cuckoos by:
   A. Ignoring them entirely
   B. Raising cuckoo chicks preferentially
   C. Building larger nests only
   D. Mobbing adults and rejecting foreign eggs

7. The cuckoo chick's begging call is a supernormal stimulus because it:
   A. Is very quiet
   B. Mimics the sound of a whole brood, drawing extra food
   C. Sounds like the male's call
   D. Warns of predators

8. Adult common cuckoos specialise on eating:
   A. Seeds
   B. Nectar
   C. Hairy caterpillars that many birds avoid
   D. Small fish

9. Parental care of the cuckoo chick is provided by:
   A. The female cuckoo
   B. Both cuckoo parents
   C. The deceived host parents
   D. A group of cuckoos

10. The cuckoo–host relationship is a classic example of:
   A. A coevolutionary arms race
   B. Mutualism benefiting both
   C. Commensalism with no effect on the host
   D. Random chance

## Answer Key

1. B
2. D
3. A
4. C
5. B
6. D
7. B
8. C
9. C
10. A
