# References / Further Reading — Book 076: The Greater Flamingo

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List of Threatened Species — species assessment for *Phoenicopterus roseus* (Greater Flamingo). The authoritative source for conservation status, range, and population.
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology, *Birds of the World* — species account for the Greater Flamingo, covering identification, distribution, feeding, breeding, and behavior.
- Alcock, J. *Animal Behavior: An Evolutionary Approach*. A standard university text useful for the general principles of foraging specialisation, honest signalling, and colonial breeding referenced in this book.
- Tinbergen, N. *The Study of Instinct*. Foundational ethological text relevant to the analysis of stereotyped displays and the study of behavior discussed throughout the series.
- Standard regional field guides (e.g., established guides to the birds of Europe, Africa, and the Middle East) for morphology, plumage development, and range.
