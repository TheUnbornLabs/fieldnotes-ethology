# Quiz — Book 076: The Greater Flamingo

1. The pink colour of a Greater Flamingo comes primarily from:
   A. Its genes alone
   B. Carotenoid pigments in its food
   C. The salinity of the water
   D. Sun exposure

2. A flamingo feeds by:
   A. Hunting fish by sight
   B. Pecking seeds from mud
   C. Filtering small organisms through lamellae in an inverted bill
   D. Scavenging carrion

3. Compared with the Greater Flamingo, the Lesser Flamingo has a bill filter that is:
   A. Coarser, taking larger prey
   B. Finer, specialising on tiny cyanobacteria
   C. Identical in structure
   D. Absent

4. Flamingo breeding is best described as:
   A. Fixed to the same date every year
   B. Random and unpredictable in the individual
   C. Opportunistic, cued by water and food, and highly synchronised
   D. Continuous throughout the year

5. Flamingo chicks are fed by their parents on:
   A. Whole fish
   B. Regurgitated seeds
   C. A carotenoid-rich "crop milk" produced by both parents
   D. Insects caught in flight

6. Within a crèche of thousands, a flamingo parent finds its own chick by:
   A. Feeding whichever chick is nearest
   B. Recognising its chick's individual call
   C. Smell
   D. Colour of the down

7. The flamingo's typical breeding habitat is:
   A. The open ocean
   B. Freshwater forest ponds
   C. Saline and alkaline lakes, lagoons, and salt pans
   D. Alpine streams

8. Wild Greater Flamingos commonly live to about:
   A. 5 years
   B. 10 years
   C. 20 to 30 years or more
   D. 60 years

9. Group displays such as head-flagging and wing-saluting mainly function to:
   A. Frighten predators
   B. Cool the birds down
   C. Synchronise and stimulate breeding across the colony
   D. Establish permanent lifelong territories

10. A newly hatched flamingo chick is:
   A. Bright pink with a bent bill
   B. Downy, grey or whitish, with a straight bill
   C. Naked and blind
   D. Fully able to filter-feed

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. C
5. C
6. B
7. C
8. C
9. C
10. B
