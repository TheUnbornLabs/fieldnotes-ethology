# Short Quiz — The European Nightjar (*Caprimulgus europaeus*)

1. The name "goatsucker" reflects a false belief that the nightjar:
   A. Eats goats
   B. Lives among goats for warmth
   C. Sounds like a goat
   D. Drinks milk from goats

2. European nightjars locate their insect prey mainly by:
   A. Echolocation
   B. Smell
   C. Vision in low light
   D. Touch

3. The nightjar's wide, bristle-fringed mouth is used to:
   A. Catch flying insects
   B. Drink milk
   C. Dig burrows
   D. Crack seeds

4. The male nightjar's sustained, mechanical song is called:
   A. Yaffling
   B. Drumming
   C. Churring
   D. Piping

5. Nightjars protect their eggs by:
   A. Building a deep hidden nest
   B. Laying on bare ground and relying on camouflage
   C. Nesting in tree cavities
   D. Guarding them aggressively day and night

6. Nightjar breeding is notably timed to:
   A. The tides
   B. Rainfall only
   C. No particular cue
   D. The lunar cycle, so chick-feeding peaks on brighter nights

7. During the day, a European nightjar typically:
   A. Hunts actively
   B. Rests motionless, relying on camouflage
   C. Flocks with other nightjars
   D. Swims

8. When a predator threatens the nest, a nightjar may:
   A. Perform a distraction display, such as feigning injury
   B. Attack the predator directly
   C. Abandon the territory permanently
   D. Call other nightjars for help

9. The nightjar's chicks hatch:
   A. Naked, blind, and helpless (altricial)
   B. Downy and mobile with open eyes (semi-precocial)
   C. Fully able to fly
   D. Underground

10. Compared with the nightjar, the common swift is:
   A. A nocturnal ground-nester
   B. A seed-eating ground forager
   C. A diurnal, high-speed aerial hunter that nests in cavities
   D. A flightless bird

## Answer Key

1. D
2. C
3. A
4. C
5. B
6. D
7. B
8. A
9. B
10. C
