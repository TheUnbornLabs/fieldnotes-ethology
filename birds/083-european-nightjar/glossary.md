# Glossary — The European Nightjar (*Caprimulgus europaeus*)

**Crepuscular** — most active at dusk and dawn.
**Nocturnal** — active at night.
**Aerial insectivore** — a bird that catches flying insects on the wing.
**Crypsis (camouflage)** — concealment through coloration and pattern that make an animal hard to detect against its background.
**Gape** — the width of the open mouth; in nightjars very wide, fringed with bristles, and used to catch insects.
**Rictal bristles** — stiff, hair-like feathers around the mouth that may help detect or funnel aerial prey.
**Churring** — the nightjar's sustained, mechanical, rolling song used to advertise territory and attract a mate.
**Wing-clapping** — a mechanical sound made by clapping the wings together in display flight.
**Distraction display** — conspicuous behavior, such as feigning injury, used to lead a predator away from eggs or young.
**Semi-precocial** — hatching downy and mobile with open eyes but still dependent on parents for food.
**Ground nesting** — laying eggs on the ground; nightjars build no nest structure.
**Lunar-cycle timing** — the scheduling of breeding so that peak chick-feeding coincides with brighter moonlit nights.
**Migrant** — a species that moves seasonally between breeding and wintering ranges, here Europe and sub-Saharan Africa.
**Torpor** — a temporary lowering of metabolism and body temperature to save energy in cold or food-poor conditions.
