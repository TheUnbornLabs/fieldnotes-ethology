# Short Quiz — The White Stork (*Ciconia ciconia*)

1. The white stork migrates mainly by:
   A. Soaring on thermals over land
   B. Continuous flapping across the open sea
   C. Swimming between islands
   D. Nocturnal flapping flight

2. Because thermals form over land and not open sea, migrating storks:
   A. Cross the widest part of the Mediterranean directly
   B. Migrate only at night
   C. Do not migrate at all
   D. Funnel around the sea at narrow points such as Gibraltar

3. The "Pfeilstorch" was important because it:
   A. Proved storks hibernate in mud
   B. Provided early evidence that storks migrate to and from Africa
   C. Showed storks deliver babies
   D. Was the first stork ever ringed

4. The main communicative signal of an adult white stork is:
   A. A loud trumpeting call
   B. Song
   C. Bill-clattering
   D. Silence with no signals at all

5. The belief that storks deliver babies is:
   A. A cultural myth linked to spring, nesting on homes, and parental devotion
   B. A confirmed biological fact
   C. Based on storks carrying prey that looks like infants
   D. A recent invention

6. White storks nest overwhelmingly on:
   A. The ground in open fields
   B. Floating platforms on water
   C. Man-made structures such as chimneys, poles, and platforms
   D. Underground burrows

7. The white stork's IUCN Red List status is:
   A. Endangered
   B. Critically Endangered
   C. Least Concern
   D. Extinct in the Wild

8. Brood reduction in white storks refers to:
   A. Reducing the number of eggs laid in advance
   B. Parents abandoning the whole brood
   C. Chicks leaving the nest early
   D. The loss of the smallest chicks when food is scarce

9. Compared with the grey heron, the white stork typically:
   A. Hunts by motionless ambush at the water's edge
   B. Flies with its neck retracted in an S-shape
   C. Walks through open ground and soars on thermals to migrate
   D. Is a resident, non-migratory bird

10. Successful, efficient migration in white storks appears to depend partly on:
   A. Purely fixed instinct with no role for experience
   B. Experience, with young birds benefiting from older ones
   C. Following ocean currents
   D. Magnetic cues alone, with no other factors

## Answer Key

1. A
2. D
3. B
4. C
5. A
6. C
7. C
8. D
9. C
10. B
