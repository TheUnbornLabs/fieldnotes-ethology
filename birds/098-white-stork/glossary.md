# Glossary — The White Stork (*Ciconia ciconia*)

**Ciconiidae** — the stork family, to which the white stork belongs.
**Soaring flight** — flight in which a bird gains height in rising air and glides, using little flapping; central to white stork migration.
**Thermal** — a rising column of warm air formed by the sun heating the ground, used by soaring birds to gain height.
**Migration bottleneck** — a narrow geographic point, such as the Strait of Gibraltar, where soaring migrants concentrate to avoid long sea crossings.
**Pfeilstorch** — literally "arrow stork"; a white stork found in Europe carrying an African arrow, which provided early evidence of long-distance migration.
**Bill-clattering** — the mechanical sound made by a stork rapidly snapping its mandibles together, its main communicative signal.
**Nest-site fidelity** — the tendency to return to and reuse the same nest in successive years.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which a male and female form a pair bond, in the white stork generally within a breeding season.
**Brood reduction** — the loss of one or more chicks, usually the smallest, when food is insufficient to raise the whole brood.
**Regurgitation** — bringing up partly digested food from the crop or stomach to feed young at the nest.
**Colonial nesting** — nesting close together in groups, as storks often do on nearby structures.
**Gregarious** — tending to gather in flocks; white storks are gregarious outside the breeding season.
**Carnivore** — an animal that feeds on other animals; the white stork's diet is entirely animal prey.
**Least Concern** — an IUCN Red List category indicating a species is not currently at high risk of extinction.
