# Glossary — The Satin Bowerbird (*Ptilonorhynchus violaceus*)

**Bower** — the structure built by a male bowerbird as a courtship arena; in the satin bowerbird, two parallel walls of sticks with a decorated court. It is not a nest.

**Avenue bower** — the bower type of the satin bowerbird, consisting of two parallel stick walls forming a passage or avenue.

**Sexual selection** — selection arising from competition for mates and mate choice, driving the evolution of traits that improve mating success.

**Female choice** — the process by which females select mates, exerting selection on male traits; the central force shaping bowerbird behaviour.

**Polygyny** — a mating system in which a male may mate with several females; the satin bowerbird's effective mating system, with strong skew in male success.

**Uniparental care** — care of young by only one parent; in the satin bowerbird, provided by the female alone.

**Altricial** — describing chicks that hatch helpless, blind, and dependent, requiring prolonged parental care, as bowerbird nestlings do.

**Delayed plumage maturation** — the acquisition of adult plumage only after several years; male satin bowerbirds wear green, female-like plumage for years before turning blue.

**Vocal mimicry** — the imitation of other species' sounds; satin bowerbirds mimic other birds and environmental sounds during display.

**Decoration** — an object collected and placed by a male at his bower; satin bowerbirds strongly favour blue objects.

**Bower painting** — the application of chewed, pigmented plant matter to the bower walls by the male, sometimes using a wad of material as an applicator.

**Bower raiding** — the theft of decorations and destruction of a rival male's bower by another male.

**Court** — the cleared, decorated ground around and within the bower where display takes place.

**Frugivore** — an animal that eats mainly fruit; the satin bowerbird is predominantly frugivorous.

**Runaway selection** — a self-reinforcing evolutionary dynamic in which a female preference and a male trait evolve together, invoked to explain extreme sexually selected traits.
