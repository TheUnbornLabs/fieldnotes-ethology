# Quiz — The Satin Bowerbird (*Ptilonorhynchus violaceus*)

1. Parental care in the satin bowerbird is provided by:
   A. The male alone
   B. Both parents equally
   C. A group of helpers
   D. The female alone

2. Bower-building and display in satin bowerbirds are best described as:
   A. Fixed instinct with no learning
   B. Learned skills that improve with experience
   C. Copied exactly from the female
   D. Present only in captivity

3. The bower built by a male satin bowerbird is:
   A. The nest where eggs are laid
   B. A winter shelter
   C. A courtship arena, not a nest
   D. A food store

4. During courtship, satin bowerbird males are notable for:
   A. Vocal mimicry and diverse display sounds
   B. Silent display only
   C. Building the nest for the female
   D. Feeding the female fish

5. Satin bowerbird males show a strong preference for collecting decorations that are:
   A. Red
   B. Blue
   C. Yellow
   D. White

6. Young male satin bowerbirds:
   A. Have adult blue plumage from their first year
   B. Are indistinguishable from adult males
   C. Wear green, female-like plumage for several years
   D. Never build bowers

7. Male satin bowerbirds "paint" the bower walls using:
   A. Chewed, pigmented plant matter
   B. Coloured stones
   C. Their own feathers
   D. Mud only

8. Bower raiding refers to:
   A. Predators eating the chicks
   B. Females rejecting a male
   C. Humans removing bowers
   D. Rival males stealing decorations and wrecking bowers

9. The satin bowerbird's mating system is best described as:
   A. Lifelong monogamy
   B. Polygyny with strong skew in male success
   C. Cooperative breeding
   D. Random pairing

10. The IUCN Red List status of the satin bowerbird is:
   A. Endangered
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Near Threatened
   D. Least Concern

## Answer Key

1. D
2. B
3. C
4. A
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. D
9. B
10. D
