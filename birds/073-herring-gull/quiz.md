# Short Quiz

1. Herring gulls originally bred mainly: a) In cities  b) On remote cliffs and islands  c) In deserts  d) Underground

2. Herring gull cognition includes: a) No problem-solving ability  b) Only fixed instinct  c) Problem-solving and individual recognition  d) Human language comprehension

3. The herring gull diet is best described as: a) Omnivorous and opportunistic  b) Only fish  c) Only scavenged human waste  d) Only crustaceans

4. In Tinbergen's classic experiments, chick pecking was released by: a) The parent's overall shape  b) The parent's call  c) The red spot on the adult's bill  d) The nest itself

5. Herring gull chicks are: a) Precocial and independent at hatching  b) Altricial and helpless  c) Semi-precocial  d) Born fully feathered

6. Typical wild lifespan of herring gulls is roughly: a) 10–15 years, occasionally up to ~30  b) 2–3 years  c) 40–50 years  d) Over 60 years

7. A major driver of some regional herring gull declines is: a) Predation by owls  b) Changes in food availability and human persecution  c) Volcanic activity  d) Excess rainfall

8. Herring gulls are native to: a) Only Europe  b) Northern Hemisphere coasts and inland waters  c) The Southern Hemisphere  d) Tropical regions only

9. Compared with rural birds, urban herring gulls typically show: a) Greater fear of humans  b) Complete avoidance of people  c) Reduced fear and increased boldness  d) No behavioral difference

10. Herring gulls typically breed in: a) Solitary isolated pairs  b) Pairs that never aggregate  c) Underground colonies  d) Colonies, from small groups to large aggregations

**Answer Key:** 1-b, 2-c, 3-a, 4-c, 5-c, 6-a, 7-b, 8-b, 9-c, 10-d
