# References / Further Reading

## Well-established sources

- Tinbergen, N. (1953). *The Herring Gull's World*. Collins. (Classic ethological study of herring gull behavior, including the red-spot begging work.)
- Cramp, S., & Simmons, K. E. L. (Eds.). *The Birds of the Western Palearctic*. Oxford University Press. (Standard reference for distribution, biology, and status.)
- BirdLife International / IUCN Red List assessment for *Larus argentatus* (Least Concern, with regional declines). (Conservation status.)

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- IUCN Red List status: **Least Concern** (global), with marked regional declines in parts of Europe. Confirmed (IUCN Red List / BirdLife DataZone; Wikipedia "European herring gull"). Book status correct.
- Weight: adults typically ~0.75–1.25 kg (Wikipedia; Animal Diversity Web). Book's stated range (0.7–1.7 kg) is a little wide on the upper end; the lower/typical figures are correct.
- Lifespan: commonly ~10–15 years in the wild; ringed individuals to ~30+ years. Book verified.
- Goumas et al. (2019): confirmed full citation — Goumas, M., Burns, I., Kelley, L. A., & Boogert, N. J. (2019). "Herring gulls respond to human gaze direction." *Biology Letters*, 15(8), 20190405. DOI: 10.1098/rsbl.2019.0405.
- Scientific name *Larus argentatus* and Tinbergen red-spot begging work: verified (widely documented classic ethology).
