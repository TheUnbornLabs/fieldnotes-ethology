# Glossary — The Eurasian Eagle-Owl (*Bubo bubo*)

- **Apex predator** — a predator at the top of its food web, not normally preyed upon by other animals once adult.
- **Nocturnal** — active mainly at night.
- **Crepuscular** — active mainly in twilight, at dusk and dawn.
- **Ear tufts** — the upright feather tufts on an owl's head, used in display and camouflage; not ears and not involved in hearing.
- **Facial disc** — the concave ruff of stiff feathers framing an owl's face that helps gather and direct sound toward the ears.
- **Territorial hoot** — the deep, far-carrying call ("ooh-hu") used to advertise ownership of a territory and to communicate with a mate.
- **Talons** — the curved claws on a raptor's toes, used to seize and kill prey; the eagle-owl's deliver a crushing grip.
- **Perch hunting** — an ambush hunting style in which the bird watches from an elevated perch and drops onto prey, rather than searching in continuous flight.
- **Low-light vision** — vision adapted to see in very dim conditions, aided by large eyes rich in light-sensitive rod cells.
- **Pair bond** — the lasting cooperative association between two mates, often maintained over multiple years in eagle-owls.
- **Nest-site fidelity** — the tendency to return to and reuse the same nesting site or territory across breeding seasons.
- **Intraguild predation** — predation on another predator that would otherwise be a competitor for shared prey.
- **Pellet** — a compacted mass of indigestible fur, feathers, and bone that an owl regurgitates after digesting prey.
- **Dispersal** — the movement of young birds away from where they were raised to establish territories of their own.
