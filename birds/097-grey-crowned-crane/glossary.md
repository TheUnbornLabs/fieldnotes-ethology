# Glossary — The Grey Crowned Crane (*Balearica regulorum*)

**Gruidae** — the crane family, to which the grey crowned crane belongs.
**Gruiformes** — the order of birds that includes cranes, rails, and their relatives.
**Basal** — describing a lineage that branches off early in a group's evolutionary tree and retains ancestral traits; the crowned cranes are the most basal living cranes.
**Precocial** — describing young that hatch well-developed, downy, and able to move and feed soon after hatching, as crane chicks do.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which a male and female form an exclusive pair bond, often lasting across breeding seasons.
**Pair bond** — the enduring social and reproductive relationship between two mated birds.
**Gular sac / wattle** — the throat pouch or fleshy hanging structure; the grey crowned crane's inflatable red gular sac aids its booming call.
**Dancing** — the coordinated bowing, leaping, wing-spreading, and object-tossing displays of cranes, used in pair bonding and social interaction.
**Stamping** — a foraging behavior in which a crane stamps its feet to flush insects and other small prey from vegetation.
**Roost** — a site, such as shallow water, a reedbed, or a tree, where birds gather to rest, especially at night.
**Dispersive** — moving locally in response to conditions such as rainfall and food, rather than undertaking regular long-distance migration.
**Omnivorous** — feeding on both plant and animal material.
**Territory** — an area a breeding pair defends around its nest.
**Endangered** — an IUCN Red List category indicating a species faces a very high risk of extinction in the wild.
