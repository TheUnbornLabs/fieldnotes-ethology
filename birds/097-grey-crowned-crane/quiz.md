# Short Quiz — The Grey Crowned Crane (*Balearica regulorum*)

1. The grey crowned crane is assessed by the IUCN as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Near Threatened
   C. Endangered
   D. Extinct in the Wild

2. Compared with "typical" cranes, crowned cranes are unusual in being able to:
   A. Perch in trees
   B. Swim underwater
   C. Migrate across oceans
   D. Feed only at night

3. Grey crowned crane dancing occurs:
   A. Only during courtship
   B. Only among males
   C. Throughout the year and at many ages
   D. Only in captivity

4. The grey crowned crane's booming call is produced with the aid of:
   A. A long, coiled windpipe
   B. An inflatable red gular sac at the throat
   C. Its wings
   D. Its golden crown

5. Grey crowned crane chicks are best described as:
   A. Altricial (blind and helpless at hatching)
   B. Precocial (downy and mobile soon after hatching)
   C. Unable to feed themselves for months
   D. Raised only by the male

6. In terms of movement, grey crowned cranes are:
   A. Long-distance transcontinental migrants
   B. Entirely sedentary and never move
   C. Nocturnal migrants only
   D. Largely resident or locally dispersive

7. The main cause of the grey crowned crane's decline is:
   A. Natural predators
   B. Disease alone
   C. Habitat loss and capture for trade
   D. Competition with storks

8. The grey crowned crane is the national bird of:
   A. Kenya
   B. South Africa
   C. Uganda
   D. Nigeria

9. Cranes, storks, and herons share a tall, long-legged form because of:
   A. Very close common ancestry
   B. Recent hybridisation
   C. Identical diets
   D. Convergent adaptation to similar habitats

10. A grey crowned crane foraging behavior that flushes hidden prey is:
   A. Diving
   B. Foot-stamping
   C. Hovering
   D. Bark-stripping

## Answer Key

1. C
2. A
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. D
7. C
8. C
9. D
10. B
