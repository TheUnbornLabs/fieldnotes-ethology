# Glossary — The African Sacred Ibis (*Threskiornis aethiopicus*)

**Threskiornithidae** — the family of ibises and spoonbills, to which the sacred ibis belongs.
**Ibis** — a wading bird with a long, down-curved bill, typically feeding by probing.
**Tactile foraging** — finding and capturing prey by touch rather than by sight, as the sacred ibis does by probing its bill into mud and water.
**Probing** — inserting the bill into a substrate such as mud or soft ground to detect and seize hidden prey.
**Colonial nesting** — breeding in dense groups of nests, often with several species together, as the sacred ibis does.
**Roost** — a communal site, often in trees or on islands, where birds gather to rest, especially at night.
**Gregarious** — tending to gather in flocks and groups; the sacred ibis is gregarious for much of the year.
**Invasive species** — a non-native species that establishes and spreads in a new region, often with harmful effects, as the sacred ibis has in parts of Europe.
**Votive offering** — an object dedicated to a deity; in ancient Egypt, mummified ibises were offered to the god Thoth.
**Thoth** — the ancient Egyptian god of writing, wisdom, and the moon, associated with the sacred ibis.
**Mummification** — preservation of a body; ancient Egyptians mummified sacred ibises in vast numbers as offerings.
**Opportunistic** — taking advantage of whatever food or habitat is available; a defining trait of the sacred ibis.
**Omnivore / carnivore** — an animal that eats a broad range of foods, including animal prey; the sacred ibis is a broad carnivore-omnivore.
**Least Concern** — an IUCN Red List category indicating a species is not currently at high risk of extinction.
