## 3. Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following facts were confirmed against live web sources on 2026-07-02:

- Scientific name *Threskiornis aethiopicus*; family Threskiornithidae; order Pelecaniformes (modern classification; older schemes used Ciconiiformes) — confirmed (Wikipedia; Animal Diversity Web).
- Native to much of sub-Saharan Africa plus small parts of the Middle East (Iraq, Iran, Kuwait); extirpated from Egypt as a wild breeding bird despite ancient prominence (associated with the god Thoth) — confirmed (Wikipedia; ADW).
- Invasive/established in western Europe (notably Atlantic France, from Branféré Zoo stock; first breeding 1993); on the EU Invasive Alien Species of Union Concern list since 2016 — confirmed (Wikipedia; British Birds 2017; ResearchGate).
- IUCN Red List status **Least Concern** across native range — confirmed (BirdLife DataZone; ADW; SeaWorld).
