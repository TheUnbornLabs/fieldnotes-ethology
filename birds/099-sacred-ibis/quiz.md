# Short Quiz — The African Sacred Ibis (*Threskiornis aethiopicus*)

1. The sacred ibis feeds mainly by:
   A. Diving for fish from the air
   B. Watching for prey and striking like a heron
   C. Probing its bill into mud and water to find prey by touch
   D. Grazing on aquatic plants

2. In ancient Egypt the sacred ibis was associated with the god:
   A. Ra
   B. Osiris
   C. Horus
   D. Thoth

3. Across its native sub-Saharan African range, the sacred ibis is assessed by the IUCN as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Endangered
   D. Critically Endangered

4. In parts of Europe, the sacred ibis is regarded as:
   A. A protected native species
   B. An invasive species subject to control
   C. Extinct
   D. A recent natural colonist welcomed by conservationists

5. The sacred ibis's diet is best described as:
   A. Strictly vegetarian
   B. Fish only
   C. Broad and opportunistic, including eggs and chicks of other birds
   D. Seeds and grain only

6. The sacred ibis nests:
   A. Colonially, often with other waterbirds
   B. Alone in isolated territories
   C. In underground burrows
   D. On the open sea

7. Today, wild breeding sacred ibises in Egypt are:
   A. As abundant as in ancient times
   B. The largest population in the world
   C. Found only in the Nile delta
   D. Essentially absent

8. The sacred ibis belongs to the same family as the:
   A. Herons
   B. Storks
   C. Spoonbills
   D. Cranes

9. Ancient Egyptians are known to have prepared sacred ibises as:
   A. Mummified votive offerings in vast numbers
   B. Food for royalty only
   C. Hunting decoys
   D. Messenger birds

10. The sacred ibis's cognitive hallmark is best described as:
   A. Advanced tool use
   B. Ecological flexibility and opportunism
   C. Complex song learning
   D. Purely fixed instinct

## Answer Key

1. C
2. D
3. A
4. B
5. C
6. A
7. D
8. C
9. A
10. B
