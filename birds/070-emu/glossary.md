# Book 070 — The Emu: Glossary

**Biparental care** — parental care provided by both parents; in emus, males provide most/all care.

**Brooding** — sitting on eggs or chicks to provide heat and care.

**Clutch** — the set of eggs laid in one breeding bout, typically 5–15 for emus.

**Flightless** — unable to fly due to reduction of wing musculature and skeletal modifications.

**Flock/Mob** — a group of emus.

**Incubation** — brooding eggs to maintain temperature and humidity until hatching.

**Monogamy** — a mating system where one male mates with one female.

**Nomadic** — moving from place to place following food and water; non-stationary.

**Precocial** — of young birds, mobile and able to forage independently at hatching.

**Ratite** — a large, flightless bird (ostriches, emus, rheas, kiwis, cassowaries).

**Vigilance** — watchfulness for predators and threats.
