## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Dromaius novaehollandiae*; ratite (Palaeognathae), order Casuariiformes (with the cassowaries) — confirmed.
- **IUCN Red List status: Least Concern** — confirmed, stable population across most of its range (IUCN Red List / BirdLife DataZone).
- **Second-largest living bird by height** (after the ostrich) and Australia's largest bird — confirmed.
- **Running speed** — the book states emus "can reach 50 km/h," which is accurate: sources give the emu's sprint speed as ~48 km/h (30 mph, Britannica/Wikipedia) up to ~50 km/h.
- Body-size figures (40–60 kg, 1.5–2 m tall) are consistent with standard accounts, though many sources cap typical height nearer 1.9 m.
