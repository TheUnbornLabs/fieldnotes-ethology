# Book 053 — The New Caledonian Crow: Glossary

- **Tool manufacture** — Making a tool by modifying an object rather than using a found object as it is; the New Caledonian crow shapes hooked stick tools and barbed Pandanus tools before use.
- **Hooked tool** — A stick tool in which the crow sculpts a small functional hook, often from a twig fork, to snag or drag prey out of holes.
- **Pandanus tool** — A tapering, naturally barbed strip cut from the serrated edge of a Pandanus leaf and used to rake grubs from crevices; its removal leaves a characteristic stepped cut on the leaf.
- **Meta-tool use** — Using one tool to obtain a second tool that is then used on the food; a multi-step, sequential form of tool use suggesting planning across steps.
- **Aesop's fable task** — An experimental test in which an animal must raise a floating reward by dropping objects into water, based on the fable of the crow and the pitcher.
- **Causal reasoning** — Understanding cause-and-effect relationships, such as that dropping stones raises water level; evidenced in this crow but its extent is actively debated.
- **Trap-tube task** — A test in which extracting food the wrong way causes it to fall into a trap, used to probe whether an animal grasps physical consequences.
- **Cumulative culture** — Culture in which socially transmitted behaviors accumulate improvements over generations; whether this crow shows it is debated.
- **Cultural transmission** — The passing of behaviors between individuals, often from parent to young, by social learning rather than genetic inheritance.
- **Laterality** — A consistent side bias; New Caledonian crows tend to hold and work their tools with an individually consistent tool-side preference.
- **Innovation** — The production of a novel behavior or solution not previously in an individual's repertoire.
- **Extended parental care** — A prolonged period of dependence of young on their parents, providing the developmental window for acquiring difficult tool skills.
- **Neophobia** — Wariness of unfamiliar objects or situations, which can trade off against a crow's exploratory tool behavior.
- **Pallium** — The forebrain region of birds functionally comparable to the mammalian cortex; in corvids it is densely packed with neurons.
- **Convergent evolution** — The independent evolution of similar traits, here tool manufacture, in unrelated lineages such as this crow and the chimpanzee.
