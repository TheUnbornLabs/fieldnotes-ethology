# Short Quiz — The Great Hornbill (*Buceros bicornis*)

1. During breeding, the female great hornbill:
   A. Builds an open nest in the canopy
   B. Seals herself into a tree cavity, fed by the male through a slit
   C. Shares incubation equally at an open nest
   D. Lays eggs on the ground

2. The great hornbill's diet is dominated by:
   A. Fish
   B. Nectar
   C. Fruit, especially figs
   D. Seeds cracked from cones

3. The casque on top of the hornbill's bill is best described as:
   A. A solid, heavy weapon
   B. A food-storage organ
   C. A tool for digging
   D. Large but mostly hollow, probably used in display and possibly resonance

4. The great hornbill is called a "gardener of the forest" because it:
   A. Swallows fruit whole and disperses intact seeds far from the parent tree
   B. Plants seeds with its bill
   C. Prunes branches
   D. Builds nests that shelter seedlings

5. The great hornbill's sealed-nest strategy is best understood as:
   A. The male cruelly imprisoning the female
   B. A cooperative, evolved strategy protecting the female and brood
   C. An accident of nest-building
   D. A defence against weather only

6. The loud whooshing sound as a great hornbill flies past is:
   A. Its main call
   B. Made by the casque
   C. A warning song
   D. Produced mechanically by air through the wing feathers

7. On the IUCN Red List, the great hornbill is assessed as:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Extinct
   C. Vulnerable
   D. Data Deficient

8. The great hornbill's pair bond is:
   A. A strong, apparently long-term monogamous bond
   B. Absent, with promiscuous mating
   C. Formed only for a single day
   D. Based on a large group

9. The great hornbill's breeding is characterised by:
   A. Many young raised quickly each year
   B. Few young raised slowly with very heavy investment
   C. No parental care
   D. Communal group nesting

10. The resemblance between hornbills and toucans is an example of:
   A. Close relatedness
   B. Mimicry
   C. Coincidental identical genes
   D. Convergent evolution in unrelated lineages

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. D
4. A
5. B
6. D
7. C
8. A
9. B
10. D
