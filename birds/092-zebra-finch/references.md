## 1. Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List assessment for the zebra finch — global conservation status and range. Note: following a 2016 BirdLife/IUCN revision the Australian zebra finch is widely treated as *Taeniopygia castanotis* (Least Concern), split from the Sunda zebra finch *Taeniopygia guttata* (also Least Concern); older literature uses *T. guttata* for the Australian bird.
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology — Birds of the World species account for the zebra finch.

## 3. Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following facts were confirmed against live web sources on 2026-07-02:

- Family Estrildidae (grassfinches/waxbills); order Passeriformes — confirmed (Wikipedia; Avibase; Animal Diversity Web).
- Taxonomy: 2016 BirdLife/IUCN split — Australian bird = *Taeniopygia castanotis* (full species), Sunda/Timor bird = *Taeniopygia guttata*; previously one species; older literature uses *T. guttata* for the Australian bird — confirmed (Wikipedia "Australian zebra finch"; Avibase; BirdLife DataZone).
- IUCN Red List status **Least Concern** for both the Australian and Sunda species — confirmed (Animal Diversity Web; BirdLife DataZone).
