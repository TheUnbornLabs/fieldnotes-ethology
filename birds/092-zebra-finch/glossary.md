# Book 092 — The Zebra Finch: Glossary

**Estrildidae** — the family of estrildid finches (waxbills and grassfinches), to which the zebra finch belongs.

**Grassfinch** — a group of small, seed-eating Australian and Old World finches within the Estrildidae.

**Vocal learning** — the ability to acquire vocalisations by imitation and practice rather than producing them innately; found in only a few animal groups, including oscine songbirds and humans.

**Oscine** — a "true songbird," a member of the large group of passerines in which learned song is widespread.

**Song system / song-control nuclei** — the network of interconnected brain regions in songbirds involved in learning and producing song; exceptionally well mapped in the zebra finch.

**Sensitive period** — a limited window early in life during which song learning is most effective.

**Tutor** — the individual (usually the father in zebra finches) whose song a young male learns.

**Template** — the memorised model of the tutor's song against which a young bird shapes its own developing song.

**Subsong / plastic song** — early, variable phases of song practice preceding the stable adult form.

**Crystallisation** — the process by which a young male's practised song stabilises into its fixed adult form.

**Socially monogamous** — forming a lasting pair bond, which may not equal strict genetic monogamy.

**Allopreening** — mutual preening between individuals, common in bonded zebra finch pairs.

**Clumping** — bonded birds sitting in close body contact.

**Altricial** — hatching helpless, naked, and blind, requiring extended parental care.

**Opportunistic breeder** — a species that breeds in response to favourable conditions rather than a fixed season.

**Model organism** — a species studied intensively because it is tractable and reveals general biological principles.
