# Book 092 — The Zebra Finch: Quiz

1. Male zebra finch song is:
   A. Fully innate from hatching
   B. Produced only by females
   C. Learned from a tutor during a sensitive period
   D. Identical in every individual

2. The zebra finch belongs to which family?
   A. Estrildidae (estrildid finches)
   B. Fringillidae (true finches)
   C. Passeridae (Old World sparrows)
   D. Corvidae (crows)

3. The zebra finch is native to:
   A. North America
   B. Mainland Australia and some islands
   C. Europe
   D. Antarctica

4. The "song system" in the zebra finch refers to:
   A. Its digestive tract
   B. A type of flocking formation
   C. Its migratory route
   D. A well-mapped network of brain regions for learning and producing song

5. Zebra finch breeding is best described as:
   A. Strictly tied to a fixed calendar season
   B. Opportunistic, responding to favourable conditions such as rain
   C. Occurring only once in a lifetime
   D. Independent of environmental conditions

6. In the zebra finch, learned courtship song is produced by:
   A. Both sexes equally
   B. Females only
   C. Males
   D. Only juveniles

7. A single zebra finch kept alone is best described as:
   A. Facing a welfare concern because the species is highly social
   B. Perfectly content
   C. More likely to sing correctly
   D. Unaffected by the absence of other finches

8. The zebra finch's scientific fame rests mainly on:
   A. Tool use
   B. General problem-solving intelligence
   C. Vocal learning and its neural basis
   D. Long-distance migration

9. The zebra finch's main natural diet is:
   A. Fish
   B. Grass seeds
   C. Nectar only
   D. Large insects only

10. Compared with the zebra finch's developmental song learning, the canary is noted for:
    A. Not singing at all
    B. Learning song only from zebra finches
    C. Having no song-control brain regions
    D. Adult song plasticity and seasonal brain changes

## Answer Key

1. C
2. A
3. B
4. D
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. C
9. B
10. D
