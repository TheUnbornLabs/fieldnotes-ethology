# Glossary

**Altricial** — of chicks, helpless at hatching and requiring extended parental care.
**Aptenodytes forsteri** — the scientific name of the emperor penguin.
**Biparental care** — a system in which both parents contribute to raising offspring.
**Brood pouch** — a fold of abdominal skin on the incubating emperor penguin under which the egg (and later small chick) is held against the feet.
**Crèche** — a group of chicks from multiple families that gather together for warmth and protection while adults forage.
**Fast ice** — sea ice that is anchored to the coastline or grounded features, providing the stable platform on which emperors breed.
**Fasting** — an extended period without feeding; incubating male emperors fast for roughly four months.
**Fledging** — the stage at which a young bird completes development and leaves the colony to become independent.
**Huddling** — the dense aggregation of penguins that conserves heat and reduces individual energy expenditure in extreme cold.
**Molt** — the shedding and replacement of feathers.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which one male pairs with one female; in emperors, typically within a season with variable between-year fidelity.
**Pelagic** — living primarily on the open ocean.
**Phenology** — the timing of seasonal biological events such as breeding.
**Pursuit diving** — capturing prey by actively swimming after it underwater.
**Thermoregulation** — the maintenance of stable body temperature.
**Vocal recognition** — identifying individuals (mate or chick) by individually distinctive calls.
