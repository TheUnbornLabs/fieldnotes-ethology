# Book 095 — The Barn Swallow: Quiz

1. The barn swallow belongs to which family?
   A. Hirundinidae (swallows and martins)
   B. Apodidae (swifts)
   C. Fringillidae (finches)
   D. Turdidae (thrushes)

2. The barn swallow feeds mainly by:
   A. Cracking seeds on the ground
   B. Diving for fish
   C. Probing mud for worms
   D. Catching flying insects on the wing

3. The old belief that barn swallows hibernate in pond mud over winter is:
   A. True
   B. False — they are long-distance migrants
   C. True only for northern populations
   D. Unstudied

4. The barn swallow's tail streamers have been studied, especially by Anders Pape Møller, as:
   A. A cooling structure
   B. A possible sexually selected signal of male quality
   C. Purely a flight adaptation with no signalling role
   D. A camouflage device

5. The scientific status of the tail-streamer sexual-selection interpretation is:
   A. A fully settled, undisputed fact
   B. Completely disproven
   C. Influential and much-studied, but debated
   D. Never investigated

6. Barn swallows characteristically build their nests from:
   A. Woven grass domes in trees
   B. Sticks in cliff crevices
   C. No nest at all
   D. Mud pellets attached to structures such as barns and bridges

7. The barn swallow's breeding range and distribution are best described as:
   A. Among the most widespread of any passerine, nearly cosmopolitan
   B. Restricted to one small region
   C. Limited to Australia
   D. Confined to islands

8. A major source of stress and mortality for barn swallows is:
   A. Competition for seeds
   B. Weather that suppresses flying insects, and the hazards of migration
   C. Lack of drinking water only
   D. Excessive sunlight

9. Compared with the barn swallow, the common swift:
   A. Is a close relative in the same family
   B. Feeds only on the ground
   C. Is even more aerial and is not closely related (an example of convergence)
   D. Does not migrate

10. Barn swallows are socially monogamous, which means:
    A. They form pair bonds but may engage in extra-pair matings
    B. They never mate outside the pair
    C. Only males provide care
    D. They breed only once in a lifetime

## Answer Key

1. A
2. D
3. B
4. B
5. C
6. D
7. A
8. B
9. C
10. A
