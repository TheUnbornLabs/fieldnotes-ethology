# Book 095 — The Barn Swallow: Glossary

**Hirundinidae** — the family of swallows and martins, to which the barn swallow belongs.

**Aerial insectivore** — a bird that catches and eats flying insects on the wing.

**Migration** — the seasonal movement between breeding and wintering areas; the barn swallow is a long-distance migrant.

**Site fidelity** — the tendency to return to the same breeding area or nest across years.

**Sexual selection** — selection arising from competition for mates and mate choice, proposed to act on traits such as the barn swallow's tail streamers.

**Tail streamers** — the elongated outer tail feathers of the barn swallow, longer in males, studied as a possible sexually selected signal.

**Socially monogamous** — forming a pair bond for breeding, which may not equal strict genetic monogamy.

**Extra-pair mating** — mating outside the social pair bond, resulting in offspring not sired by the social mate.

**Colonial breeding** — nesting in groups of multiple pairs, as barn swallows often do on shared structures.

**Communal roost** — a site where large numbers gather to sleep, formed by swallows especially before and during migration.

**Mobbing** — collective harassment of a predator by several birds to drive it off.

**Altricial** — hatching helpless, nearly naked, and blind, requiring extended parental care.

**Convergent evolution** — the independent evolution of similar traits or lifestyles in unrelated lineages, as in swallows and swifts.

**Commensal (with humans)** — closely associated with and benefiting from human structures or activity, as the barn swallow does for nesting.

**Bolus** — a compressed mass of many insects that a parent carries to feed nestlings.
