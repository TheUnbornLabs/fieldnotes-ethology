# Quiz — Book 078: The Greylag Goose

1. The Greylag Goose is historically important in ethology mainly as the study animal of:
   A. Charles Darwin
   B. Konrad Lorenz
   C. Niko Tinbergen
   D. B. F. Skinner

2. Imprinting is best described as:
   A. An instant, indiscriminate bond to the first thing seen, unique to Lorenz's discovery
   B. Rapid learning during a sensitive period, with predispositions, described before Lorenz and studied by him
   C. A purely genetic behavior with no learning
   D. A behavior seen only in captivity

3. Greylag goslings are:
   A. Naked, blind, and helpless at hatching
   B. Downy, mobile, and able to feed themselves within a day or so
   C. Unable to leave the nest for weeks
   D. Fed crop milk by both parents

4. Long-term studies of marked Greylags show that flocks are:
   A. Random, unstructured crowds
   B. Made up of pairs and families with alliances and social structure
   C. Ruled by a single permanent leader
   D. Composed of unrelated solitary birds

5. The "triumph ceremony" functions mainly in:
   A. Catching prey
   B. Cooling the body
   C. Pair bonding and reaffirming the bond after aggression
   D. Navigation

6. Greylag pair bonds are best described as:
   A. Absent; geese are promiscuous
   B. Strong and often lifelong, but not unbreakable
   C. Lasting exactly one season only
   D. Formed only in captivity

7. Young Greylag Geese learn migration routes chiefly by:
   A. Innate knowledge requiring no experience
   B. Travelling with their experienced parents
   C. Following unrelated adults at random
   D. Trial and error alone as adults

8. During the summer moult, adult Greylags are:
   A. More aggressive than usual and highly mobile
   B. Flightless and especially cautious, staying near water
   C. Unable to feed
   D. Solitary and silent

9. The Greylag Goose is the wild ancestor of:
   A. The domestic chicken
   B. The domestic duck
   C. Most domestic geese
   D. The turkey

10. The V-formation of flying geese is best explained as:
   A. A device for one permanent leader to command the flock
   B. An aerodynamic and coordinating arrangement in which position changes
   C. A purely decorative pattern
   D. A response to the moon

## Answer Key

1. B
2. B
3. B
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. B
8. B
9. C
10. B
