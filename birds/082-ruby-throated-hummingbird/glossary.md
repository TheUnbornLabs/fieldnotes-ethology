# Glossary — The Ruby-throated Hummingbird (*Archilochus colubris*)

**Torpor** — a controlled, deep reduction in body temperature and metabolic rate, used overnight or in food shortage to save energy.
**Hovering** — sustained stationary flight in still air, achieved by a figure-eight wingbeat generating lift on both strokes.
**Gorget** — the iridescent throat patch of a male hummingbird, a structural-colour signal used in display.
**Iridescence (structural colour)** — colour produced by microscopic structure that reflects light at particular angles, rather than by pigment.
**Trap-lining** — foraging along a repeated circuit of scattered flowers, revisiting each as it refills, instead of defending one patch.
**Territoriality** — defence of a resource such as a rich nectar source against rivals when it is economically worth guarding.
**Nectarivore** — an animal that feeds mainly on nectar.
**Metabolic rate** — the rate at which an animal uses energy; hummingbirds have among the highest known for their mass.
**Coevolution** — reciprocal evolutionary change between interacting species, as between hummingbirds and the flowers they pollinate.
**Altricial** — hatching naked, blind, and helpless, needing extended parental care.
**Pendulum (dive) display** — the male's arcing courtship flight in front of a female, combining gorget flash and display sounds.
**Pre-migratory fattening** — the deposition of fat before migration to fuel long flights, including over-water crossings.
**Promiscuous mating system** — a system with no pair bond, in which males and females associate only to mate.
**Ultraviolet vision** — the ability to see ultraviolet light, contributing to how hummingbirds perceive flowers and plumage.
