# Short Quiz — The Ruby-throated Hummingbird (*Archilochus colubris*)

1. The ruby-throated hummingbird's ability to hover comes from:
   A. Gliding on thermals
   B. A figure-eight wingbeat generating lift on both strokes
   C. Beating the wings only on the downstroke
   D. Holding still in updrafts

2. Nectar provides sugar but not protein, so hummingbirds also eat:
   A. Small insects and spiders
   B. Seeds
   C. Fruit pulp
   D. Fish

3. Torpor in hummingbirds is:
   A. A daytime feeding frenzy
   B. A migratory flock behaviour
   C. A form of moult
   D. A controlled deep drop in body temperature and metabolism to save energy

4. Trap-lining refers to:
   A. Guarding a single feeder aggressively
   B. Catching insects in spider webs
   C. Following a repeated circuit of scattered flowers, revisiting as they refill
   D. Sleeping in the air

5. The male's iridescent throat patch is called the:
   A. Crest
   B. Wattle
   C. Gorget
   D. Speculum

6. All parental care in the ruby-throated hummingbird is provided by:
   A. The male alone
   B. Both parents equally
   C. A group of helpers
   D. The female alone

7. Whether a hummingbird defends a nectar source depends mainly on:
   A. The bird's mood
   B. Whether the resource is economically worth guarding
   C. The time of year only
   D. The bird's age only

8. On migration, many ruby-throated hummingbirds:
   A. Cross the Gulf of Mexico under their own power, fuelled by stored fat
   B. Ride on the backs of geese
   C. Walk along the coastline
   D. Stay in one place year-round

9. Hummingbird foraging efficiency depends heavily on:
   A. Echolocation
   B. A poor sense of colour
   C. Spatial and temporal memory of flowers
   D. Following other species

10. Compared with hummingbirds, swifts (their close relatives) are:
   A. Slow hovering nectar-feeders
   B. Fast forward-flying aerial insectivores that are highly social
   C. Ground-foraging seed-eaters
   D. Solitary flightless birds

## Answer Key

1. B
2. A
3. D
4. C
5. C
6. D
7. B
8. A
9. C
10. B
