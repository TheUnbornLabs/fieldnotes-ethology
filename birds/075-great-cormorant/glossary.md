# Glossary

**Altricial** — of chicks, helpless at hatching and requiring extended parental care.
**Colony** — a breeding aggregation of many nesting pairs.
**Foot-propelled diving** — swimming underwater using the webbed feet for propulsion, as in cormorants.
**Habituation** — a decline in response to a repeated, harmless stimulus; relevant to trained fishing cormorants' tolerance of humans.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which individuals form pair bonds, in cormorants typically during the breeding season.
**Phalacrocorax carbo** — the scientific name of the great cormorant.
**Pursuit diving** — chasing prey underwater using body propulsion.
**Regurgitation** — bringing up partially digested food to feed young.
**Spread-wing posture** — the characteristic wing-holding-open behavior cormorants use to dry their wettable plumage after diving.
**Thermoregulation** — the maintenance of stable body temperature.
**Trade-off** — an evolutionary compromise in which improving one trait carries a cost to another, as with wettable feathers aiding diving but reducing insulation.
**Ukai** — the traditional Japanese practice of fishing with trained cormorants.
**Wettable plumage** — feathers that absorb water, reducing buoyancy and aiding diving but requiring subsequent drying.
