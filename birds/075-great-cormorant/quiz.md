# Short Quiz

1. Compared with most seabirds, cormorant plumage is: a) Fully waterproof  b) Partly wettable, reducing buoyancy  c) Absent  d) Coated in heavy oil

2. Great cormorant dives commonly reach depths of about: a) 2–3 meters  b) 10 meters  c) 20+ meters routinely  d) They do not dive

3. Traditional cormorant fishing is most associated with: a) Europe only  b) North America  c) East Asia (e.g., Japan and China)  d) South America

4. Great cormorant chicks at hatching are: a) Altricial and helpless  b) Precocial  c) Semi-precocial  d) Independent immediately

5. The typical great cormorant clutch is: a) 1–2 eggs  b) 5 or more eggs  c) No fixed number  d) 3–4 eggs

6. Great cormorants inhabit: a) Only saltwater  b) Only tropical regions  c) Both coastal and freshwater environments  d) The Northern Hemisphere only

7. The cormorant's primary hunting method is: a) Surface filter-feeding  b) Underwater pursuit diving  c) Aerial plunge-diving  d) Cooperative herding

8. The characteristic spread-wing posture in cormorants functions mainly to: a) Attract mates  b) Threaten rivals  c) Dry the wettable plumage after diving  d) Cool the feet

9. Because cormorant feathers are wettable, the main trade-off is: a) Faster flight  b) Better insulation  c) Enhanced diving at the cost of insulation and drying time  d) Improved eyesight

10. A significant modern threat to cormorants in many regions is: a) No threats at all  b) Only habitat loss  c) Only hunting  d) Human persecution and culling over perceived competition with fisheries

**Answer Key:** 1-b, 2-b, 3-c, 4-a, 5-d, 6-c, 7-b, 8-c, 9-c, 10-d
