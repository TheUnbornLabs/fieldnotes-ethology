# Glossary — The Bald Eagle

- **Sea-eagle** — a member of the genus *Haliaeetus*, large fish-eating eagles associated with coasts, rivers, and lakes.
- **Kleptoparasitism** — obtaining food by stealing it from another animal that caught or gathered it.
- **Scavenging** — feeding on dead animals (carrion) rather than killing live prey.
- **Communal roost** — a site, often a stand of large trees, where many individuals gather to rest, especially in winter.
- **Cartwheel display** — a courtship maneuver in which two eagles lock talons in the air and tumble downward together.
- **Spicules** — rough projections on the underside of the toes that improve grip on slippery prey such as fish.
- **Delayed plumage maturation** — taking several years (about five in the bald eagle) to develop full adult plumage.
- **Biomagnification** — the increasing concentration of a persistent toxin at higher levels of a food chain.
- **DDT** — a synthetic pesticide whose breakdown products thinned eggshells in fish-eating birds and drove eagle declines.
- **Delisting** — official removal of a species from an endangered or threatened species list after recovery.
- **Nest fidelity** — the tendency to return to and reuse the same nest across years, enlarging it over time.
- **Talon-locking** — gripping talons with another eagle in flight, seen in courtship and in aerial disputes.
- **Opportunist** — an animal that exploits whatever food or resource is most readily available at the time.
- **Reversed size dimorphism** — the pattern, common in raptors, in which the female is larger than the male.
- **Apex predator** — a predator at the top of its food chain with few or no natural predators of its own.
