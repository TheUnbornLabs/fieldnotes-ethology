# References / Further Reading — The Bald Eagle

Sourcing follows the collection's citation policy: well-established resources are named plainly; specific claims that should be checked against a database are flagged. No citations are invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Cornell Lab of Ornithology — *Birds of the World* account for the bald eagle (*Haliaeetus leucocephalus*), and the associated *All About Birds* species profile.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Haliaeetus leucocephalus* (Least Concern). Confirm the current assessment year on the Red List website.
- U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service — materials on the Bald and Golden Eagle Protection Act, the Endangered Species Act, and the 2007 delisting of the bald eagle.
- Standard ethology and ornithology texts, e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior* (foraging economics and optimal foraging) and Bradbury & Vehrencamp, *Principles of Animal Communication* (signaling and displays).
- The general DDT-and-recovery conservation history — biomagnification and eggshell thinning in fish-eating birds — widely documented in conservation biology texts and government agency accounts.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Haliaeetus leucocephalus*; Accipitriformes / Accipitridae; genus *Haliaeetus* (sea/fish-eagles) — confirmed.
- **IUCN Red List status: Least Concern** — confirmed (IUCN Red List; U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service).
- **2007 ESA delisting** — confirmed: the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service rule removing the bald eagle (lower-48) from the list of threatened and endangered wildlife was published July 9, 2007 and became effective August 8, 2007 (Federal Register 72 FR 37346). Protections under the Bald and Golden Eagle Protection Act and Migratory Bird Treaty Act continue.
- Incubation ~35 days and fledging ~10–12 weeks — consistent with standard accounts (Cornell Birds of the World); precise figures not exhaustively re-checked.
- Benjamin Franklin "bad moral character" quotation (1784 letter) remains flagged for exact wording.
