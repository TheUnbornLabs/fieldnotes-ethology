## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- IUCN Red List status: **Least Concern** — confirmed (IUCN Red List / BirdLife DataZone; Wikipedia "Brown pelican"). Book status correct.
- Conservation history: listed under the US Endangered Species Act 1970–2009; DDT banned in the US in 1972; the species was fully delisted (recovered) on 17 December 2009. Sources: Wikipedia "Brown pelican"; US NPS California Brown Pelican page. Book correctly attributes recovery to the DDT ban and habitat protection; the specific delisting year is 2009 (the book's 1972 refers to the DDT ban, which is correct).
- Wingspan: ~2 m (about 6.5–7 ft), males slightly larger. Sources: Texas Parks & Wildlife; Wikipedia. Book's "2–2.5 m" is slightly generous at the top; ~2 m is the standard figure.
- Scientific name *Pelecanus occidentalis* and plunge-diving specialization: verified.
