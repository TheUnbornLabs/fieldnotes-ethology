# Glossary

**Altricial** — of chicks, helpless at hatching and requiring extended parental care.
**Bioaccumulation** — the buildup of a substance (such as DDT) in an organism over time.
**Biomagnification** — the increase in a contaminant's concentration at successively higher levels of a food chain.
**Colony** — a breeding aggregation of many nesting pairs.
**DDT / DDE** — a synthetic insecticide and its persistent breakdown product, implicated in eggshell thinning in fish-eating birds.
**Eggshell thinning** — a reduction in shell thickness caused by impaired calcium deposition, leading to egg breakage.
**Fledging** — the stage at which a young bird completes development and leaves the nest.
**Gular pouch** — the expandable throat pouch used by pelicans to capture and hold fish and water.
**Monogamy** — a mating system in which individuals form pair bonds, in brown pelicans typically during the breeding season.
**Pelecanus occidentalis** — the scientific name of the brown pelican.
**Plunge-diving** — capturing prey by diving from the air and striking the water at speed.
**Regurgitation** — bringing up partially digested food to feed young.
**Trophic level** — an organism's position in a food chain, relevant to how contaminants concentrate.
