# Quiz — The Superb Lyrebird (*Menura novaehollandiae*)

1. The superb lyrebird is best known for its:
   A. Long migrations
   B. Underwater diving
   C. Exceptional vocal mimicry
   D. Cooperative hunting

2. Widely shared clips of lyrebirds imitating chainsaws and camera shutters are:
   A. Typical of all wild lyrebirds
   B. Largely associated with captive or human-habituated birds and exceptional
   C. Completely fabricated
   D. Produced only by females

3. Parental care in the superb lyrebird is provided by:
   A. The female alone
   B. The male alone
   C. Both parents equally
   D. A group of helpers

4. The superb lyrebird's clutch size is typically:
   A. Six to eight eggs
   B. Four eggs
   C. Two eggs
   D. A single egg

5. Lyrebird mimicry is best described as:
   A. Innate and present from hatching
   B. Learned from the acoustic environment and refined with experience
   C. Random and unstructured
   D. Impossible to change

6. The male lyrebird's ornate tail is:
   A. The organ that produces its song
   B. Used to dig nests
   C. A visual display ornament unrelated to sound production
   D. Used for flight over long distances

7. The superb lyrebird's mating system is:
   A. Lifelong monogamy
   B. Cooperative breeding
   C. Random pairing
   D. Polygyny with strong skew in male success

8. Through its raking foraging, the superb lyrebird:
   A. Has no effect on its habitat
   B. Turns over large amounts of litter and soil, acting as an ecosystem engineer
   C. Pollinates flowers
   D. Disperses only large seeds

9. Lyrebird song is an example of cultural transmission because it is:
   A. Encoded entirely in genes
   B. Identical in every individual
   C. Learned and passed between individuals, forming local traditions
   D. Never varied

10. The IUCN Red List status of the superb lyrebird is:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Endangered
   D. Critically Endangered

## Answer Key

1. C
2. B
3. A
4. D
5. B
6. C
7. D
8. B
9. C
10. A
