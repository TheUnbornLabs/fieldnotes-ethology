# Quiz — Book 077: The Mute Swan

1. The Mute Swan belongs to which family?
   A. Ardeidae (herons)
   B. Anatidae (waterfowl)
   C. Phoenicopteridae (flamingos)
   D. Rallidae (rails)

2. An adult male swan is called a:
   A. Pen
   B. Cygnet
   C. Cob
   D. Drake

3. The statement "Mute Swans mate for life" is best described as:
   A. Always true without exception
   B. Completely false
   C. Usually true but not absolute; divorce and re-pairing occur
   D. True only in captivity

4. The "busking" posture is used mainly for:
   A. Attracting insects
   B. Territorial threat and defence
   C. Keeping cool
   D. Long-distance migration

5. The claim that a swan can easily break a person's arm with its wing is:
   A. Well documented and accurate
   B. Exaggerated folklore, though a wing blow can hurt
   C. True only for the largest males
   D. Impossible; swans cannot strike at all

6. The Mute Swan reaches submerged plants mainly by:
   A. Diving deep underwater
   B. Up-ending and stretching its long neck to the bottom
   C. Filtering water like a flamingo
   D. Catching floating seeds only

7. Mute Swan cygnets are:
   A. Naked, blind, and helpless at hatching
   B. Downy, mobile, and able to swim within a day or two
   C. Unable to leave the nest for weeks
   D. Born fully white

8. Compared with the Mute Swan, the Whooper Swan is:
   A. Silent
   B. Much smaller
   C. Loudly vocal with far-carrying bugling calls
   D. Unable to fly

9. The "swan song" — a beautiful song sung just before death — is:
   A. A real and studied behavior
   B. A myth with no basis in the bird's biology
   C. Produced only by cygnets
   D. The sound of the swan's wings

10. Outside the breeding season, Mute Swans typically:
   A. Remain fiercely territorial year-round
   B. Live entirely alone
   C. Gather in flocks with much less aggression
   D. Migrate individually to the tropics

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. C
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. C
9. B
10. C
