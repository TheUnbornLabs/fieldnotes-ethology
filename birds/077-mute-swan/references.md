## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- IUCN Red List status: **Least Concern** — confirmed (IUCN Red List / BirdLife DataZone; Wikipedia "Mute swan"). Book status correct.
- Mass and size: adults commonly ~9–12 kg (males up to ~14.3 kg, females up to ~10.6 kg); wingspan ~200–240 cm; one of the heaviest flying birds and the largest/heaviest member of Anseriformes. Sources: Wikipedia "Mute swan"; Dimensions.com. Book's figures ("roughly 9 to 12 kg or more," "2 to 2.4 metres," "one of the largest and heaviest flying birds") verified.
- Scientific name *Cygnus olor*; family Anatidae, order Anseriformes; native Eurasia and widely introduced (incl. North America) where treated as invasive: verified.
- "Mute" is comparative (not silent); "swan song" is folklore; the "breaks-your-arm" claim is exaggerated: consistent with standard sources. No factual corrections needed.
