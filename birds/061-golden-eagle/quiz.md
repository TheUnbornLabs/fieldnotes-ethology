# Quiz — The Golden Eagle (*Aquila chrysaetos*)

1. The golden eagle is called a "booted" eagle because:
   A. Feathers extend down its legs to the toes
   B. It kicks its prey
   C. It nests in old boots
   D. It has unusually large feet

2. The golden eagle's primary hunting method is:
   A. Diving underwater for fish
   B. Hunting by sound in darkness
   C. Soaring search followed by a stooping strike
   D. Chasing prey on foot

3. Golden eagles have how many foveae in each eye?
   A. None
   B. One
   C. Two
   D. Four

4. "Cainism" in golden eagles refers to:
   A. Building multiple nests
   B. A type of migration
   C. One nestling killing its younger sibling
   D. Cooperative hunting

5. The golden eagle's aerial cartwheeling and undulating flights are primarily:
   A. Mid-air mating
   B. A hunting technique for fish
   C. A response to predators
   D. Courtship and territorial display

6. Compared with the golden eagle, the bald eagle is:
   A. A booted upland mammal-hunter
   B. A sea-eagle specialized for fish and scavenging
   C. Smaller and insect-eating
   D. Nocturnal

7. The global IUCN Red List status of the golden eagle is:
   A. Critically Endangered
   B. Endangered
   C. Vulnerable
   D. Least Concern

8. A major modern, human-caused threat to golden eagles is:
   A. Lead poisoning from ammunition in carrion
   B. Competition with penguins
   C. Loss of coral reefs
   D. Overheating from too many thermals

9. The claim that golden eagles routinely carry off children and livestock is:
   A. Well documented and common
   B. True only for males
   C. Grossly exaggerated; they cannot lift such weights
   D. True only at night

10. The traditional Kazakh practice of hunting with trained golden eagles reflects:
    A. That eagles are easy to domesticate fully
    B. A long-standing working partnership with a wild predator
    C. That eagles hunt only fish
    D. That eagles cannot be trained

## Answer Key

1: A — 2: C — 3: C — 4: C — 5: D — 6: B — 7: D — 8: A — 9: C — 10: B
