# Short Quiz — The Barn Owl (*Tyto alba*)

1. The barn owl's ability to catch prey in complete darkness depends mainly on:
   A. Its extraordinarily precise directional hearing
   B. Its sense of smell
   C. Echolocation like a bat
   D. Heat-sensing pits

2. The vertical asymmetry of the barn owl's ears mainly allows it to determine a sound's:
   A. Loudness only
   B. Frequency
   C. Elevation (up-down position)
   D. Distance in kilometres

3. The heart-shaped facial disc of the barn owl functions as:
   A. A purely decorative marking
   B. A shield against wind
   C. A heat radiator
   D. A parabolic collector that funnels sound to the ears

4. The barn owl's characteristic call is:
   A. A long, harsh, rasping screech
   B. A mellow hoot
   C. A musical whistle
   D. A drumming sound

5. A barn owl pellet is:
   A. A store of surplus food
   B. A compacted mass of indigestible fur and bone ejected through the mouth
   C. A nest-building material
   D. A scent marker

6. Asynchronous hatching in barn owls produces:
   A. Identical chicks of the same size
   B. Eggs that all hatch on the same day
   C. A brood of graded ages and sizes
   D. Only one chick per nest

7. Brood reduction refers to:
   A. Parents laying fewer eggs each year
   B. Chicks leaving the nest early
   C. Predators removing eggs
   D. The loss of the youngest chicks when food is scarce

8. Nestling "negotiation" in barn owls is:
   A. Fighting to the death among chicks
   B. A vocal exchange in which hungrier chicks call more and siblings defer
   C. Begging directed only at the mother
   D. A courtship display

9. A barn owl can rotate its head approximately:
   A. 90 degrees
   B. 180 degrees
   C. 270 degrees
   D. A full 360 degrees

10. Compared with the barn owl, the Eurasian eagle-owl is best described as:
   A. A smaller acoustic specialist on voles
   B. A large, powerful generalist predator
   C. A daytime insect-eater
   D. A non-predatory owl

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. D
4. A
5. B
6. C
7. D
8. B
9. C
10. B
