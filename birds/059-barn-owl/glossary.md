# Glossary — The Barn Owl (*Tyto alba*)

**Nocturnal** — active primarily at night; the barn owl hunts mainly after dusk and through the night.

**Ear asymmetry** — the vertically offset placement of the barn owl's two ear openings, one higher than the other, which enables localisation of sound in elevation as well as in horizontal direction.

**Sound localization** — determining the direction and, in the barn owl, the elevation of a sound source, using differences in the time and loudness of the sound's arrival at the two ears.

**Facial ruff (facial disc)** — the heart-shaped arrangement of stiff feathers on the owl's face that acts as a parabolic collector, funnelling sound toward the ears.

**Auditory space map** — an ordered neural representation in the midbrain in which neurons are tuned to particular directions in space, so that a sound activates a corresponding location in the map.

**Interaural time difference** — the small difference in the arrival time of a sound at the two ears, used chiefly to compute horizontal direction (azimuth).

**Interaural level difference** — the difference in the loudness of a sound at the two ears, used — because of the barn owl's ear asymmetry — to compute vertical direction (elevation).

**Silent flight** — flight made nearly noiseless by feather modifications, allowing the owl both to hear faint prey and to approach undetected.

**Feather serration** — the comb-like teeth along the leading edge of the owl's flight feathers, which, together with the soft trailing-edge fringe, reduce aerodynamic noise.

**Pellet** — a compacted mass of indigestible fur and bone ejected through the mouth, whose contents reveal the owl's diet.

**Quartering flight** — the slow, low, back-and-forth search flight over open ground during which the owl listens for prey and may hover before striking.

**Asynchronous hatching** — hatching of eggs over several days in the order they were laid, producing a brood of graded ages and sizes.

**Brood reduction** — the loss of the youngest, smallest chicks when food is scarce, concentrating limited food into offspring that can be raised successfully.

**Nestling negotiation** — the vocal exchange among barn owl chicks, in the parents' absence, in which hungrier chicks call more and better-fed siblings defer over who receives the next prey item.

**Low-light vision** — the barn owl's highly light-sensitive vision that supports hunting at very low light levels, complementing its hearing.
