# Short Quiz — The Griffon Vulture (*Gyps fulvus*)

1. The griffon vulture finds its food mainly by:
   A. Smell
   B. Hearing dying animals
   C. Echolocation
   D. Sight, including watching other vultures

2. The griffon vulture is best described as a(n):
   A. Predator that kills live prey
   B. Obligate scavenger
   C. Seed-eater
   D. Fish-eater

3. Compared with the griffon vulture, the New World turkey vulture is notable for:
   A. A well-developed sense of smell for finding carrion
   B. Nesting colonially on cliffs
   C. Being unable to soar
   D. Hunting live mammals

4. The resemblance between Old World and New World vultures is an example of:
   A. Close common ancestry
   B. Convergent evolution
   C. Hybridisation
   D. Mimicry

5. The griffon vulture typically raises how many chicks per breeding attempt?
   A. One
   B. Four to six
   C. Ten or more
   D. None; it does not care for young

6. The veterinary drug that caused catastrophic Gyps vulture declines in Asia is:
   A. Penicillin
   B. Aspirin
   C. Ivermectin
   D. Diclofenac

7. The griffon vulture's ecological role is best described as:
   A. Top predator of large mammals
   B. Pollinator
   C. Efficient disposer of carcasses (natural sanitation)
   D. Seed disperser

8. The griffon vulture's global IUCN Red List status is:
   A. Critically Endangered
   B. Endangered
   C. Least Concern (masking regional threats)
   D. Extinct in the Wild

9. Griffon vultures nest:
   A. Colonially on cliffs and in gorges
   B. Alone in dense forest
   C. On the open ground in fields
   D. In underground burrows

10. The gravest threat to modern griffon vultures is:
   A. Natural predators
   B. Poisoning
   C. Competition with eagles
   D. Cold weather

## Answer Key

1. D
2. B
3. A
4. B
5. A
6. D
7. C
8. C
9. A
10. B
