# Glossary — The Griffon Vulture (*Gyps fulvus*)

**Accipitridae** — the family of hawks, eagles, and Old World vultures, to which the griffon vulture belongs.
**Old World vulture** — a vulture of Europe, Africa, and Asia belonging to the Accipitridae, distinct from the separate New World vultures.
**New World vulture** — a vulture of the Americas belonging to a different lineage; some, like the turkey vulture, find food by smell.
**Obligate scavenger** — an animal that depends almost entirely on carrion and does not normally kill live prey; the griffon vulture is one.
**Carrion** — the flesh of dead animals, the griffon vulture's food.
**Soaring flight** — flight using rising air (thermals and updrafts) to gain height and travel with little flapping.
**Thermal** — a rising column of warm air used by soaring birds; central to vulture foraging.
**Colonial nesting** — breeding in groups, in the griffon vulture's case on cliffs and in gorges.
**Social information use** — gaining information by observing the behavior of other animals, as vultures do by watching one another to find carcasses.
**Convergent evolution** — the independent evolution of similar features in unrelated groups, as in Old World and New World vultures.
**Diclofenac** — a veterinary anti-inflammatory drug that is highly toxic to Gyps vultures and caused catastrophic declines in Asia.
**Vulture restaurant** — a supplementary feeding site where safe carcasses are provided for vultures as a conservation measure.
**Gorge-and-fast** — the pattern of eating a very large amount at one carcass and then fasting for extended periods, suited to an unpredictable food supply.
**Least Concern** — an IUCN Red List category indicating a species is not currently at high risk of extinction globally, which may mask regional threats.
