# Glossary — The Mourning Dove (*Zenaida macroura*)

- **Columbid** — a member of the family Columbidae, the pigeons and doves.
- **Perch-coo (advertising call)** — the male Mourning Dove's soft, mournful cooing song, used to hold territory and attract a mate; often mistaken for an owl.
- **Wing-whistle** — the sharp whistling sound produced mechanically by air passing over modified outer flight feathers during takeoff and landing, functioning as an alarm signal rather than a vocalization.
- **Crop milk** — a nutritious, curd-like secretion sloughed from the lining of the crop that both parent columbids regurgitate to feed their young.
- **Squab** — a young, nest-bound dove or pigeon chick.
- **Granivore** — an animal that feeds mainly on seeds and grain.
- **Gastrolith (grit)** — a small stone or coarse particle swallowed and held in the gizzard to grind hard food such as seeds.
- **Suction drinking** — the columbid method of drinking by keeping the bill immersed and drawing water up continuously, without tilting the head back to swallow.
- **Monogamy** — a mating system in which one male and one female form a breeding pair; in Mourning Doves the pair bond is typically seasonal.
- **Display flight** — a ritualized courtship flight in which the male climbs with noisy wingbeats, then glides down in a stiff-winged circle to advertise himself.
- **Multi-brooding** — raising several successive broods within a single breeding season.
- **Altricial** — hatched in a helpless, undeveloped state (naked, blind), requiring extended parental care, as in dove squabs.
- **Precocial** — hatched relatively developed and mobile, able to leave the nest and feed soon after hatching; the contrast to altricial development.
- **Crop** — a muscular pouch of the throat/esophagus used to store food and, in columbids, to produce crop milk.
- **Gizzard** — the muscular stomach that, aided by grit, grinds hard seeds.
