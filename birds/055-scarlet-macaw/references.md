# References / Further Reading — Scarlet Macaw (*Ara macao*)

Per the collection's citation policy, sources are separated into those safe to name and those that must be verified before publication. No citations have been invented; specific article-level details are deliberately not fabricated.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- IUCN Red List — *Ara macao* (Scarlet Macaw) species assessment page. A checkable primary source for range, population trend, and conservation status (Least Concern).
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology — *Birds of the World* / *Neotropical Birds*, species account for the scarlet macaw. Standard reference for identification, distribution, and life history.
- Research associated with **Donald Brightsmith and the Tambopata Macaw Project** (Peru) on macaw ecology, clay-lick (geophagy) use, nesting, brood reduction, and conservation. A well-known body of field research on this species and its relatives; individual papers should be located and cited specifically before publication.
- Animal Diversity Web (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — *Ara macao* species account.
- Standard animal-behavior texts for general principles cited (mating systems, communication, life history): Alcock, *Animal Behavior*; Bradbury & Vehrencamp, *Principles of Animal Communication*.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Scientific name *Ara macao*, order Psittaciformes, family Psittacidae, genus *Ara* — confirmed (BirdLife DataZone; Animal Diversity Web).
- IUCN Red List status **Least Concern** (globally), with an overall **decreasing** population trend and regionally reduced/extirpated northern populations — confirmed (BirdLife DataZone; IUCN). Book's "globally Least Concern, yet locally wiped out" framing is accurate.
- Two subspecies *A. m. macao* (South America) and *A. m. cyanoptera* (Central America/Mexico) — confirmed (Wikipedia/Birds of the World; USFWS lists cyanoptera as endangered).
