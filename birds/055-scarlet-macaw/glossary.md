# Glossary — Scarlet Macaw (*Ara macao*)

- **Psittacine** — A member of the parrot order (Psittaciformes); macaws, greys, cockatoos, and their relatives.
- **Pair bond** — A stable, cooperative association between two mated individuals, in macaws typically long-term.
- **Monogamy** — A mating system in which one individual pairs with one partner; in macaws, social monogamy sustained across seasons.
- **Contact call** — A vocalization used to maintain contact and coordinate between mates or flockmates over distance.
- **Communal roost** — A shared nighttime gathering site where many birds sleep together, offering safety in numbers.
- **Geophagy** — The deliberate eating of soil or clay; in macaws, visiting exposed riverbank "clay licks."
- **Clay lick (collpa)** — An exposed bank of mineral-rich clay where macaws and other animals gather to eat soil.
- **Zygodactyl** — Having two toes pointing forward and two backward, giving parrots a strong, dexterous grip.
- **Cavity nester** — A species that nests inside enclosed hollows, such as tree cavities, rather than open nests.
- **Biparental care** — Care of offspring provided by both parents.
- **Seed predator** — An animal that destroys seeds by eating them, as opposed to dispersing them intact.
- **Seed disperser** — An animal that moves viable seeds away from the parent plant, aiding plant reproduction.
- **Allopreening** — Mutual grooming of feathers between individuals, important in maintaining pair and social bonds.
- **Flock** — A group of birds foraging, moving, or roosting together; in macaws, loose and fluid in composition.
- **Fission-fusion** — A social pattern in which group size and membership change over time around stable core units.
- **Altricial** — Describing young that hatch helpless — naked, blind, and dependent — requiring extended parental care.
