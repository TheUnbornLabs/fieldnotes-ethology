# Quiz — Scarlet Macaw (*Ara macao*)

1. To which order do scarlet macaws belong?
   A. Psittaciformes
   B. Passeriformes
   C. Falconiformes
   D. Columbiformes

2. The scarlet macaw's mating system is best described as:
   A. Seasonal promiscuity
   B. Lifelong solitary living
   C. Long-term social monogamy
   D. Colonial group breeding

3. "Zygodactyl" feet have:
   A. Two toes forward, two back
   B. Three toes forward, one back
   C. All four toes forward
   D. Webbing between the toes

4. Clay-lick (geophagy) behavior is thought to function in:
   A. Sharpening the bill
   B. Sodium supplementation and/or binding dietary toxins
   C. Attracting mates only
   D. Cooling the body

5. Scarlet macaws build their nests in:
   A. Open cup nests on branches
   B. Tree cavities
   C. Burrows in riverbanks
   D. Floating vegetation

6. In feeding ecology, scarlet macaws are primarily:
   A. Filter feeders
   B. Nectar specialists
   C. Scavengers
   D. Seed predators

7. Their dominant sense for social display and mate assessment is:
   A. Smell
   B. Echolocation
   C. Vision (including UV)
   D. Electroreception

8. Communal roosts benefit macaws mainly by:
   A. Providing warmth from clay
   B. Increasing predator detection through many eyes and ears
   C. Reducing the need to fly
   D. Allowing underwater sleep

9. The African grey parrot differs from the scarlet macaw most notably in being:
   A. Flightless
   B. A nocturnal hunter
   C. A colonial ground nester
   D. Subtly plumaged and famed for studied cognition

10. The scarlet macaw's global IUCN status is:
    A. Critically Endangered
    B. Endangered
    C. Least Concern
    D. Extinct in the Wild

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. A
4. B
5. B
6. D
7. C
8. B
9. D
10. C
