## 3. Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following facts were confirmed against live web sources on 2026-07-02:

- Scientific name *Apus apus*; family Apodidae, order Apodiformes; swifts closely related to hummingbirds — confirmed (Wikipedia; Audubon).
- Up to ~10-month continuous aerial non-breeding phase (airborne >99% of the time; some individuals never landing) — confirmed against the primary study (Hedenström et al., "Annual 10-Month Aerial Life Phase in the Common Swift Apus apus," Current Biology 2016) and Lund University / Audubon / Sci.News coverage.
- Relatively long-lived for a small bird (individuals into their teens) — consistent with sources.
- IUCN Red List status **Least Concern** globally, with regional declines from loss of nesting cavities — confirmed (Cornell Birds of the World; BirdLife DataZone).
