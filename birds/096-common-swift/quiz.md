# Short Quiz — The Common Swift (*Apus apus*)

1. The common swift is most closely related to which group?
   A. Swallows
   B. Falcons
   C. Sparrows
   D. Hummingbirds

2. The name "Apodidae" refers to the swift's:
   A. Long tail
   B. Very short legs and feet
   C. Loud call
   D. Migratory habit

3. Non-breeding common swifts have been shown by tracking studies to:
   A. Roost on cliffs every night
   B. Migrate only short distances
   C. Feed mainly on the ground
   D. Remain airborne almost continuously for months

4. A swift carries food to its young as:
   A. Regurgitated fish
   B. Whole large insects one at a time
   C. A saliva-bound ball of many small insects
   D. Seeds and berries

5. When cold, wet weather cuts off the insect supply, swift nestlings can:
   A. Fly to Africa early
   B. Enter a torpor-like state of reduced metabolism
   C. Switch to eating seeds
   D. Be fed by neighbouring pairs

6. The resemblance between swifts and swallows is best explained by:
   A. Close common ancestry
   B. Convergent evolution
   C. Hybridisation
   D. Coincidental colouration only

7. The "screaming party" of swifts is:
   A. A fast, calling group flight around the colony
   B. A defensive mobbing of predators
   C. A migratory departure signal only
   D. A feeding technique

8. Swift nests are built from:
   A. Mud, like swallow nests
   B. Twigs woven into an open cup
   C. Airborne material glued with saliva inside a cavity
   D. Feathers alone, with no binding

9. After leaving the nest, a young common swift:
   A. Receives no further parental care and flies into an independent aerial life
   B. Is fed by its parents for several weeks
   C. Returns to the nest each night for a month
   D. Joins a crèche guarded by adults

10. The main modern conservation concern for common swifts in parts of their range is:
   A. Hunting for food
   B. Pesticide poisoning of adults
   C. Loss of nesting cavities as buildings are sealed or replaced
   D. Competition with hummingbirds

## Answer Key

1. D
2. B
3. D
4. C
5. B
6. B
7. A
8. C
9. A
10. C
