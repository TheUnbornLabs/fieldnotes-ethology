## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

The following facts in Section 2 were checked against the live web and confirmed:

- Scientific name *Sturnus vulgaris*, family Sturnidae (order Passeriformes) — confirmed via Cornell Birds of the World and Britannica.
- IUCN Red List status: Least Concern globally (enormous total range/population) despite documented declines in parts of the native European range — confirmed.
- North American introduction: releases in New York's Central Park in the 1890s (~60 birds in 1890, ~40 in 1891; ~100 total), from which the continental population descends — confirmed via Cornell Birds of the World and multiple sources. The book's Section 13 appropriately keeps the "Shakespeare/Acclimatization Society" motivation as a debated detail, which the sources support.
