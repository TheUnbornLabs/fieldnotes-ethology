# Quiz — The European Starling (*Sturnus vulgaris*)

1. A starling murmuration is best described as:
   A. A flock led by a single dominant bird
   B. Self-organised collective motion emerging from local rules
   C. A migration route
   D. A courtship display by one male

2. The coordinated patterns of a murmuration arise because each bird:
   A. Follows a leader at the front
   B. Memorises a fixed flight plan
   C. Responds to the movements of a few near neighbours
   D. Is guided by a call from the ground

3. The European starling is native to:
   A. North America
   B. Australia
   C. South America
   D. Eurasia and North Africa

4. The North American starling population originated from:
   A. Birds that flew across the Atlantic naturally
   B. Escaped pets in California
   C. Deliberate releases in New York in the 1890s
   D. Ancient native populations

5. The starling's foraging technique of inserting the closed bill into the ground and opening it is called:
   A. Open-bill probing
   B. Plunge-diving
   C. Hawking
   D. Gleaning

6. With respect to nest sites, the starling:
   A. Excavates its own holes like a woodpecker
   B. Builds an open cup nest in the open
   C. Nests on bare ground
   D. Uses existing cavities and competes aggressively for them

7. The starling's status as both a declining native and a superabundant introduced bird shows that:
   A. It is extinct in Europe
   B. Global abundance can conceal regional decline
   C. It never declines anywhere
   D. It is native to every continent

8. Starling vocal behaviour is notable for:
   A. Complete silence
   B. A single simple call only
   C. Complex song with vocal mimicry
   D. Echolocation

9. Flocking helps protect starlings from predators mainly by:
   A. Making the birds invisible
   B. Poisoning the predator
   C. Allowing them to fight the predator
   D. Confusing predators and diluting each bird's risk

10. The murmuration should be understood as an example of:
   A. A group mind directing the flock
   B. Emergent behaviour from simple individual rules
   C. Advanced individual reasoning
   D. Learned choreography taught by parents

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. D
4. C
5. A
6. D
7. B
8. C
9. D
10. B
