## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Struthio camelus*; ratite (Palaeognathae), order Struthioniformes — confirmed. The Somali Ostrich *Struthio molybdophanes* is now widely treated as a separate species, as the book notes.
- **IUCN Red List status: Least Concern** — confirmed (common ostrich; the split-off Somali Ostrich is assessed separately as Vulnerable).
- **World's largest and heaviest living bird** — confirmed. Height up to ~2.8 m and mass up to ~145 kg (adults 63.5–145 kg) — confirmed (PBS, Live Science, Natural History Museum, Wikipedia). Book figure of "up to 145 kg" is accurate.
- **Fastest running bird / land speed** — confirmed: sustained ~50 km/h with bursts to ~70 km/h (43 mph). Book figures accurate.
- **Largest egg of any living bird** — confirmed; ostrich eggs weigh roughly 1.3–2 kg (commonly ~1.4–1.8 kg). Book's "1.3–2 kg" is accurate.
