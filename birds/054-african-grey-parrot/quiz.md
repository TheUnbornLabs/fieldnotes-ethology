# Quiz — The African Grey Parrot

1. The scientific name of the African grey parrot is:
   A. *Psittacus erithacus*
   B. *Corvus corax*
   C. *Ara macao*
   D. *Melopsittacus undulatus*

2. Irene Pepperberg's most famous grey parrot was named:
   A. Koko
   B. Alex
   C. Betty
   D. Nkisi

3. In controlled tests, Alex demonstrated the ability to:
   A. Speak in full grammatical sentences
   B. Read written text
   C. Label colours, shapes, materials, and numbers referentially
   D. Compose novel songs

4. The grey's ability to use a word for "none" is best described as:
   A. Proof of full human language
   B. Random mimicry
   C. A courtship display
   D. A concept approaching a zero-like or absence idea

5. A "contact call" in a wild grey flock functions most like:
   A. A name identifying a specific individual
   B. A song sung only by males
   C. An alarm for predators only
   D. A digestive aid

6. Feather-plucking in captive greys is most accurately described as:
   A. Normal seasonal moulting
   B. Feather-damaging behaviour often linked to stress and boredom
   C. A sign of good health
   D. A mating display

7. The African grey's conservation status is:
   A. Least Concern
   B. Extinct in the Wild
   C. Endangered
   D. Domesticated and secure

8. Compared with the African grey, the common raven is especially noted for:
   A. Referential colour labelling
   B. Ultraviolet-based navigation
   C. Filter feeding
   D. Physical problem-solving, caching, and planning

9. Geophagy in African greys refers to:
   A. Deliberately eating mineral clay
   B. Eating other birds
   C. Migrating between continents
   D. Building mud nests

10. Which statement about grey intelligence is most scientifically honest?
    A. Greys possess full human language
    B. Greys only mimic and understand nothing
    C. Greys show trained referential label use but not open-ended grammar
    D. Greys cannot be studied experimentally

## Answer Key
1. A
2. B
3. C
4. D
5. A
6. B
7. C
8. D
9. A
10. C
