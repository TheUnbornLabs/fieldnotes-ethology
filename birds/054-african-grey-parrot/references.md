# References / Further Reading — The African Grey Parrot

Sources are separated into those safe to name and those needing verification before publication, per the collection's citation policy. No citations have been invented.

## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- Irene M. Pepperberg, *The Alex Studies: Cognitive and Communicative Abilities of Grey Parrots* — the foundational scholarly account of the referential-communication research with Alex.
- Irene M. Pepperberg, *Alex & Me* — an accessible first-person narrative of the decades-long work with the parrot Alex.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Psittacus erithacus* (African grey parrot); primary source for its Endangered status, threats, and range.
- CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora) — Appendix I listing for *Psittacus erithacus*.
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology, Birds of the World — species account for the African grey parrot (natural history, ecology, and distribution).

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Scientific name *Psittacus erithacus*, order Psittaciformes, family Psittacidae — confirmed (BirdLife DataZone; Cornell Lab).
- IUCN Red List status **Endangered** — confirmed: uplisted from Vulnerable to Endangered in **2016** due to trapping for the pet trade and habitat loss (BirdLife DataZone; Cornell Bird Academy). CITES **Appendix I** listing (October 2016) also confirmed.
- Timneh parrot (*Psittacus timneh*) recognised as a separate species (formerly a subspecies) — confirmed (BirdLife/IOC).
