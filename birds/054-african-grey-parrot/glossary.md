# Glossary — The African Grey Parrot

- **Vocal learning** — The ability to acquire and modify vocalizations through experience rather than inheriting them fixed; well developed in parrots, songbirds, and hummingbirds, and the foundation of the grey's mimicry.
- **Referential communication** — Using a signal or label that stands for a specific thing, quality, or category in the world, rather than just expressing an internal state.
- **Label** — A learned word or sign used to designate an object, colour, shape, material, or number; the unit of Alex's trained vocabulary.
- **Concept of same/different** — The abstract ability to judge whether two items share or differ in a given quality (colour, shape, material), independent of the specific items shown.
- **Zero-like / absence concept** — An understanding of "none" or the empty set; in Alex's case, using a label when there was no difference or no item to report — a step toward a zero-like idea.
- **Contact call** — An individually distinctive call used to identify and stay in touch with specific flock-mates, functioning somewhat like a name.
- **Pair bond** — A strong, often long-term partnership between two mates, maintained by behaviours such as allopreening and courtship feeding.
- **Prosociality** — Behaviour that benefits another individual, such as a grey voluntarily helping a partner obtain food by transferring exchange tokens.
- **Forebrain neurons** — Nerve cells in the forebrain (pallium); parrots pack them at high density, giving small brains large neuron counts comparable to bigger-brained primates.
- **CITES Appendix I** — The strictest category of the international wildlife-trade convention, effectively banning commercial international trade in wild-caught specimens.
- **Feather-plucking** — Feather-damaging behaviour in which a bird pulls out its own feathers, often linked to stress, boredom, loneliness, or medical and dietary causes.
- **Enrichment** — Provision of stimulation, foraging challenges, and social contact designed to meet the psychological and social needs of captive animals.
- **Geophagy** — The deliberate eating of soil or mineral clay, used by greys to obtain minerals and help neutralize plant toxins in the diet.
- **Zygodactyl** — A foot arrangement with two toes pointing forward and two back, aiding climbing and precise manipulation of food and objects.
- **Altricial** — Hatching helpless, blind, and nearly naked, requiring extended parental care and feeding in the nest cavity.
- **Psittacine** — Relating to parrots (order Psittaciformes), as distinct from corvids (crows and ravens); the two represent separate lineages of avian intelligence.
