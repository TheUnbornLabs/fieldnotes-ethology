# Book 066 — The Indian Peafowl: Quiz

**Multiple-choice questions. Answers at the end.**

1. Tail coverts bearing eyespots in Indian peafowl are displayed through:
   a) Raising upward to create vertical fan  
   b) Rapid vibrations producing sound  
   c) Sweeping ground motions  
   d) Trailing behind as bird walks  

2. In peafowl lek-mating, which sex chooses the mate?
   a) Males based on plumage  
   b) Females based on display  
   c) Pairs through duets  
   d) Older birds arrange pairings  

3. The evolutionary concept explaining the peafowl's costly train is:
   a) Natural selection for survival  
   b) Kin selection  
   c) Sexual selection for mating success  
   d) Group selection  

4. Peafowl chicks are best described as:
   a) Altricial (helpless)  
   b) Semi-altricial  
   c) Nidicolous (nest-bound)  
   d) Precocial (active soon after hatching)  

5. How many individuals typically incubate peafowl eggs?
   a) Female alone  
   b) Breeding pair  
   c) Multiple females cooperatively  
   d) Males exclusively  

6. Primary predators of adult Indian peafowl include:
   a) Large insects  
   b) Fish  
   c) Herbivorous birds  
   d) Raptors, carnivorous mammals, snakes  

7. Indian peafowl have been associated with humans for:
   a) Less than 200 years  
   b) ~500 years  
   c) At least 1500 years (~1500 BCE)  
   d) Less than 50 years  

8. The peafowl's diet is:
   a) Strictly seeds and vegetation  
   b) Omnivorous: seeds, insects, small animals, vegetation  
   c) Exclusively insects  
   d) Large prey hunted cooperatively  

9. The train's honesty as a signal depends on:
   a) Bright color alone  
   b) Male age  
   c) Female preference alone  
   d) Size and cost to male survival  

10. Feral Indian peafowl populations are established in:
    a) Europe, North America, Australia, Pacific islands  
    b) Only India and nearby countries  
    c) Zoos only  
    d) Only warm tropical regions  

**Answer Key:** 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d, 5-a, 6-d, 7-c, 8-b, 9-d, 10-a
