# Book 067 — The Domestic Chicken: Quiz

1. Domestic chickens are descended from:
   a) The Red Junglefowl (*Gallus gallus*)  b) Wild chickens still common in nature  c) An extinct wild species  d) Multiple wild species

2. A chicken flock's "pecking order" primarily functions to:
   a) Establish permanent social roles  b) Reduce constant fighting through predictable hierarchies  c) Determine which birds reproduce  d) Organize foraging efficiency

3. Unfertilized chicken eggs:
   a) Are sterile and toxic  b) Develop without a rooster  c) Do not develop; are edible  d) Cannot be laid without a rooster

4. Chickens show cognitive abilities including:
   a) No problem-solving  b) Tool use and cooperation  c) Abstract reasoning like humans  d) Individual recognition and observational learning

5. Broody hens:
   a) Stop laying eggs temporarily while raising chicks  b) Are unable to care for chicks  c) Reject human contact  d) Require rooster assistance for brooding

6. Primary wild predators of chickens include:
   a) Only snakes  b) Raptors, carnivorous mammals, and some snakes  c) No natural predators exist  d) Only humans

7. Domestication of chickens occurred:
   a) Less than 500 years ago  b) In Europe and North America  c) At least 4,000–8,000 years ago in Asia  d) In recorded history only

8. Feral chickens:
   a) Cannot survive without human care  b) Become helpless in the wild  c) Always maintain domesticated traits  d) Establish populations and revert toward wild phenotypes

9. A hen will lay eggs:
   a) Only with a rooster present  b) Even without a rooster (unfertilized eggs)  c) Only during certain seasons  d) Only when broody

10. Intensive confinement of chickens is associated with:
    a) Improved health and behavior  b) Enhanced natural behaviors  c) Reduced environmental impact  d) Stress-related behavioral stereotypies and welfare problems

**Answer Key:** 1-a, 2-b, 3-c, 4-d, 5-a, 6-b, 7-c, 8-d, 9-b, 10-d
