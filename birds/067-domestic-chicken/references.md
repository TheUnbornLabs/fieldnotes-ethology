## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Gallus gallus domesticus*; descended from Red Junglefowl *Gallus gallus* (Phasianidae) — confirmed.
- **IUCN status: not separately assessed** — correct. As a domesticated form, the domestic chicken is not given its own IUCN Red List category (the wild ancestor Red Junglefowl is assessed as Least Concern). The book's statement that IUCN status "is not assessed separately for domesticated populations" is accurate.
- Domestication timeframe: the book's "at least 4,000, possibly over 8,000 years ago" spans the range in the literature; recent genomic/archaeological work has argued for a more recent primary domestication (~3,500 years ago) in Southeast Asia, so the older end is contested. Left as-is with this note; not a factual error given the hedged phrasing.
- Cognition/welfare citations (Marino 2017; Mench & Keeling 2001; Jones 1996) remain flagged for exact pages/authorship as noted.
