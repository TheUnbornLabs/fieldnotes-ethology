- Bernd Heinrich, *Mind of the Raven* — a classic account of raven cognition and the string-pulling experiments, by a researcher long associated with the species.
- Bernd Heinrich, *Ravens in Winter* — field study of communal feeding and recruitment at carcasses.
- IUCN Red List — species assessment for *Corvus corax* (Least Concern); a checkable authority for status, range, and population trend.
- Cornell Lab of Ornithology (All About Birds / Birds of the World) — species profile for the Common Raven covering identification, range, and natural history.
- Standard animal-behavior texts such as Alcock, *Animal Behavior*, for general treatment of foraging, communication, and cognition.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

- Scientific name *Corvus corax*, family Corvidae, order Passeriformes — confirmed (IUCN Red List; Animal Diversity Web).
- IUCN Red List status **Least Concern** (assessed 2016, BirdLife International) — confirmed via IUCN Red List species page (iucnredlist.org, species 113271893) and BirdLife DataZone factsheet.
- The planning study is **Kabadayi & Osvath (2017), "Ravens parallel great apes in flexible planning for tool-use and bartering," *Science* 357(6347):202–204** — confirmed (science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aam8138; PubMed 28927634). Ravens selected/kept tools or tokens for delayed rewards (up to 17 h) and outperformed 4-year-old children on a comparable task.

## Recent research (verified 2026-07-09)

- **Ravens anticipate wolf kill sites (2026):** Loretto, Marzluff, and colleagues, *"Ravens anticipate wolf kill sites across broad scales,"* **Science** (published 12 March 2026; doi:10.1126/science.adz9467) — confirmed via science.org and the University of Washington news release (12 March 2026). About 69 GPS-tagged ravens and 20 satellite-collared wolves were tracked in Greater Yellowstone for ~2.5 years. Contrary to the "ravens follow wolves" assumption, sustained following was almost absent (one prolonged case); ravens relied on spatial memory of where wolf kills are most frequent, reaching ~50% of observed kills within seven days and sometimes flying >150 km in a day. This resolves the item flagged as "raven–wolf commensal foraging" above with a verified primary source.
