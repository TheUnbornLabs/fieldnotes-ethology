# Glossary — The Common Raven (*Corvus corax*)

- **Corvid** — a member of the family Corvidae, the crows, ravens, jays, and magpies, noted for large brains and complex behavior.
- **Caching** — storing surplus food in hidden locations for later retrieval.
- **Pilfering** — stealing food from another individual's cache, often after watching it being hidden.
- **Perspective-taking** — adjusting behavior according to what another individual can perceive; debated in ravens as a possible form of reading others' viewpoints.
- **Re-caching** — moving previously hidden food to a new location, often after being observed, as a countermeasure against theft.
- **Future planning** — selecting or keeping an item now for a benefit available only later, rather than acting only on the immediate situation.
- **Object play** — manipulating and exploring objects without immediate survival benefit, as when ravens slide down snowbanks or toss and catch items in flight.
- **String-pulling task** — a physical problem in which an animal must pull up a suspended string in stages to obtain hanging food.
- **Vocal mimicry** — learned imitation of sounds, including other species' calls or human words, without comprehension of their meaning.
- **Recruitment call** — a vocalisation that attracts other individuals, notably young ravens calling others to a carcass.
- **Territoriality** — the defence of a fixed area against rivals, characteristic of bonded adult raven pairs.
- **Pallium / forebrain neurons** — the outer forebrain region and its nerve cells; in corvids these are packed in unusually high numbers, a likely basis for their cognition.
- **Neophobia** — cautious avoidance of novel objects, common in ravens near food.
- **Commensal foraging** — feeding in association with another species, such as ravens exploiting carcasses opened by wolves.
- **Monogamy** — a mating system based on a lasting bond between one male and one female.
