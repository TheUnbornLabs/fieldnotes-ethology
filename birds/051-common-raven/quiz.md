# Quiz — The Common Raven (*Corvus corax*)

1. What is the scientific name of the Common Raven?
   A. *Corvus corax*
   B. *Corvus corone*
   C. *Columba livia*
   D. *Pica pica*

2. Which feature best distinguishes a raven from a crow in flight?
   A. A forked tail
   B. A rounded tail
   C. A wedge-shaped tail
   D. A very short tail

3. What is "re-caching" in ravens?
   A. Moving hidden food to a new spot, often after being watched
   B. Eating stored food immediately
   C. Sharing food with nestlings
   D. Calling other ravens to a carcass

4. The high cognitive capacity of corvids is linked to which neural feature?
   A. An unusually large body-to-brain ratio only
   B. A specialised smell centre
   C. Echolocation circuitry
   D. Very high neuron density in the forebrain (pallium)

5. In Heinrich's classic experiment, ravens solved which problem?
   A. Navigating home over long distances
   B. Pulling up meat on a string in stages
   C. Opening milk bottles
   D. Counting to ten

6. Kabadayi and Osvath's study is best known for suggesting ravens can:
   A. Migrate thousands of kilometres
   B. Understand human grammar
   C. Plan for the future by selecting a tool or token for a later reward
   D. Digest bone

7. Adult raven pairs are best described as:
   A. Territorial and long-bonded
   B. Colonial nesters
   C. Solitary and non-breeding
   D. Migratory flock members

8. A raven imitating human words demonstrates:
   A. Comprehension of language
   B. Instinctive alarm calling
   C. Echolocation
   D. Vocal mimicry without understanding meaning

9. Ravens often feed at carcasses opened by wolves. This is an example of:
   A. Parasitism
   B. Commensal foraging
   C. Predation
   D. Migration

10. On the IUCN Red List, the Common Raven is classified as:
    A. Endangered
    B. Vulnerable
    C. Least Concern
    D. Extinct in the Wild

## Answer Key
1. A
2. C
3. A
4. D
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. D
9. B
10. C
