# Book 093 — The Domestic Canary: Quiz

1. The wild ancestor of the domestic canary is native to:
   A. The Canary Islands and other Atlantic islands (Macaronesia)
   B. Mainland Australia
   C. North America
   D. Antarctica

2. Research on canary song provided influential evidence for:
   A. Echolocation
   B. Adult neurogenesis and neuroplasticity in the songbird brain
   C. Magnetic navigation
   D. Tool use

3. Compared with the zebra finch, the canary is notable for:
   A. Not singing at all
   B. Song that crystallises once and never changes
   C. Adult song plasticity — song that can change across seasons and years
   D. Female-only song

4. The bright-yellow colour of pet canaries is:
   A. The natural colour of wild canaries
   B. Caused by their diet alone in the wild
   C. Present only in females
   D. A product of selective breeding under domestication

5. The complex, sustained canary song is produced mainly by:
   A. Females
   B. Both sexes equally
   C. Males
   D. Only juveniles

6. The wild canary belongs to which family?
   A. Estrildidae
   B. Passeridae
   C. Fringillidae
   D. Corvidae

7. The "canary in the coal mine" arose because canaries:
   A. Were trained to detect gas
   B. Are especially brave
   C. Can smell carbon monoxide
   D. Succumb to toxic gases sooner than humans due to small size and high metabolism

8. The canary's song-control brain regions were found to:
   A. Stay exactly the same size all year
   B. Change in size across the seasons
   C. Disappear in adulthood
   D. Exist only in females

9. Song breeds of domestic canary demonstrate:
   A. That song cannot be inherited or shaped
   B. Artificial selection acting on a behavior
   C. That all canaries sing identically
   D. Natural selection in the wild

10. The canary's diet is mainly:
    A. Fish
    B. Nectar only
    C. Large vertebrate prey
    D. Seeds, supplemented by greens and some animal matter

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. C
4. D
5. C
6. C
7. D
8. B
9. B
10. D
