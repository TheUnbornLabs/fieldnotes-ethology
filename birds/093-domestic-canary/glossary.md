# Book 093 — The Domestic Canary: Glossary

**Fringillidae** — the family of true finches, to which the wild canary (*Serinus canaria*) belongs.

**Domesticated form** — a population shaped by generations of selective breeding under human control, as distinct from its wild ancestor.

**Artificial selection** — selective breeding by humans for desired traits, here including plumage colour, body type, and song.

**Song-control nuclei** — the network of brain regions in songbirds involved in learning and producing song; shown in canaries to change seasonally.

**Adult neurogenesis** — the generation of new neurons in the adult brain, evidenced in the songbird brain through canary research.

**Neuroplasticity** — the capacity of the brain to change its structure and function, including across seasons in adult songbirds.

**Song plasticity** — the ability to modify song after initial learning; pronounced in the canary and linked to seasonal brain change.

**Seasonal breeder** — a species whose reproduction is timed to particular times of year and conditions.

**Granivore** — an animal that feeds mainly on seeds.

**Altricial** — hatching helpless, naked, and blind, requiring extended parental care.

**Socially monogamous** — forming a pair bond for breeding.

**Song breed** — a variety of domestic canary selectively bred for a particular vocal style.

**Macaronesia** — the Atlantic island region (including the Canary Islands, Azores, and Madeira) that is the wild canary's native range.

**Territoriality** — defence of an area, shown by breeding male canaries.

**Crystallisation** — the stabilisation of song into a fixed form; contrasted with the canary's ongoing plasticity.
