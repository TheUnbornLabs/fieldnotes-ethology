# Quiz — The Village Weaver (*Ploceus cucullatus*)

1. In the village weaver, the woven nest is built by:
   A. The male
   B. The female alone
   C. Both parents equally
   D. Unrelated helpers

2. The female village weaver's key role in mate choice is to:
   A. Build the nest first
   B. Inspect the completed nest and accept or reject it
   C. Sing a duet with the male
   D. Fight rival females

3. Weaver nest-building is best described as:
   A. A rigid, flawless instinct identical in every bird
   B. Purely learned by copying others
   C. Having a strong innate basis but improving with experience
   D. Random and without structure

4. The village weaver's mating system is:
   A. Polygyny
   B. Lifelong monogamy
   C. Cooperative breeding with helpers
   D. Solitary and territorial

5. The enclosed, hanging form of the weaver nest primarily serves to:
   A. Attract insects as food
   B. Store seeds for winter
   C. Cool the eggs
   D. Protect eggs and young from predators

6. The village weaver is regarded in parts of its range as:
   A. A pollinator
   B. An agricultural pest of cereal crops
   C. A predator of livestock
   D. A rare, threatened bird

7. Some weavers defend against brood parasites through:
   A. Building underground
   B. Migrating away
   C. Recognising and rejecting foreign eggs
   D. Abandoning the colony each year

8. Village weavers breed in:
   A. Colonies of many nests close together
   B. Isolated single nests far apart
   C. Burrows in the ground
   D. Tree cavities they excavate

9. A male whose nests are repeatedly rejected by females may:
   A. Leave the colony forever
   B. Stop building for the season
   C. Take over the female's role
   D. Tear down old nests and rebuild fresh ones

10. The IUCN Red List status of the village weaver is:
   A. Endangered
   B. Vulnerable
   C. Near Threatened
   D. Least Concern

## Answer Key

1. A
2. B
3. C
4. A
5. D
6. B
7. C
8. A
9. D
10. D
