# Glossary — The Village Weaver (*Ploceus cucullatus*)

**Weaver** — a bird of the family Ploceidae, noted for constructing woven nests; the village weaver is a typical example.

**Woven nest** — an enclosed nest built by threading, looping, and knotting strips of vegetation; the male village weaver's signature construction.

**Pendent nest** — a nest that hangs from a branch, often with a downward-facing entrance, providing protection from predators.

**Colonial breeding** — breeding in aggregations of many nests placed close together, as village weavers do.

**Polygyny** — a mating system in which a male may mate with several females; the village weaver's mating system.

**Female choice** — the selection of a mate (and, in the weaver, a nest) by the female, driving male building and display.

**Nest inspection** — the female's examination of a completed nest before accepting or rejecting it and the male.

**Altricial** — describing chicks that hatch helpless and dependent, as weaver nestlings do.

**Innate behaviour** — behaviour with a strong genetic and developmental basis; in weavers, the drive and general form of nest-building.

**Egg recognition** — the ability to identify and sometimes reject foreign eggs, shown by some weavers and relevant to brood parasitism.

**Brood parasite** — an animal that lays its eggs in another's nest for that host to raise; weaver egg recognition is a defence against this.

**Mobbing** — the collective harassment of a predator by colony members.

**Generalist** — a species with a broad diet and habitat tolerance; the village weaver is a dietary and habitat generalist.

**Agricultural pest** — a species that damages crops; village weavers can damage cereal crops when feeding in flocks.

**Passerine** — a member of the order Passeriformes, the perching birds; the weaver is a passerine.
