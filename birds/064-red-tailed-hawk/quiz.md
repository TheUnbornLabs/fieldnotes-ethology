# Short Quiz — The Red-Tailed Hawk

1. To which group of raptors does the red-tailed hawk belong?
   A. Buteos (broad-winged soaring hawks)
   B. Falcons
   C. Owls
   D. Accipiters (bird-hawks)

2. Which hunting method involves holding position in the air by facing into a steady wind?
   A. Stooping
   B. Perch-and-pounce
   C. Kiting (hovering)
   D. Scavenging

3. The dramatic "eagle scream" in films is usually the call of which bird?
   A. The bald eagle
   B. The golden eagle
   C. The peregrine falcon
   D. The red-tailed hawk

4. What is the core of the red-tailed hawk's diet?
   A. Fish
   B. Small mammals such as voles and ground squirrels
   C. Insects
   D. Carrion only

5. When does the brick-red tail typically appear?
   A. At hatching
   B. At fledging, about six weeks old
   C. Only in adults, around the second year
   D. Never; it is a myth

6. What is the red-tailed hawk's mating system?
   A. Polygynous harems
   B. Socially monogamous, often long-term pairs
   C. Promiscuous, no pair bond
   D. Cooperative breeding in groups

7. The aerial courtship of paired red-tails is called the:
   A. Lek
   B. Roost display
   C. Stoop
   D. Sky-dance

8. Which best describes the red-tailed hawk compared with the peregrine falcon?
   A. A high-speed aerial specialist
   B. A broad-winged soaring generalist
   C. A nocturnal hunter
   D. A cooperative pack hunter

9. Why is the red-tailed hawk commonly used in falconry?
   A. It is hardy, trainable, and common — the classic beginner's bird
   B. It is endangered and needs captive breeding
   C. It cannot survive in the wild
   D. It is the fastest raptor

10. "Pale Male" was famous as:
    A. A dark colour morph of the species
    B. A captive falconry bird
    C. A red-tailed hawk that nested on a Manhattan apartment building
    D. A subspecies from Jamaica

## Answer Key

1. A
2. C
3. D
4. B
5. C
6. B
7. D
8. B
9. A
10. C
