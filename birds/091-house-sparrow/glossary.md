# Book 091 — The House Sparrow: Glossary

**Commensal** — an organism that lives closely associated with another species (here, humans) and benefits from that association without necessarily harming or helping it.

**Passeridae** — the family of Old World sparrows, to which the house sparrow belongs; distinct from the New World sparrows (Passerellidae).

**Granivore** — an animal that feeds mainly on seeds and grain.

**Altricial** — describing young that hatch helpless, naked or nearly so, and blind, requiring extended parental care.

**Badge of status** — a plumage or morphological signal, such as the male house sparrow's black bib, hypothesised to advertise dominance or fighting ability.

**Dominance hierarchy** — a ranking among individuals that influences access to resources such as food or nest sites.

**Socially monogamous** — forming a pair bond for breeding, which may not correspond to exclusive genetic parentage because of extra-pair matings.

**Extra-pair mating** — mating outside the social pair bond, resulting in offspring not sired by the social mate.

**Cavity nester** — a bird that nests in holes or enclosed spaces, such as crevices in buildings or nest boxes.

**Dust-bathing** — working dry soil or dust through the plumage, thought to help maintain feather condition and control parasites.

**Communal roost** — a site where many individuals gather to sleep, providing warmth and possibly information exchange and safety.

**Local enhancement** — being drawn to a location or food source because other individuals are already there.

**Diurnal** — active during daylight hours.

**Fledgling** — a young bird that has left the nest but may still depend on parents for food.

**Sedentary** — remaining year-round in one area rather than migrating.
