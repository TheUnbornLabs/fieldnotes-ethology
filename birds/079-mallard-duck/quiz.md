# Quiz — Book 079: The Mallard

1. The Mallard is best described as which kind of duck?
   A. A diving duck
   B. A dabbling duck
   C. A fish-eating sea duck
   D. A filter-feeding specialist like a flamingo

2. The striking difference in plumage between drake and female Mallards is explained mainly by:
   A. Diet
   B. Age
   C. Sexual selection
   D. Water chemistry

3. The loud, familiar "quack" is given mainly by:
   A. The drake
   B. The female
   C. Ducklings
   D. Both sexes equally

4. Coercive and extra-pair mating in Mallards are best understood as:
   A. Evidence that the birds are cruel
   B. Evolutionary phenomena driven by sexual selection and sexual conflict
   C. Behaviors seen only in captivity
   D. A recent result of urban living

5. Mallard pair bonds are:
   A. Lifelong and exclusive
   B. Seasonal and non-exclusive
   C. Absent entirely
   D. Maintained only by the drake

6. Mallard ducklings are:
   A. Naked, blind, and fed in the nest
   B. Precocial, feeding themselves within a day of hatching
   C. Unable to swim for weeks
   D. Raised mainly by the drake

7. "Eclipse plumage" refers to:
   A. The female's nesting camouflage
   B. The drab plumage drakes adopt during the flightless summer moult
   C. Duckling down
   D. Bright breeding colours

8. The unusual co-evolution of male and female reproductive anatomy in ducks is a classic example of:
   A. Kin selection
   B. Imprinting
   C. Sexual conflict
   D. Convergent evolution

9. A major conservation concern created by the Mallard's success is:
   A. Overhunting of its prey
   B. Hybridisation with rarer related duck species
   C. Destruction of forests
   D. Spread of one-legged standing

10. Parental care of Mallard young is provided:
   A. By both parents equally
   B. Mainly by the female, who leads and guards but does not feed them
   C. By the drake alone
   D. By a communal crèche of adults

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. B
4. B
5. B
6. B
7. B
8. C
9. B
10. B
