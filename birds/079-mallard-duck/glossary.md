# Glossary — Book 079: The Mallard

**Dabbling duck** — a duck that feeds at or near the surface and by up-ending, rather than by diving; the Mallard is the archetype.

**Up-ending** — feeding by tipping forward so the body is vertical and the head reaches food underwater.

**Lamellae** — comb-like plates along the bill edges used to filter food from water and mud.

**Speculum** — the coloured, often iridescent wing patch (blue-purple in the Mallard) bordered with white.

**Sexual dimorphism** — consistent differences in appearance between the sexes, marked in the Mallard's breeding plumage.

**Sexual selection** — selection arising from competition for mates and mate choice, shaping traits such as the drake's plumage.

**Sexual conflict** — the divergence of the reproductive interests of males and females, driving counter-adaptations.

**Extra-pair copulation** — mating outside the pair bond.

**Coercive (forced) copulation** — mating imposed on a female against resistance; documented in Mallards and part of sexual conflict.

**Eclipse plumage** — the drab plumage adopted by drakes during the late-summer flightless moult, resembling the female.

**Precocial** — describing young that hatch developed and able to move and feed soon after hatching.

**Imprinting** — rapid learning during a sensitive period by which ducklings attach to their mother.

**Inciting** — a female display encouraging a preferred drake and directing aggression at others.

**Distraction display** — feigned-injury or other behavior by which a female lures predators from her brood.

**Hybridisation** — interbreeding between species, which in Mallards can threaten rarer related ducks.
