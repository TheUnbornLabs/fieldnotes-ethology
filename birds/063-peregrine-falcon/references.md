## Well-established sources (safe to name)

- **Derek Ratcliffe**, *The Peregrine Falcon* — the classic scientific monograph on peregrine biology, population history, and the DDT-era decline. Safe to name by author and title; verify the specific edition and any detailed figures before final publication.
- The peregrine recovery work associated with **Tom Cade** and **The Peregrine Fund** — the captive-breeding and "hacking" (release) programmes that rebuilt North American populations after the DDT crash. Name generally; verify specific publications and dates before final publication.
- **IUCN Red List** — species assessment for *Falco peregrinus* (global status: Least Concern). Consult the current Red List entry for range, population trend, and threats.
- **Cornell Lab of Ornithology** (All About Birds / Birds of the World) — species account and behavioural information for the peregrine falcon.
- **Animal Diversity Web** (University of Michigan Museum of Zoology) — species profile for *Falco peregrinus*.
- Standard animal-behavior and ornithology textbooks (e.g. Alcock, *Animal Behavior*) for general concepts such as sensory ecology, predatory behaviour, and reproductive strategy.

## Verified (checked 2026-07-02)

Fact-check pass against live web sources:

- **Scientific name** *Falco peregrinus*; Falconiformes / Falconidae — confirmed. Molecular placement of falcons nearer parrots/passerines than to Accipitridae is well established.
- **IUCN Red List status: Least Concern** — confirmed (IUCN Red List / BirdLife International 2021 assessment).
- **Stoop speed** — confirmed. Typical hunting-dive terminal velocity is commonly cited at ~320 km/h (~200 mph); the fastest documented dive is 389.46 km/h (242 mph), recorded in 1999 for the falcon "Frightful" (Ken Franklin) using a chip data-logger after a high-altitude release (Guinness World Records). Some ornithologists note this record was under non-natural, high-altitude conditions. The book's "exceed 300 km/h... roughly 390 km/h (about 240 mph)" is accurate and appropriately hedged.
- Fovea/UV-vision, nostril-baffle aerodynamics, tomial-tooth mechanics, and DDT/hacking items remain flagged (not individually source-verified in this pass).
