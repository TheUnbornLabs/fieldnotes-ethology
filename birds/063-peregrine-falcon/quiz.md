# Short Quiz — The Peregrine Falcon (*Falco peregrinus*)

1. The peregrine falcon holds the record as the fastest animal on Earth because of its:
   A. Level cruising flight
   B. High-speed hunting dive, or stoop
   C. Running speed
   D. Migration distance

2. During a stoop, the peregrine usually kills or stuns its prey by:
   A. Biting it with the beak in mid-air
   B. Grabbing and squeezing it with its talons like an eagle
   C. Striking it with a clenched foot at high speed
   D. Herding it into the ground

3. The notched structure on the peregrine's upper beak used to sever prey's neck is the:
   A. Tomial tooth
   B. Nostril baffle
   C. Nictitating membrane
   D. Keel

4. Falcons (family Falconidae) are most closely related to:
   A. Hawks and eagles
   B. Owls
   C. Vultures
   D. Parrots and songbirds

5. The small bony projections inside the peregrine's nostrils are widely thought to:
   A. Sharpen its sense of smell
   B. Help manage airflow into the nostrils at high speed
   C. Filter out dust only
   D. Amplify sound

6. The translucent third eyelid that protects and cleans the peregrine's eye during the stoop is the:
   A. Fovea
   B. Tomial tooth
   C. Nictitating membrane
   D. Cere

7. The peregrine's population collapse in the mid-20th century was caused mainly by:
   A. The pesticide DDT, which thinned eggshells
   B. Hunting for falconry
   C. Loss of cliff habitat
   D. Competition with eagles

8. "Hacking," in peregrine conservation, refers to:
   A. Trimming the birds' feathers
   B. Illegally taking eggs
   C. Tagging birds with transmitters
   D. Gradually releasing captive-reared young from an artificial ledge until they can hunt

9. A peregrine's nest is best described as:
   A. A large stick platform
   B. A simple scrape on a ledge, cliff, or building
   C. A tree cavity lined with fur
   D. A floating raft of vegetation

10. A nestling peregrine still in the nest is called an:
    A. Eyas
    B. Eyrie
    C. Cere
    D. Tercel

## Answer Key

1. B
2. C
3. A
4. D
5. B
6. C
7. A
8. D
9. B
10. A
