# Batch 03 — Books 011–015

**Theme:** Social carnivores and the wolf's relatives. Two cooperative pack societies often misjudged (hyena, wild dog), a solitary omnivore (brown bear), the ultimate generalist (red fox), and the domesticated end of the canid story (dog) — a coherent set on carnivore sociality and human coexistence.

**Status:** All five drafted; 20 sections and 5 files each verified. Pending citation verification.

## References needing verification

Priority flagged items: hyena rank-inheritance mechanism and primate-comparison cooperation experiments; wild-dog "sneeze-to-hunt" quorum study (Walker et al.) and hunting-success percentages; brown-bear delayed-implantation/fat-reserve link and salmon nutrient-transport studies; red-fox magnetic-alignment pounce (Červený et al., debated) and Belyaev/Trut farm-fox papers; dog oxytocin gaze-bonding (Nagasawa et al.) and domestication-timing genetics. Safe anchors: Kruuk, Holekamp, Creel & Creel, Herrero, Macdonald, Miklósi, Coppinger, AVSAB, IUCN Red List.

## Weak sections to revisit

Cognition and quantitative life-history figures (body mass, gestation, litter size, populations) kept approximate across all five. Notably hedged: hyena/primate cognition comparison, wild-dog vs lion success-rate figures, brown-bear "displaces black bear" claim, red-fox mating-system variability, dog domestication timing (deliberately open).

## Notable myth corrections this batch

Hyenas as mere scavengers / hermaphrodites; wild dogs as feral or cruel; bears as deep hibernators and standing-means-attack; foxes as dangerous vermin or cats; and the persistent dog "alpha/dominance" training myth (tied back to the corrected captive-wolf model in Book 001).
