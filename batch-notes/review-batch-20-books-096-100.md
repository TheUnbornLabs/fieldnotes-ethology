## 2. Major Corrections Made (authored from stub)

All content is newly authored; there was no prior prose to correct. "Major" items below are the principal scientific-accuracy decisions made during authoring, plus one production fix.

- All five books — Each book.md was written from an empty stub to a complete 20-section chapter (2,500–5,000 words; final range 4,319–4,570 words), plus a companion summary (300–500 words), a section-17-matched glossary (14 terms each), a 10-item quiz with answer key identical to book Section 19, and a references.md following the citation policy.
- 096 Common Swift — Framed continuous months-long flight, on-the-wing sleep, and nestling torpor as well-supported-but-cautiously-stated findings (tracking studies) rather than folklore; explicitly corrected the common "swift = swallow" confusion (convergent evolution; swifts are Apodiformes, allied to hummingbirds).
- 099 Sacred Ibis — Used the correct binomial *Threskiornis aethiopicus*; placed the species in Threskiornithidae and noted the modern Pelecaniformes placement (with the older Ciconiiformes scheme flagged); kept ancient-Egyptian religious/mummification content clearly separated from biology; addressed invasive European populations and their control.
- 100 Griffon Vulture — Stated clearly that Old World *Gyps* vultures find carrion by SIGHT and social information (watching other vultures), explicitly contrasted with the smell-guided New World turkey vulture; treated the diclofenac-driven Asian *Gyps* collapse as a documented warning while keeping *Gyps fulvus* global status as Least Concern.
- Production fix (all five) — The Edit/Write path truncated large files on this mount (book.md persisted only ~1.5 KB). All book.md files were re-authored via a reliable write path and verified on disk (word counts, 6 myth fences, 20 sections, matching answer keys). Corrected one binomial typo in 099 (*Platalea leucorolia* → *Platalea leucorodia*).

## 3. Minor Corrections Made

- Anti-anthropomorphism sweep — Removed/avoided "instinct-only" framing for all five species; softened interpretive claims (e.g. crane crown signalling, stork pair "reunion" via nest-site fidelity rather than romance, swift sleep mechanism) with explicit caution.
