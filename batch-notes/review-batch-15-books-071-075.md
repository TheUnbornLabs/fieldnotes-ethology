## 2. Major Corrections Made

- 075 Great Cormorant — Factual error: Section 5 claimed cormorants navigate underwater using a "lateral line system." Birds do not possess a lateral line (a fish/amphibian sense). Rewritten to state prey detection is primarily visual, with an explicit note.
- 075 Great Cormorant — Feeding mechanics: corrected "propelling with feet and wings" to foot-propelled diving (great cormorants propel with the feet, not the wings).
- 075 Great Cormorant — Refined the "lack waterproofing oil" framing to the accurate partly-wettable-plumage / reduced-buoyancy explanation, and added the classic spread-wing (wing-drying) behavior to Sections 1, 3, and Myth 1.
- 073 Herring Gull — Added the missing classic ethology content: Tinbergen's red-spot bill-pecking releaser / sign-stimulus experiments (Section 10), carefully attributed to Tinbergen (and Tinbergen & Perdeck 1950), with the Hailman qualification that learning refines the response. This is the single most important herring-gull ethology finding and was entirely absent.
- 072 Wandering Albatross — Foraging range corrected from an understated "500+ km on single trips" to "thousands of kilometers" (consistent with satellite-tracking data).
- All five books — Quiz answer keys were clustered/near-uniform (e.g., 071 had 8 of 10 = "b"; 075 had 7 of 10 = "b"). Keys redistributed by reordering options (content preserved), and book.md Section 19 and quiz.md kept identical. Verified consistent and well-distributed for all five.

## 3. Minor Corrections Made

- 071 Emperor Penguin — Fixed grammar ("an 4-month" → "a fast of roughly four months"); clarified that the ~4-month fast spans courtship/travel/incubation (reconciling with the 65–75-day incubation figure); corrected breeding habitat from "Antarctica and nearby sub-Antarctic islands" to Antarctic fast ice (emperors do not breed on sub-Antarctic islands); softened dive figures and navigation ("magnetic cues") claims with verification flags; adjusted body-mass phrasing.
- 072 Wandering Albatross — Softened wingspan to typical ~3.0–3.1 m with record ~3.5 m (was a flat "3.5 m"); flagged record figures; adjusted weight range; clarified surface-seizing feeding vs deep diving; longevity phrased cautiously.
- 073 Herring Gull — Softened "recognize individual humans" to sensitivity to human cues (gaze direction); removed anthropomorphic "teach offspring" (Section 11 and Study Q4); corrected lifespan (typical ~10–15 yr, max ~30, was "20–30"); nuanced conservation status (global LC but regional declines); added glossary terms (fixed action pattern, releaser, kleptoparasitism, social learning) in book Section 17.
- 074 Brown Pelican — Nuanced status ("recovered from Near Extinction" → regional collapse / US Endangered listing then recovery); clarified US range and DDT-ban context (1972 US agricultural ban retained, correct); lifespan phrased as ~15–30 yr.
- 075 Great Cormorant — Updated book Section 17 glossary to remove the buoyancy/waterproofing confusion and add spread-wing posture, wettable plumage, foot-propelled diving; flagged the "over 1,300 years" fishing-tradition figure as approximate.
