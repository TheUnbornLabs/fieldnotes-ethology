## 2. Major Corrections Made (and stubs authored)
- **076 Flamingo (major rewrite):** The existing book.md was accurate in outline but far below benchmark — telegraphic, filler-thin, ~1,000 words of fragments, and it contained a real error: it called chicks flatly "altricial." Corrected to the accurate nuance (flamingo chicks hatch downy with open eyes, mobile within days — closer to precocial, yet dependent for food). Expanded every section to benchmark depth (~2,600 words), added cautious framing on one-legged standing (debated, not single-cause), added the parent–chick vocal-recognition correction, added crop-milk detail, expanded myths to 6 in individual code fences, and rewrote the quiz to 10 MCQ × 4 options (original had inconsistent 4-option lettering and clustered answers). Companion files were empty placeholders ("[Companion file content]") — all four authored fresh from the book.
- **077, 078, 079, 080:** All four book.md files were empty template stubs ("[Thematic Focus]", "[description]"). Authored in full (4,200–4,400 words each) plus all companion files.
- **079 Mallard:** Handled promiscuity, extra-pair and forced copulation, and the genital co-evolution arms race factually as sexual selection / sexual conflict, with an explicit anti-moralising myth entry — per the species accuracy note.
- **078 Greylag:** Attributed imprinting carefully — credited Spalding/Heinroth as predecessors and Lorenz for systematic study and popularisation, and corrected the "instant bond to first thing seen" caricature.
- **077 Mute Swan:** Applied the required nuance to "mate for life" (durable but not unbreakable; divorce, re-pairing) and to the "break-your-arm" myth (a wing blow can hurt; the broken-bone claim is exaggeration/folklore).
