## 2. Major Corrections Made

- **066 — Peahen mate-choice presented as settled (scientific-honesty failure).** The book asserted that "females prefer males with extravagant displays" and that the train's function is established. Per the ongoing scientific debate, I rewrote the Opening Summary, Section 3 (Evolutionary Background), Myth 1, the summary, and references to present peahen use of train ornaments as **debated, not settled**: early Petrie et al. (1990s) results vs. the Takahashi et al. (2008) Japanese study finding no train-based preference, plus Dakin & Montgomerie (2011). Added Fisherian runaway alongside the handicap framework. Inserted "Needs verification before publication" markers on the debate-specific citations.
- **067 — False "tool use" claim (accuracy).** The Opening Summary stated chickens "use tools," contradicting Section 11 ("no evidence of tool use in wild-type chickens") and the biological record. Rewrote the opening to remove the tool-use claim and instead cite individual recognition, observational learning, referential alarm calls, and the pecking order.
- **067 — Missing pecking-order attribution (required).** Added Thorleif Schjelderup-Ebbe (1920s) as the originator of the pecking-order concept in Section 7, the Opening Summary, the summary, the glossary, and references — the founding study of vertebrate dominance hierarchies.
- **067 — Referential alarm calls strengthened (required, "dumb bird" myth).** Expanded Section 8 to explain functionally referential aerial-vs-ground alarm calls and explicitly framed this as a rebuttal to the "dumb bird" stereotype; added glossary term and a cautious note that "theory of mind" claims remain debated.
- **068 — Taxonomic/size error.** "The second-largest bird species (after the ostrich) native to North America" was corrected — the ostrich is not North American. Now: among the largest North American birds and by far the heaviest North American galliform, with a note on Phasianidae/Meleagridinae placement.
- **069 & 070 — Outdated formal taxon "Ratitae."** Replaced with correct framing: ratites within Palaeognathae; ostrich in order Struthioniformes (069), emu in order Casuariiformes (070). Added note that the Somali Ostrich is often treated as a separate species (069).
- **070 — Irrelevant references removed.** references.md and book.md Section 20 carried citations on African buffalo (Jarman & Sinclair) and sperm competition / female reproductive tract (Dickemann), plus an ostrich-nutrition paper (Minnaar & Meissner) — none relevant to emu behavior. Removed and replaced with emu/ratite-appropriate sources (Frith 1962; Davies 1978, 2002; Serventy & Whittell 1976).
- **All five — Quiz answer keys clustered (required fix).** Every book had answer keys concentrated on b/c (e.g., 067 had seven "b" answers; 070 had seven "b"). Redistributed all keys by reordering options (content preserved) to roughly even a/b/c/d spreads, and kept book.md Section 19 and quiz.md identical. New keys:
  - 066: a,b,c,d,a,d,c,b,d,a
  - 067: a,b,c,d,a,b,c,d,b,d
  - 068: b,b,c,d,c,d,d,a,a,c
  - 069: b,c,d,d,c,b,d,b,a,a
  - 070: b,d,d,b,c,d,b,a,c,a

## 7. Final Batch Verdict

A high-quality bird batch that needed one substantive scientific-honesty fix (066 peahen debate) and one factual-error fix (067 tool-use), plus consistent taxonomy cleanup (069/070 "Ratitae"), a North-America size error (068), and reference hygiene (removing off-topic citations, especially in 070). Anthropomorphism is well controlled, no book calls birds "instinct-only," and the required myth debunks (ostrich head-in-sand, chicken "dumb bird") are handled correctly. All five are publication-ready drafts pending light citation verification; none requires structural rework.
